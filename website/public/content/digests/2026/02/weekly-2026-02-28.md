## TL;DR

Authoritarian regimes deployed unprecedented digital repression techniques this week, with Iran implementing 'digital apartheid' internet systems during deadly protests while governments globally escalated surveillance and censorship tactics. The Trump administration's contradictory approach—launching 'anti-censorship' portals while cutting freedom tech funding—highlights the selective nature of digital rights advocacy.

## Top Signals

### Iran's Two-Tiered Internet Control

- [Iran Deploys Two-Tiered 'Digital Apartheid' Internet During 2026 Protests](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/iran-deploys-two-tiered-digital-apartheid-internet-during-20) — Class-based system with privileged 'white SIM cards' for loyalists while disconnecting ordinary citizens
- [Iran's DPI-Powered Internet Whitelisting System Built with Russian Tech Support](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/iran-s-dpi-powered-internet-whitelisting-system-built-with-r) — Infrastructure-level whitelisting using Deep Packet Inspection with Russian Protei systems
- [Iran's Total Internet Shutdown Enables Mass State Violence During 2026 Uprising](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/iran-s-total-internet-shutdown-enables-mass-state-violence-d) — 15-day blackout enabled 30,000+ deaths while regime allies shaped international narratives

Iran's 2026 internet shutdown represents a new paradigm in digital authoritarianism: stratified access that preserves regime communications while completely isolating opposition voices. The technical sophistication—combining Russian DPI technology with administrative privilege systems—suggests this model will spread to other authoritarian contexts.

**Proposed Solution**: Anonymous communication platform using mix networks and zero-knowledge proofs that makes it cryptographically impossible to link users to their activities, even under legal compulsion.

**Impact Score**: 85/100 | **Feasibility Score**: 70/100 ([GitHub Issue](https://github.com/FGUTech/libertas/issues/8))

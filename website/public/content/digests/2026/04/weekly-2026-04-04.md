## TL;DR

2025 shattered records with 313 internet shutdowns across 52 countries—zero days without blackouts. Governments evolved from crude blocking to sophisticated platform control, while new surveillance tools target VPN users and AI safety guardrails. Two project ideas emerged for identity compartmentalization and surveillance accountability.

## Top Signals

### Record-Breaking Year of Digital Darkness

- [Global Internet Shutdowns Hit Record High: 313 Blackouts Across 52 Countries in 2025](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/global-internet-shutdowns-hit-record-high-313-blackouts-acro) — Myanmar led with 95 shutdowns; authorities increasingly target satellite internet systems
- [MENA Region Hit by 52 Internet Shutdowns Across 15 Countries](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/mena-region-hit-by-52-internet-shutdowns-across-15-countries) — Regional breakdown shows systematic targeting of satellite alternatives
- [Access Now: 2025 Saw Record Internet Shutdowns, 30 in 15 African Countries](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/access-now-2025-saw-record-internet-shutdowns-30-in-15-afric) — Protests and elections remain primary triggers

2025 marked a watershed moment in digital authoritarianism: 313 documented shutdowns with literally zero days free of internet blackouts globally. The systematic targeting of satellite internet represents a concerning evolution—authorities are learning to close traditional circumvention gaps.
### From Crude Blocks to Platform Control

- [EFF Analysis: How Governments Evolved From Crude Blocking to Sophisticated Censorship Systems](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/eff-analysis-how-governments-evolved-from-crude-blocking-to-) — Egypt's transformation from Arab Spring catalyst to digital dystopia as case study
- [UAE Criminalizes Citizen Documentation of Drone Strikes, Arrests 135+ People](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/uae-criminalizes-citizen-documentation-of-drone-strikes-arre) — 2+ years imprisonment and $55K fines for contradicting official narratives
- [Ugandan Journalist Disappeared After Discussing Struck-Down Surveillance Laws](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/ugandan-journalist-disappeared-after-discussing-struck-down-) — Plainclothes security disappeared journalist for discussing Constitutional Court ruling

The EFF's analysis reveals a critical shift: governments moved beyond simple website blocking to sophisticated platform control systems that make traditional circumvention tools less effective. This represents a fundamental challenge to freedom tech—we're not just fighting censorship, but censorship infrastructure.
### Weaponizing Procurement and Technology

- [US Govt Rewrites Procurement Rules to Force AI Companies to Disable Safety Guardrails](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/us-govt-rewrites-procurement-rules-to-force-ai-companies-to-) — GSA rules require contractors to license systems for 'all lawful purposes'
- [FAA Issues Nationwide 21-Month Drone Ban Near ICE Vehicles](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/faa-issues-nationwide-21-month-drone-ban-near-ice-vehicles-e) — Half-mile restriction effectively criminalizes accountability journalism
- [US iPhone Hacking Toolkit 'Coruna' Leaked to Russia](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/us-iphone-hacking-toolkit-coruna-leaked-to-russia-now-used-b) — 23 iOS vulnerability toolkit leaked by L3Harris employee, now used by adversaries
- [Citizen Lab Warns of Digital Transnational Repression 'Tsunami'](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/citizen-lab-warns-of-digital-transnational-repression-tsunam) — U.S. authoritarianism and AI expansion will intensify cross-border repression

The U.S. government is systematically weaponizing procurement rules and regulatory authority to undermine AI safety measures and accountability journalism. Meanwhile, sophisticated hacking tools are proliferating to adversaries, creating a dangerous feedback loop.
### VPN Users in Government Crosshairs

- [FISA 702 Renewal Could Target VPN Users as 'Foreign' for Warrantless Surveillance](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/fisa-702-renewal-could-target-vpn-users-as-foreign-for-warra) — VPN traffic may be classified as 'foreign' by default under Section 702
- [Proton Meet Launches with MLS Encryption to Counter AI Video Surveillance](https://libertas.fgu.tech/posts/proton-meet-launches-with-mls-encryption-to-counter-ai-video) — End-to-end encrypted video conferencing addresses AI-powered surveillance risks

The proposed FISA 702 renewal represents a direct assault on privacy tools: Americans using VPNs could be reclassified as 'foreign' targets, stripping away constitutional protections. This makes the deployment of robust alternatives like Proton's MLS-encrypted video conferencing increasingly critical.

## Project Ideas Generated This Week

This week's high-signal insights inspired 2 new project ideas:

### Travelers and activists face device searches that compromise identity across all apps, while OS-level identity verification creates persistent tracking. Current throwaway device advice is impractical for extended travel or residence.

**Proposed Solution**: Cryptographic identity compartmentalization system creating multiple unlinkable personas on single device. Each compartment has separate OS identity, encrypted storage, and network isolation. Border mode creates sterile compartment with tourist-appropriate data.

**Impact Score**: 85/100 | **Feasibility Score**: 65/100 ([GitHub Issue](https://github.com/FGUTech/libertas/issues/18))

**Affected Groups**: International activists, Journalists crossing borders, Dissidents traveling internationally, Privacy-conscious travelers

### Human rights defenders need better tools to document surveillance tech supply chains and digital repression incidents to support legal accountability efforts, but current documentation is ad-hoc and insufficient for litigation.

**Proposed Solution**: Cryptographically-verified incident reporting platform combining technical forensics with supply chain mapping. Uses anonymous reporting, blockchain timestamping for evidence integrity, and automated analysis to connect incidents across victims and technologies.

**Impact Score**: 75/100 | **Feasibility Score**: 80/100 ([GitHub Issue](https://github.com/FGUTech/libertas/issues/19))

**Affected Groups**: Human rights researchers, Digital rights organizations, Surveillance victims, Legal advocacy groups

# Ugandan Journalist Disappeared After Discussing Struck-Down Digital Censorship Laws

**TL;DR:** Arnold Anthony Mukose, journalist and program director at Alternative Digitalk, was forcibly seized by security operatives after broadcasting about a court ruling that nullified Uganda's Computer Misuse Act.

## Key Points

- Arnold Anthony Mukose forcibly taken by plainclothes security officers outside Metro FM on March 22, 2026
- Seizure occurred after live broadcast discussing Constitutional Court ruling that struck down Computer Misuse Act sections
- Joint Anti-Terrorism Task Force conducted house search, seizing electronic devices including laptop, modem, and smart watches
- Computer Misuse Act had been frequently used to target journalists and digital rights activists
- Police confirmed custody but timeline for court appearance unclear
- Pattern matches Uganda's documented history of enforced disappearances targeting media and opposition
- Alternative Digitalk previously raided in 2022, staff charged under now-annulled Computer Misuse Act provisions

## Deep Dive

## Context and Pattern

This incident represents a clear escalation in Uganda's crackdown on digital rights discourse. The timing is significant: Mukose was seized immediately after discussing a Constitutional Court victory that struck down key provisions of the Computer Misuse Act—laws that had been primary tools for prosecuting journalists and activists over digital content. The government's response suggests they view even discussion of legal victories against digital censorship as threatening.

## Operational Security Implications

The brazen daylight seizure outside a radio station sends a message that no venue is safe for digital rights advocacy. The systematic search and seizure of electronic devices follows established patterns for digital surveillance operations. The use of the Joint Anti-Terrorism Task Force—typically reserved for national security cases—indicates authorities are framing digital rights advocacy as a security threat rather than legitimate journalism.

## Broader Freedom Tech Impact

Uganda's response reveals the government's concern about losing legal tools for digital repression. When courts strike down censorship laws, authorities appear to be shifting to extrajudicial methods. This creates a chilling effect not just on journalism, but on any technical work or advocacy around circumvention tools, secure communications, or digital rights education.

## What This Means for the Ecosystem

The incident demonstrates that even victories in court can trigger violent backlash. Freedom tech builders and advocates in similar jurisdictions should expect escalated responses when legal frameworks for digital repression are challenged. The systematic device seizure also highlights the importance of secure operational practices for anyone involved in digital rights work in authoritarian contexts.

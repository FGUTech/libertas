# Azerbaijan Supreme Court Upholds 7-9 Year Sentences for Independent Journalists

**TL;DR:** Azerbaijan's Supreme Court rejected appeals for 7 journalists from Abzas Media and RFE/RL, upholding lengthy prison sentences for alleged illegal foreign funding in escalating press freedom crackdown.

## Key Points

- Azerbaijani Supreme Court upheld prison sentences of 7-9 years for journalists from Abzas Media and RFE/RL
- Six Abzas Media journalists and one RFE/RL correspondent convicted on charges of receiving illegal Western donor funding
- Abzas Media is Azerbaijan's most prominent anti-corruption investigative outlet
- Charges include currency smuggling, money laundering, and tax evasion related to alleged foreign funding
- Police reportedly found 40,000 euros during 2023 office raid, accusing US, French, and German embassies of illegal funding
- At least 24 journalists currently jailed in Azerbaijan, most detained since late 2023 on foreign funding charges
- European Court of Human Rights previously ruled that funding registration violations warrant fines, not criminal sanctions
- Independent experts report authorities refuse to register organizations seeking foreign grants, making legal funding impossible
- Journalists plan to file complaint with European Court of Human Rights

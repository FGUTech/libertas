# Israeli Pressure Forces Closure of Palestinian Children's Rights Group DCIP

**TL;DR:** Defense for Children International-Palestine (DCIP) shut down operations after Israeli military designated it a 'terrorist organization' using secret evidence, raided offices, and confiscated equipment.

## Key Points

- DCIP, a Palestinian children's rights group operating since 1991, ceased operations on April 7
- Israeli military designated DCIP and 5 other Palestinian rights groups as 'terrorist organizations' in 2021 using classified evidence
- European funders suspended support pending investigation, later concluded Israeli accusations were baseless
- Israeli forces raided DCIP offices in Ramallah, confiscated equipment and files, welded doors shut
- Group documented decades of Israeli military abuses: arbitrary arrests, torture, unfair trials, lethal force against children
- DCIP's work informed 2013 UNICEF report finding 'widespread, systematic' ill-treatment of Palestinian children in Israeli detention
- Closure eliminates key documentation source amid escalating rates of child displacement, detention, and killings in Gaza and West Bank
- Demonstrates how states use 'terrorism' designations and secret evidence to silence civil society documentation

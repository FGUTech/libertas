# Proton Warns Age Verification Laws Create New Privacy Risks for Children

**TL;DR:** Proton argues that government age verification laws are creating dangerous privacy vulnerabilities, citing Discord's recent breach of 70K government IDs collected under UK regulations.

## Key Points

- Age verification laws requiring government IDs, biometrics, and selfies create new privacy risks for children
- Discord breach in September exposed 70,000+ government ID images collected under UK age verification law
- Most companies outsource verification to third-party vendors, creating centralized data targets
- UK's Office of Communications reports inconsistent record-keeping compliance among platforms
- Proton advocates for decentralized verification solutions that preserve anonymity
- Privacy-focused company warns these laws trade one risk (harmful content) for another (data breaches)
- Demonstrates how well-intentioned child safety laws can create surveillance infrastructure

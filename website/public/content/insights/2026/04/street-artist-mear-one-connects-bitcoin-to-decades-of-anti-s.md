# Street Artist Mear One Connects Bitcoin to Decades of Anti-System Protest Culture

**TL;DR:** Veteran LA street artist Mear One discusses parallels between Bitcoin's monetary revolution and four decades of anti-war, anti-financial system graffiti activism in interview ahead of Bitcoin 2026.

## Key Points

- Mear One pioneered Melrose graffiti movement, has created anti-system street art since 1980s
- Artist participated in Be the Revolution tour (2004) and Occupy Wall Street protest art
- Views Bitcoin as continuation of permissionless, anonymous resistance culture from graffiti/punk scenes
- Connects Bitcoin's Genesis Block bank bailout message to decades of anti-banker war messaging
- 2008 financial crash shifted his focus to understanding monetary system mechanics
- Street art served as pre-internet tool for reaching masses with anti-establishment messaging
- Work being displayed at Bitcoin 2026 Conference as 'Relics of a Revolution' exhibition

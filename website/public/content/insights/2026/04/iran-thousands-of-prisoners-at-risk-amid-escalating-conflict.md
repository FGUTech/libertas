# Iran: Thousands of Prisoners at Risk Amid Escalating Conflict and Mass Executions

**TL;DR:** Human Rights Watch reports thousands of Iranian detainees, including political prisoners and activists, face dual threats from US/Israeli strikes and mass executions by authorities who continue arresting dissidents for documenting attacks.

## Key Points

- Thousands of prisoners in Iran at risk from military strikes and state-executed atrocities [Human Rights Watch]
- Iranian authorities conducting mass arbitrary detentions of protesters, activists, and dissidents since February 2026
- 446 people arrested for 'disturbing public opinion' and sharing footage/photos of strikes with media
- At least 8 men arbitrarily executed on political charges between March 18-31, 2026
- Prisoners detained in facilities targeted by US/Israeli strikes, including police stations and IRGC facilities
- Authorities threatening protesters with lethal force, warning they will be treated as 'enemies'
- Prison conditions deteriorating with reduced food, denied medical care, and violent suppression of protests
- Iran has legal mechanisms for humanitarian prisoner release during crises but refuses to implement them

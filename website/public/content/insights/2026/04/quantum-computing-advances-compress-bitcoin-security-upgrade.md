# Quantum Computing Advances Compress Bitcoin Security Upgrade Timeline

**TL;DR:** New research shows quantum computers may need only 10,000-500,000 qubits to break Bitcoin's encryption (down from 10M), accelerating development of quantum-resistant upgrades like BIP-360.

## Key Points

- Google and Caltech papers show quantum attack requirements dropped from 10M qubits to potentially 10,000-500,000 qubits
- Current quantum computers still far below threat levels (Google's Willow has ~100 qubits)
- BIP-360 proposal introduces new address format preventing public key exposure during transactions
- Bitcoin testnet for quantum resistance has attracted 50+ miners and 100+ cryptographers
- StarkWare's new 'Quantum Safe Bitcoin' (QSB) scheme proposed as protocol-compatible solution
- Existing Taproot upgrade provides foundation for quantum-resistant verification methods
- NIST finalized post-quantum crypto standards in 2024, federal agencies have 2035 deadline
- Bitcoin's decentralized nature requires consensus-based upgrades rather than mandated transitions

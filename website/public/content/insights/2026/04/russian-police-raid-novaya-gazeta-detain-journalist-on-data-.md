# Russian Police Raid Novaya Gazeta, Detain Journalist on Data Privacy Charges

**TL;DR:** Russian authorities raided independent newspaper Novaya Gazeta's Moscow office and detained journalist Oleg Roldugin under criminal charges for allegedly misusing personal data in reporting.

## Key Points

- Moscow police conducted 12-hour search of Novaya Gazeta office on April 9, 2026
- Journalist Oleg Roldugin detained for 48+ hours on charges of illegal use/transfer of personal data
- Criminal case filed under Article 272.1 alleging use of personal data to create 'negative content about Russian citizens'
- Authorities investigating potential ties to Latvia-based Novaya Gazeta Europe (banned as 'undesirable' in 2023)
- Over 10 staff held inside building during search; lawyers denied access for 7+ hours
- Charges carry up to 6 years prison under 'group activity causing significant damage' provision
- Escalation against outlet already stripped of print/online licenses in September 2022
- Pattern continues targeting of Nobel Prize winner Dmitry Muratov's publication with 6 journalists killed since 1993

# Hungary Files Espionage Charges Against Journalist Who Exposed Russian Influence Operations

**TL;DR:** Hungarian government charged investigative journalist Szabolcs Panyi with espionage after he reported on Russian influence operations, escalating press freedom attacks ahead of elections.

## Key Points

- Hungary filed espionage charges against Direkt36/VSquare journalist Szabolcs Panyi on March 26, 2026
- Charges followed Panyi's reporting on Russian influence operations ahead of April 12 parliamentary elections
- Government alleges Panyi spied 'in coordination with a foreign country' and intercepted communications between Hungarian Foreign Minister and Russian officials
- Panyi was previously surveilled with Pegasus spyware in 2021, indicating sustained government targeting
- Espionage conviction carries 8-15 year prison sentence depending on classification level
- State Sovereignty Protection Office labeled Panyi as 'financed and directed by European Commission and U.S. Democratic-party linked backers'
- Committee to Protect Journalists calls tactics 'straight out of Putin's playbook' and unprecedented attack on EU press freedom
- Pattern demonstrates escalating authoritarian tactics to silence independent media before elections

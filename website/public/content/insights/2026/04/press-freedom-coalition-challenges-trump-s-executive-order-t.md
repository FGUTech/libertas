# Press Freedom Coalition Challenges Trump's Executive Order Targeting Law Firms

**TL;DR:** 42 media organizations led by The Intercept filed amicus brief challenging Trump's executive order sanctioning law firms that represent his political opponents, arguing it undermines press freedom.

## Key Points

- The Intercept's Press Freedom Defense Fund leads coalition of 42 media organizations in legal challenge
- Trump executive order imposed sanctions on law firms representing his political opponents
- Four lower district courts already ruled the executive order unconstitutional
- Coalition filed amicus brief with U.S. Court of Appeals for D.C. Circuit to affirm lower court rulings
- Argument centers on executive order restricting press access to legal counsel, especially pro bono representation
- Coalition includes news organizations, press associations, and First Amendment attorneys with 500+ years collective experience
- Legal challenge positions independent legal counsel as essential infrastructure for press freedom

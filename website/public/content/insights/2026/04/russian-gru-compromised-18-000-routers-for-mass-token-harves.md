# Russian GRU Compromised 18,000+ Routers for Mass Token Harvesting

**TL;DR:** Russian military intelligence (GRU/APT28) hijacked DNS settings on 18,000+ vulnerable routers to intercept Microsoft OAuth tokens from 200+ orgs, bypassing MFA without malware.

## Key Points

- Forest Blizzard (APT28/Fancy Bear) compromised 18,000+ end-of-life SOHO routers at peak activity
- Attackers modified DNS settings to redirect traffic through GRU-controlled servers
- Campaign harvested OAuth authentication tokens from 200+ organizations and 5,000+ consumer devices
- Technique bypassed multi-factor authentication by intercepting post-login tokens
- Primarily targeted government agencies, foreign affairs ministries, and law enforcement
- Used known vulnerabilities in older Mikrotik and TP-Link routers without deploying malware
- Group adapted tactics after August 2025 NCSC report, scaling from targeted to mass compromise
- FCC responded by banning certification of foreign-made consumer routers citing national security

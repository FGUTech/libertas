# EFF Warns UN: Cybercrime Laws and Surveillance Targeting Human Rights Defenders

**TL;DR:** EFF submission to UN documents how cybercrime laws, digital surveillance, and platform policies are being weaponized against human rights defenders globally, with insufficient safeguards.

## Key Points

- EFF submitted analysis to UN Office of High Commissioner for Human Rights on threats to digital rights defenders
- Cybercrime and national security laws increasingly used to prosecute lawful expression and expand surveillance
- UK's Online Safety Act spreading 'duty of care' frameworks globally with weaker protections in some jurisdictions
- Internet shutdowns, throttling, and geo-blocking disrupting HRDs' ability to communicate and document abuses
- Cross-border spyware and biometric surveillance deployed against activists and journalists
- Platform content moderation systems removing documentation of human rights violations through automated enforcement
- EFF calls for narrowly tailored legal frameworks, independent oversight, and meaningful civil society engagement

# China Human Rights Lawyer Yu Wensheng Expected Release After 3-Year Detention

**TL;DR:** Human rights lawyer Yu Wensheng faces release April 13 after serving 3 years for 'inciting subversion' - part of systematic targeting of legal advocates challenging state power.

## Key Points

- Yu Wensheng expected release April 13, 2026 after 3-year sentence for 'inciting subversion of state power'
- Detained with wife Xu Yan in April 2023 while meeting EU officials in Beijing
- Yu defended 709 Crackdown lawyers and advocated for constitutional reforms limiting state power
- Wife Xu Yan released January 2025 but faces ongoing surveillance and harassment
- Family transferred 1000km from Beijing to Suzhou, hampering legal access and family visits
- Prison conditions reportedly harsh with inadequate heat, nutrition, and restricted purchases
- Authorities blocked Xu Yan's legal certification despite passing requirements
- Pattern demonstrates systematic deterrence against human rights defenders and international engagement
- Multiple UN experts raised concerns about arbitrary detention and ill-treatment

# Ghana's Anti-LGBTQ+ Bill Threatens Digital Rights and Online Privacy

**TL;DR:** Ghana's proposed Human Sexual Rights and Family Values Bill 2025 would force social media censorship, mandate public reporting of suspected LGBTQ+ individuals, and criminalize online advocacy with up to 10 years prison.

## Key Points

- Access Now and Rightify Ghana oppose Ghana's Human Sexual Rights and Family Values Bill 2025
- Bill would force social media companies to censor LGBTQ+ content
- Requires public to report suspected LGBTQ+ people to police and religious leaders
- Online advocates for LGBTQ+ rights could face up to 10 years in prison
- Creates mandatory reporting system that could enable doxxing and extrajudicial violence
- Rightify Ghana reports increased entrapment and blackmail on dating apps and messaging platforms
- Bill violates digital rights including privacy and freedom of expression online
- Would prevent documentation of discrimination and violence against LGBTQ+ people

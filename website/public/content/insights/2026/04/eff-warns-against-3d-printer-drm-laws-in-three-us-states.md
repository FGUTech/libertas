# EFF Warns Against 3D Printer DRM Laws in Three US States

**TL;DR:** Three US states propose laws requiring 3D printer manufacturers to install 'print-blocking' software to detect gun-like shapes, effectively mandating DRM that would restrict open-source software and enable vendor surveillance.

## Key Points

- Three US states proposed laws mandating 3D printers include algorithmic 'print-blocking' to detect forbidden shapes
- Bills would require printers only work with manufacturer software, criminalizing bypasses or reselling unrestricted devices
- EFF argues this creates DRM system that punishes law-abiding users while being easily bypassed by criminals
- Would eliminate open-source alternatives like OrcaSlicer, forcing users into vendor lock-in arrangements
- Print-blocking targets 'ghost guns' but these are typically mass-produced kits, not home-printed items
- Criminal penalties proposed for anyone bypassing censorship software or circumventing restrictions
- Pattern mirrors historical DRM implementation that enables anti-competitive manufacturer control
- Threatens 3D printing's role as democratized manufacturing tool for innovation and research

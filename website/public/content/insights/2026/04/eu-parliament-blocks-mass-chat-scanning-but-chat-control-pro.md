# EU Parliament Blocks Mass Chat Scanning, But 'Chat Control' Proposal Still Alive

**TL;DR:** EU Parliament voted to end temporary derogation allowing voluntary mass scanning of private messages, but the broader Chat Control proposal for mandatory CSAM detection continues negotiations with focus on age verification.

## Key Points

- EU Parliament voted against extending interim derogation from e-Privacy rules that allowed voluntary chat scanning
- Major tech companies (Google, Meta, Microsoft, Snap) signal intent to continue voluntary scanning despite legal uncertainty
- EU member states previously abandoned forced scanning of encrypted messages from Chat Control proposal
- Current Chat Control negotiations focus on 'risk mitigation' including problematic age verification requirements
- Mass scanning now risks breaching EU law without specific legal basis, unlike in US where no comprehensive privacy law exists
- Big tech has history of non-compliance with EU privacy rules, making enforcement uncertain
- EFF warns proposal could return through backdoor via 'voluntary' activities becoming compliance expectations

## Deep Dive

## Policy Context

The EU's Chat Control proposal represents one of the most significant threats to encrypted communications in recent years. While the most draconian element—mandatory scanning of encrypted messages—was abandoned by EU member states, the broader framework remains under negotiation. The expiration of the e-Privacy derogation creates a legal vacuum where continued scanning practices by major platforms may violate EU law.

## Technical and Legal Implications

The legal uncertainty creates a complex enforcement landscape. Unlike the US, where platforms can scan communications without comprehensive federal privacy protections, EU law generally prohibits indiscriminate scanning without specific legal basis. However, major tech companies' public commitment to 'continue voluntary action' suggests they may risk legal exposure rather than halt existing detection systems. This sets up potential confrontation between platform policies and EU privacy law.

## What This Means for Freedom Tech

The ongoing negotiations around 'risk mitigation' measures pose significant concerns for communication privacy. Age verification requirements could create new surveillance infrastructure, while reframing mandatory scanning as 'voluntary activities' could achieve similar results through regulatory pressure. The characterization as a 'zombie proposal' reflects the persistent nature of these surveillance initiatives—victories may be temporary without sustained resistance.

## Strategic Considerations

For builders and activists, this development highlights both opportunities and risks. The legal uncertainty around mass scanning creates potential leverage for privacy advocates, but also demonstrates how surveillance proposals can evolve rather than disappear. The focus on age verification as an alternative approach suggests future battles will center on identity verification systems that could undermine anonymous communication.

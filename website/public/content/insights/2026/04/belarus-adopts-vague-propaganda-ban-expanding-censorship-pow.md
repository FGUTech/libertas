# Belarus Adopts Vague 'Propaganda' Ban Expanding Censorship Powers

**TL;DR:** Belarus parliament passed sweeping law banning 'propaganda' of LGBT relationships and childlessness with deliberately vague definitions, mirroring Russia's playbook for suppressing dissent and controlling discourse.

## Key Points

- Belarus parliament adopted law on April 2 banning 'propaganda' of same-sex relationships, gender reassignment, and childlessness
- 'Propaganda' vaguely defined as disseminating 'appealing' information 'intended to influence citizens' perceptions'
- Penalties include fines up to $310 for individuals, $2,320 for organizations, plus community service and detention for content accessible to minors
- Law intentionally conflates LGBT content with pedophilia to stigmatize and enable broader suppression
- Builds on 2024 measures classifying LGBT depictions as 'pornography' and banning related advertising
- Mirrors Russia's 'LGBT extremism' laws that enabled arrests, fines, and civil society organization closures
- Vague definitions give authorities sweeping discretion to silence journalists, educators, health professionals, and activists
- Represents broader assault on independent thought and civil society under guise of 'traditional values'

# Vietnamese Journalist Gets 8.5 Years for Social Media Posts Critical of State

**TL;DR:** Vietnam sentenced journalist Huynh Ngoc Tuan to 8.5 years in prison for Facebook posts and videos deemed anti-state propaganda, highlighting the need for censorship-resistant publishing tools.

## Key Points

- Vietnamese court sentenced journalist Huynh Ngoc Tuan to 8 years, 6 months under anti-propaganda laws
- Conviction based on 11 live videos and 21 Facebook posts about politics and human rights
- Tuan held incommunicado for 6 months without lawyer access before closed one-day trial
- Family subjected to ongoing harassment, surveillance, and equipment confiscation
- Tuan previously served 10 years for critical writings; daughter served nearly 3 years for blogging
- Vietnam currently imprisons 17 journalists, among world's worst for press freedom
- Case demonstrates vulnerability of centralized platforms to state censorship and surveillance

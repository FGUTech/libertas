# US Media Consolidation Creates Centralized Censorship Infrastructure

**TL;DR:** Six corporations now control almost all US media, with the FCC enabling consolidation that creates centralized points for government censorship pressure, illustrated by recent Trump administration editorial compliance.

## Key Points

- Media ownership consolidated from 50 companies (1983) to 6 corporations controlling nearly all US media today
- Latest Tegna-Nexstar merger gives one company reach to 80% of US TV households despite antitrust concerns
- FCC Chairman Brendan Carr accused of politicizing agency to enable consolidation rather than prevent it
- Documented censorship: Nexstar blocked Jimmy Kimmel show across affiliates after Trump administration criticism
- CBS settled $16M Trump lawsuit before parent company merger approval, demonstrating compliance pressure
- Consolidation eliminates local news sources, creating 'news deserts' and reducing information diversity
- Pattern mirrors authoritarian consolidation documented in Hungary, India, Malaysia, and Venezuela

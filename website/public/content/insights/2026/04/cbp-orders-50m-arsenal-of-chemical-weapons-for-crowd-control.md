# CBP Orders $50M Arsenal of Chemical Weapons for Crowd Control

**TL;DR:** U.S. Customs and Border Protection plans to purchase up to $50 million in tear gas, projectiles, and other 'less lethal' weapons, including 123 different munition types for potential use against protesters.

## Key Points

- CBP procurement documents reveal plans to buy 123 types of 'less lethal' munitions across 10 categories
- Arsenal includes CS tear gas (banned for military use) and OC pepper spray in various delivery systems
- Purchase quantities include 242,000+ pyrotechnic canisters and 100,000+ impact munition rounds
- Weapons include 'ferret rounds' designed to penetrate barriers and 175-decibel sound grenades
- Medical experts warn of reproductive health impacts from chemical exposure and permanent hearing damage
- Procurement follows documented abuse of similar weapons against protesters during previous administration
- Rubber-ball grenades and scattershot projectiles mirror weapons used by Chilean and Iranian forces against demonstrators

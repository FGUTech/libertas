# EFF Highlights Police Surveillance Mission Creep in Latest Newsletter

**TL;DR:** EFF's latest newsletter examines how police surveillance tech sold for serious crimes gets repurposed for traffic enforcement and protest monitoring, plus NSA reform updates.

## Key Points

- EFF newsletter focuses on surveillance 'mission creep' - technology marketed for preventing serious crimes being used for routine enforcement
- Covers license plate readers normalizing mass surveillance through expanded use cases
- Discusses urgent need for NSA spying reforms and FISA extension issues
- Reports Supreme Court victory for internet access regarding ISP copyright enforcement requirements
- Features interview with EFF Privacy Litigation Director Adam Schwartz on mission creep examples
- Newsletter now available as podcast on major platforms with news quiz format
- Pattern demonstrates how surveillance tools consistently expand beyond original stated purposes

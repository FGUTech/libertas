# US Bans All Foreign-Made Consumer Routers, Requires FCC Approval

**TL;DR:** US now requires FCC approval for all foreign-made routers, citing supply chain vulnerabilities and cybersecurity risks. Companies must disclose foreign investors and plan US manufacturing to get approval.

## Key Points

- Executive Branch banned import/sale of new foreign-made consumer routers without FCC approval
- Policy cites supply chain vulnerabilities and cybersecurity risks to critical infrastructure
- Router manufacturers must disclose foreign investors and create US manufacturing plans
- DOD and DHS can exempt specific routers, but no exceptions listed yet
- Affects major brands like Netgear (US company manufacturing abroad)
- Starlink WiFi router appears to be main US-manufactured exception currently
- Policy will likely increase router costs as US manufacturing is more expensive
- Could impact freedom tech tools that depend on router functionality and customization

# CPJ Issues 10-Point Plan to Restore Press Freedom in Hungary Ahead of Election

**TL;DR:** Committee to Protect Journalists outlines steps to dismantle Hungary's sophisticated media capture system, including ending Pegasus surveillance of reporters and abolishing Russia-style 'foreign agent' laws.

## Key Points

- CPJ calls on Hungarian election candidates to commit to 10-point press freedom restoration plan ahead of April 12 elections
- Orbán's Fidesz party controls ~80% of Hungary's media market through direct and indirect mechanisms
- Plan includes abolishing Sovereignty Protection Office and shelving Russia-style 'foreign agent' bill targeting independent media
- Demands end to state-sponsored smear campaigns labeling journalists as 'foreign agents' and 'traitors'
- Calls for protection from unlawful surveillance, specifically addressing 2019 Pegasus spyware deployment against journalists
- Requires reform of public service media receiving 415 million euros annually without independent oversight
- Addresses systematic barriers to information access through weakened FOIA and denied press conference access
- Notes Hungary's failure to adopt GDPR Article 85 journalistic exemptions, treating reporters as standard data controllers
- European Court of Human Rights has repeatedly condemned Hungary's lack of surveillance safeguards for journalists
- Tisza party under Péter Magyar poses serious electoral challenge to ruling Fidesz party

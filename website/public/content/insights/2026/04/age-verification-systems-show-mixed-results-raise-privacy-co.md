# Age Verification Systems Show Mixed Results, Raise Privacy Concerns for All Users

**TL;DR:** New research on UK and Australia's age verification laws shows limited effectiveness while creating privacy risks for all internet users, with children easily bypassing restrictions.

## Key Points

- UK's Online Safety Act led to one-third decline in pornography site visits but caused over-moderation of safe content
- Australia's social media ban for under-16s resulted in removal of 4.7 million accounts
- Research shows children routinely circumvent age restrictions at account creation stage
- Age verification systems create privacy risks for all users, not just children
- Systems block access to legitimate educational content including sexual health information
- Global deployment has resulted in 'privacy-invasive or ineffective methods' according to 2024 study
- Effectiveness remains unclear with regulators acknowledging need for more long-term data

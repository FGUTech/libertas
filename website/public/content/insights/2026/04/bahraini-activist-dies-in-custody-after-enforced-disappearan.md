# Bahraini Activist Dies in Custody After Enforced Disappearance, Digital Surveillance Exposed

**TL;DR:** Sayed Mohamed Almosawi died in Bahraini custody with torture signs after 9-day enforced disappearance. Authorities accessed his WhatsApp and tracked location data during detention.

## Key Points

- Sayed Mohamed Almosawi, 32, forcibly disappeared March 19, 2026, found dead March 27 with torture signs
- Medical expert confirmed injuries consistent with repeated blunt force trauma and systematic torture
- Authorities accessed victim's WhatsApp account and location data during disappearance
- National Intelligence Agency detained him on unsubstantiated espionage charges
- Family denied custody when contacted, constituting enforced disappearance under international law
- Part of broader crackdown: 286+ detained since regional war began for social media posts and protests
- Case demonstrates how digital surveillance enables state repression and enforced disappearance
- WhatsApp messages marked as read near police station, showing real-time digital access by authorities

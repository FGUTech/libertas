# 42 Media Orgs Challenge Trump's Executive Order Sanctioning Law Firms

**TL;DR:** The Intercept's Press Freedom Defense Fund leads 42 media organizations filing amicus brief to block Trump's executive order that sanctions law firms representing his political opponents.

## Key Points

- 42 media organizations filed amicus brief in D.C. Circuit Court challenging Trump executive order
- Executive order sanctions law firms for providing pro bono representation to Trump's political opponents
- Four lower courts already ruled the executive order unconstitutional
- Brief argues order restricts press organizations' access to legal counsel
- Coalition includes news outlets, advocacy groups, and First Amendment attorneys
- The Intercept's Press Freedom Defense Fund led the legal challenge
- Order specifically threatens outlets relying on pro bono or reduced-fee legal representation
- Legal team argues press requires oppositional relationship with government to function

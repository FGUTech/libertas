# Amazon Uses Computer Crime Laws to Block AI Price Comparison Tool

**TL;DR:** Amazon successfully sued Perplexity's AI browser Comet under CFAA for helping users compare prices, setting dangerous precedent that could criminalize research and competition.

## Key Points

- Amazon sued Perplexity over Comet, an AI browser that helps users find best prices and make purchases
- Federal district court ruled Perplexity violated Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA) by accessing Amazon's public website
- Court relied on problematic Facebook v Power Ventures precedent rather than better hiQ Labs reasoning
- EFF filed amicus brief supporting Perplexity's Ninth Circuit appeal, warning of broad consequences
- Ruling could criminalize legitimate research by allowing companies to ban researchers via simple notification
- Decision enables anti-competitive behavior by letting companies block price comparison and data scraping tools
- Case represents corporate weaponization of computer crime laws against consumer choice tools

# Hack-for-Hire Campaign Targets Egyptian Journalists with Persistent Spear-Phishing

**TL;DR:** Access Now exposes sophisticated hack-for-hire campaign targeting Egyptian journalists Mostafa Al-A'sar and Ahmed Eltantawy through persistent spear-phishing attacks from 2023-2024, likely linked to Asian threat actors.

## Key Points

- Access Now's Digital Security Helpline documented hack-for-hire campaign against two Egyptian journalists and government critics
- Targets: Mostafa Al-A'sar and Ahmed Eltantawy, both previously faced political imprisonment
- Persistent spear-phishing attacks from 2023-2024 aimed to steal credentials and financial data
- Lookout mobile security identified likely connection to Asian threat actor
- At least one attack appears to originate from Egypt, though full attribution uncertain
- Similar attack documented against Lebanese journalist in 2025, likely same actor
- Report highlights spear-phishing as cheaper alternative to commercial spyware
- Campaign demonstrates sophisticated knowledge of targets and their networks

## Deep Dive

## Context and Threat Landscape

This investigation reveals the expanding ecosystem of hack-for-hire operations targeting civil society in MENA. The campaign's targeting of journalists who have already faced state persecution suggests a coordinated effort to maintain pressure on independent voices through digital means. The persistence and sophistication of the attacks - spanning over a year with detailed target knowledge - indicates professional-grade operations rather than opportunistic attacks.

## Technical and Operational Characteristics

The spear-phishing campaign demonstrates several concerning trends in digital repression. The attacks used sophisticated social engineering, impersonating legitimate contacts and services to harvest credentials and financial information. The collaboration between Access Now and Lookout provided forensic analysis linking the campaign to Asian threat actors, though the complexity of hack-for-hire operations makes definitive attribution challenging. The cross-border nature - with Egyptian targets, Asian operators, and similar attacks in Lebanon - illustrates the globalized nature of digital mercenary services.

## Implications for Digital Security

This case study reinforces the shift toward hack-for-hire services as a cost-effective alternative to expensive commercial spyware. For activists and journalists in authoritarian contexts, this represents a democratization of sophisticated attack capabilities that previously required significant state resources. The persistence and customization of these attacks suggests that standard security awareness training may be insufficient against well-resourced adversaries with detailed target intelligence.

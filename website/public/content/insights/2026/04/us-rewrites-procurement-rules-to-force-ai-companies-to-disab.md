# US Rewrites Procurement Rules to Force AI Companies to Disable Safety Guardrails

**TL;DR:** New GSA procurement rules would require AI contractors to license systems for 'all lawful purposes' and disable safety guardrails that refuse government requests, effectively weaponizing procurement against AI safety measures.

## Key Points

- General Services Administration (GSA) proposing new federal procurement rules for AI systems
- Rules would require contractors to license AI tools for 'all lawful purposes' to government
- Companies must disable safety guardrails that might refuse government data requests
- Move follows public dispute between Department of Defense and Anthropic over surveillance use
- EFF and partners filed joint comments opposing the rules as threat to AI safety
- Provisions would become standard in all federal contracts if adopted
- Government using procurement power to override company resistance to surveillance applications
- Rules include 'anti-woke' requirements that are 'technologically incoherent' [EFF]

# Trump Admin Seeks Medical Records of 8M Federal Workers Through Insurance Companies

**TL;DR:** Trump administration quietly proposed requiring insurance companies to hand over detailed medical records of 8+ million federal workers, raising major surveillance and privacy concerns among experts.

## Key Points

- Office of Personnel Management seeks 'service use and cost data' from 65 insurance companies
- Data collection would include medical claims, pharmacy records, diagnoses, and provider information
- Affects 8+ million federal workers, retirees, and their families
- Proposal was quietly revealed in December notice with vague justification
- Legal and health policy experts raising immediate privacy concerns
- Unprecedented scope of government medical surveillance of its own workforce
- Demonstrates expansion of surveillance capabilities beyond traditional targets

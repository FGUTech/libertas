# Google Moves Quantum Cryptography Deadline to 2029, EFF Warns of Encryption 'Y2K Moment'

**TL;DR:** Google accelerated post-quantum cryptography deadline to 2029 due to breakthrough research. Current encryption protecting Signal, websites, and secure communications will be vulnerable to quantum attacks.

## Key Points

- Google moved quantum preparedness deadline from 2030s to 2029 (33 months away) based on new research papers
- Quantum computers will break current public key cryptography used in HTTPS, Signal, iMessage authentication
- 'Store-now-decrypt-later' attacks already possible - quantum computers will decrypt years of captured traffic
- Symmetric encryption (disk encryption) remains quantum-resistant, but key exchange and authentication are vulnerable
- Signal and iMessage have announced post-quantum implementations, other platforms may be behind schedule
- Hardware deployments like Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) won't upgrade in time
- Users should avoid AI features in encrypted chats that could leak content to vulnerable cloud processing
- NGINX released post-quantum support in version 26.04, Ubuntu rolling out updates

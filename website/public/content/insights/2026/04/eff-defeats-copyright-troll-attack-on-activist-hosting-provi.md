# EFF Defeats Copyright Troll Attack on Activist Hosting Provider May First

**TL;DR:** EFF successfully defended May First Movement Technology against baseless copyright demands from Higbee & Associates, protecting critical hosting infrastructure for social justice groups worldwide.

## Key Points

- Higbee & Associates targeted May First Movement Technology with copyright claim over photo posted by hosted human rights group
- May First provides web hosting to social justice organizations globally but didn't post the allegedly infringing content
- EFF represented May First, arguing hosting providers aren't liable for user-posted content under copyright law
- Higbee backed down after EFF explained legal protections for service providers who promptly remove content
- Case highlights how copyright trolling threatens infrastructure critical to activist organizations
- Statutory damages up to $150,000 per work enable mass demand letter campaigns targeting innocent parties
- Victory establishes precedent protecting hosting providers from liability for user content

# Georgia Deports Azerbaijani Journalist Despite EU Court Order, Exposes Cross-Border Collusion

**TL;DR:** Georgian authorities forcibly deported exiled Azerbaijani journalist Afgan Sadigov in defiance of European Court interim protections, using fabricated charges to circumvent extradition restrictions.

## Key Points

- Georgia deported Azel.tv editor Afgan Sadigov to Azerbaijan despite European Court of Human Rights interim measure prohibiting transfer
- Sadigov faces persecution for reporting on government corruption; previously sentenced to 7 years on dubious extortion charges
- Georgian court convicted him at 4am on social media 'insult' charges, ordered immediate expulsion with 3-year entry ban
- Azerbaijani authorities strategically dropped criminal charges, then Georgia used administrative pretext to bypass extradition protections
- Pattern suggests coordinated effort between governments to circumvent international human rights obligations
- Demonstrates vulnerability of journalists relying on traditional asylum processes against state-level cooperation
- Case exposes how administrative charges can be weaponized to bypass judicial protections for press freedom

# 40 Uyghurs Forcibly Returned to China by Thailand Remain Missing After One Year

**TL;DR:** Thailand deported 40 Uyghur men to China in February 2025 despite torture risks. Their whereabouts remain unknown, constituting forced disappearances under international law.

## Key Points

- Thai authorities forcibly returned 40 Uyghur men to China in February 2025 after decade in detention
- Men had fled systematic persecution in Xinjiang and faced grave risks of torture upon return
- Deportation violated international nonrefoulement principle prohibiting return to persecution
- Family members abroad have received no contact since deportation occurred
- Chinese government's refusal to report their fate constitutes forced disappearances under international law
- UNHCR was denied access to assess refugee status before deportation
- Pattern of Uyghur deportations documented across multiple countries under Chinese pressure
- Highlights critical need for secure communications and identity protection tools for at-risk populations

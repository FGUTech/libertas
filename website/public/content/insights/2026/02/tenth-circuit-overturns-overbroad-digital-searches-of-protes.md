# Tenth Circuit Overturns Overbroad Digital Searches of Protesters

**TL;DR:** Federal appeals court ruled Colorado Springs police violated Fourth Amendment with sweeping device searches of housing protester, denying qualified immunity in rare victory for digital privacy rights.

## Key Points

- Tenth Circuit overturned dismissal of lawsuit challenging warrants to search protester's devices and nonprofit's social media
- Police obtained warrants for two-month search of all photos, videos, emails, texts, and location data for simple assault allegation
- Warrants included unlimited keyword searches for broad terms like 'bike,' 'assault,' 'celebration,' and 'right'
- Court found warrants 'overbroad and lacking in particularity' regarding scope and duration
- Officers denied qualified immunity protection - extremely rare outcome in search warrant cases
- Police also searched Facebook page of Chinook Center nonprofit that organized protest despite no criminal allegations
- Creates important precedent protecting protesters' digital privacy rights under Fourth Amendment

# OONI Explorer Launches Thematic Censorship Tracking Pages

**TL;DR:** OONI Explorer released new dedicated pages tracking global censorship of social media, news media, and circumvention tools with charts and data-driven reports.

## Key Points

- OONI Explorer launched three new thematic censorship pages with interactive charts
- New pages track blocking of social media, news media, and circumvention tools globally
- Data comes from OONI's measurement network - the world's largest open censorship dataset
- Pages provide country-specific blocking data and trends over time
- Tool enables researchers and activists to identify censorship patterns
- Represents significant improvement in accessibility of censorship intelligence
- Previously this data required manual analysis of raw OONI measurements

## Deep Dive

## Context and Significance

OONI (Open Observatory of Network Interference) has operated as the Tor Project's internet measurement initiative for years, collecting data on network interference through a global volunteer network. The new thematic pages represent a major usability improvement, transforming raw measurement data into accessible intelligence about three critical areas of censorship.

## Technical Implementation

The thematic pages aggregate OONI's distributed measurement data into focused views covering social media platforms, news outlets, and circumvention tools. This approach allows researchers to quickly identify blocking patterns without needing to parse individual probe measurements or understand OONI's technical methodology.

## Intelligence Value for Freedom Tech

These pages provide actionable intelligence for several use cases: activists can identify which platforms remain accessible during crackdowns, circumvention tool developers can track blocking of their services, and researchers can document censorship trends over time. The country-specific data enables targeted responses to local censorship patterns.

## What Builders Should Consider

This represents the gold standard for censorship monitoring infrastructure. The open dataset approach and thematic organization could be replicated for other types of freedom tech monitoring. Developers should consider integrating OONI data into their threat models and using these pages to validate their tools' censorship resistance in specific regions.

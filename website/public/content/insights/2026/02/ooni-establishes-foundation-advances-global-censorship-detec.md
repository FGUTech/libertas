# OONI Establishes Foundation, Advances Global Censorship Detection Tools in 2024

**TL;DR:** OONI launched a new foundation and significantly upgraded its censorship detection toolkit with OONI Run v2, multiplatform apps, and enhanced data analysis capabilities to support global internet freedom monitoring.

## Key Points

- Open Observatory of Network Interference Foundation (OONI) ETS officially established in Rome, Italy in February 2024
- Launched OONI Run v2 for coordinated censorship measurement campaigns
- Developing multiplatform OONI Probe app to expand measurement coverage
- Enhanced OONI Explorer with thematic censorship findings presentation
- Introduced new experiments and expanded testing model for richer input analysis
- Advanced data analysis methods for better censorship detection accuracy
- Conducted OONI Partner Gathering 2024 in Malaysia with global research community
- Published research reports and censorship findings accessible via OONI Explorer
- Expanded localization efforts including Test Lists Editor and Outreach Kit
- Strengthened partnerships and conducted workshops globally to build measurement capacity

## Deep Dive

## Foundation and Institutional Development

OONI's establishment as a formal foundation in Italy represents a significant milestone for the internet freedom measurement ecosystem. The legal structure provides institutional stability for one of the most critical censorship detection platforms globally, ensuring continuity of operations for researchers, activists, and technologists who depend on OONI's data.

## Technical Infrastructure Advances

The launch of OONI Run v2 addresses a key limitation in coordinated censorship measurement. The platform now supports more sophisticated testing campaigns, allowing researchers to design targeted measurements across multiple networks and geographies simultaneously. The multiplatform OONI Probe development suggests OONI is working to reduce technical barriers for users across different operating systems and devices.

## Research and Data Analysis Evolution

OONI's advancement in data analysis methods is particularly significant given the increasing sophistication of censorship techniques. ISPs and governments are deploying more nuanced blocking methods that require correspondingly advanced detection capabilities. The expansion of OONI's testing model to support "richer testing input" likely addresses the need to detect DNS manipulation, traffic shaping, and other subtle interference methods.

## Global Community Building

The Malaysia partner gathering and expanded workshop series indicate OONI's focus on building local measurement capacity. This distributed approach is crucial for comprehensive censorship monitoring, as local knowledge and sustained measurement from diverse vantage points are essential for detecting and documenting internet restrictions effectively.

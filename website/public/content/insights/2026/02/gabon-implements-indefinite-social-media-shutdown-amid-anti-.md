# Gabon Implements Indefinite Social Media Shutdown Amid Anti-Government Protests

**TL;DR:** Gabon's communications authority ordered indefinite social media blocking of Facebook, WhatsApp, TikTok, Instagram and YouTube during ongoing protests, with #KeepItOn coalition demanding immediate restoration.

## Key Points

- Gabon's High Authority for Communication ordered indefinite social media shutdown on February 17, 2026
- OONI confirms blocking of Facebook, WhatsApp, TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube as of February 18, 2026
- Shutdown coincides with ongoing anti-government protests and labor strikes
- Government claims measures prevent 'spread of false information' and protect national unity
- Coalition of 345+ human rights organizations from 105 countries demands immediate reversal
- Gabon has history of shutdowns: 2023 elections, 2021 COVID protests, 2019 coup attempt, 2016 protests
- ISPs Moov Gabon, Gabon Telecom, and Airtel Gabon called to refuse enforcement of shutdown orders
- Pattern demonstrates recurring use of digital repression during political instability

## Deep Dive

## Context and Pattern Analysis

Gabon's latest social media shutdown represents a continuation of a well-documented pattern of digital repression during periods of political instability. The #KeepItOn coalition's documentation reveals systematic use of internet controls: the 2023 election shutdown lasted from August 26-30 during disputed results, 2021 throttling during COVID protest suppression, 2019 complete internet blackout following coup attempt, and 2016 shutdown during post-election violence. This pattern suggests digital repression has become a standard crisis response tool regardless of the ruling administration, as current President Nguema's government is employing identical tactics to his predecessor Ali Bongo's regime.

## Technical Implementation and Verification

The Open Observatory of Network Interference (OONI) provides technical verification of the blocking, confirming that major platforms including Facebook, WhatsApp, TikTok, Instagram, and YouTube are being actively filtered. The shutdown appears to be implemented at the ISP level through the three major providers: Moov Gabon, Gabon Telecom, and Airtel Gabon. Unlike complete internet shutdowns, this targeted social media blocking allows basic internet functionality while specifically disrupting platforms used for organizing and information sharing during protests.

## Implications for Freedom Tech Development

This incident highlights several critical considerations for censorship-resistant communication tools. The targeting of specific platforms while maintaining basic internet connectivity suggests authorities are becoming more sophisticated in their approach, potentially making traditional VPN circumvention more detectable. The coalition's emphasis on ISP responsibility and their direct naming of telecommunications providers indicates potential pressure points for both advocacy and technical countermeasures. The recurring nature of shutdowns in Gabon makes it an ideal testing ground for mesh networking solutions and decentralized communication protocols.

## Strategic Response Patterns

The #KeepItOn coalition's response demonstrates coordinated advocacy combining legal frameworks (citing specific UN resolutions and African Charter provisions), technical documentation (OONI measurements), and direct pressure on both government and private sector actors. Their explicit call for ISPs to refuse government orders represents an escalation in targeting the implementation layer of censorship infrastructure, which could inform both policy advocacy and technical circumvention strategies.

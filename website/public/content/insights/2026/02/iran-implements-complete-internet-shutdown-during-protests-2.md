# Iran Implements Complete Internet Shutdown During Protests, 2,403+ Killed

**TL;DR:** Iran shut down all internet and mobile connectivity nationwide on Jan 8, 2026 amid currency protests. Over 2,400 protesters killed, 18,000+ arrested as authorities jam even satellite internet to conceal violence.

## Key Points

- Complete nationwide internet shutdown began Jan 8, 2026 following currency collapse protests starting Dec 28, 2025
- Cloudflare data confirms internet traffic dropped to near zero at 18:45 UTC on Jan 8
- At least 2,403 protesters killed and 18,000+ arrested as of Jan 13, 2026 [HRANA]
- Authorities initially throttled internet by 35% and blocked circumvention tools before total shutdown
- Satellite internet services also disrupted through signal jamming and equipment confiscation
- Tech activists and internet freedom community achieved limited connectivity restoration during shutdown
- Pattern matches previous Iran shutdowns during 2019 and 2022 protests that preceded mass violence
- Access Now and 40+ organizations demand immediate restoration and accountability

## Deep Dive

## Context and Escalation Pattern

Iran's latest internet shutdown follows a documented playbook of escalating digital repression during unrest. What began as protests over currency collapse on December 28, 2025 quickly evolved into broader anti-regime demonstrations. The government's response escalated from 35% throttling and circumvention tool blocking on December 29 to complete connectivity severing on January 8 - a pattern consistent with Iran's 2019 and 2022 protest responses where shutdowns preceded mass casualties.

## Technical Scope and Countermeasures

This shutdown represents one of the most comprehensive connectivity disruptions documented, extending beyond traditional terrestrial networks to include satellite internet jamming and equipment confiscation. The targeting of satellite services is particularly significant as these typically serve as backup channels during terrestrial shutdowns. Despite these measures, local tech activists and the broader internet freedom community managed to restore limited connectivity, suggesting some circumvention techniques remain viable even under extreme conditions.

## Implications for Freedom Tech

The shutdown's comprehensive nature - confirmed by Cloudflare's traffic data showing near-zero connectivity - demonstrates the critical importance of distributed, hardened communication tools. The fact that even satellite services faced active jamming indicates state-level sophistication in disruption capabilities. However, the partial restoration achieved by activists suggests resilient technologies and networks can still function, providing crucial intelligence on what works under extreme censorship conditions.

## What This Means for Builders

This case study provides real-world validation that internet shutdowns serve as early warning indicators for mass violence, with over 2,400 confirmed deaths following the connectivity cut. The comprehensive nature of disruptions - including satellite jamming - should inform threat models for censorship-resistant tools. The success of activist restoration efforts, even under total shutdown conditions, indicates that properly designed and distributed freedom tech can maintain some functionality even under nation-state level suppression.

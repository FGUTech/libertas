# Bangladesh's 2026 Election Tests Digital Democracy After Youth-Led Uprising

**TL;DR:** Bangladesh conducts major election 18 months after youth uprising toppled autocratic regime, but complex referendum structure, forced digitization, and low women's representation reveal democratic transition challenges.

## Key Points

- Bangladesh holds parliamentary election and constitutional referendum on February 12, 2026, following 2024 student-led overthrow of Sheikh Hasina
- Referendum forces yes/no vote on 185-word constitutional package with no option to approve individual reforms
- If elected parliament fails to finalize amendments within 270 days, unelected interim government's bill automatically becomes law
- Election Commission banned physical campaign materials, forcing campaigns entirely online for first time
- 40% of voters are under 37, with youth parties like National Citizen Party using TikTok and Instagram for mobilization
- Digital shift excludes women, rural, elderly, and low-income populations from political discourse
- Women represent only 4.22% of candidates despite being 50%+ of population and having higher turnout historically
- Bangladesh Nationalist Party nominated just 3.5% women candidates, lowest rate since 1991

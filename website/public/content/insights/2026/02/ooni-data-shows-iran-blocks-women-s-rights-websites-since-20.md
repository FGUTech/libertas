# OONI Data Shows Iran Blocks Women's Rights Websites Since 2014

**TL;DR:** OONI measurement data documents systematic blocking of women's rights websites in Iran since at least 2014, with censorship intensifying despite 2022 Women, Life, Freedom protests.

## Key Points

- OONI network measurements confirm Iran blocks women's rights websites since 2014
- Censorship has persisted despite widespread Women, Life, Freedom protests following Mahsa Jina Amini's death in 2022
- UN reports that repression of women in Iran has intensified in recent years
- OONI Explorer data provides technical evidence of systematic information control
- Women's rights websites join broader pattern of severely restricted information access in Iran
- Demonstrates need for censorship circumvention tools targeting gender-focused activism
- Technical measurement data offers verifiable documentation of state repression

## Deep Dive

## Context and Scale

The Open Observatory of Network Interference (OONI) has documented a decade-long pattern of systematic internet censorship targeting women's rights information in Iran. This technical evidence provides concrete proof of information warfare tactics that predate and outlasted the 2022 Women, Life, Freedom movement protests that erupted following Mahsa Jina Amini's death in police custody.

## Technical Documentation of Repression

OONI's network measurement data offers verifiable, technical evidence of censorship that goes beyond anecdotal reports. The fact that women's rights websites have been consistently blocked since 2014 demonstrates a sustained, systematic approach to controlling information access around gender equality. This data is particularly valuable because it provides empirical proof that can't be dismissed as political rhetoric.

## Implications for Freedom Tech Builders

The persistence of this censorship despite international attention and mass protests highlights the critical need for robust circumvention tools. The Iranian government's sustained focus on blocking women's rights content suggests these restrictions are seen as strategically important to maintaining control. Builders should consider that gender-focused activism faces particularly intense digital repression and may require specialized approaches to information access and secure communications.

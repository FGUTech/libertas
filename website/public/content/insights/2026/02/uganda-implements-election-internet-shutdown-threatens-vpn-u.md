# Uganda Implements Election Internet Shutdown, Threatens VPN Users with Device Targeting

**TL;DR:** Uganda shut down internet and blocked major platforms during 2026 elections despite international pressure. Authorities warn VPN users could be targeted via their devices, highlighting escalating digital surveillance threats.

## Key Points

- Uganda Communications Commission ordered 95% internet shutdown during January 2026 elections [Cloudflare Radar/IODA data]
- WhatsApp, Instagram, X, Facebook, and mobile money services remain blocked even after partial restoration
- UCC explicitly threatened VPN users could be 'targeted via their devices' for bypassing restrictions
- Starlink services were disabled weeks before elections, cutting satellite internet backup
- Pattern continues from 2016 and 2021 elections when Uganda imposed similar shutdowns and VPN blocks
- Access Now and #KeepItOn coalition documented real-time shutdown despite prior government commitments
- African Commission on Human and Peoples' Rights issued statement condemning the restrictions
- Demonstrates urgent need for censorship-resistant infrastructure in authoritarian contexts

## Deep Dive

## Context and Pattern

Uganda's 2026 election shutdown represents an escalation in the government's systematic approach to digital control during politically sensitive periods. This marks the third consecutive election cycle (2016, 2021, 2026) where authorities have imposed internet restrictions, despite international pressure and legal commitments. The shutdown affected over 45 million people and followed a predictable playbook: pre-election warnings, complete connectivity cuts, then partial restoration while maintaining platform-specific blocks.

## Technical and Surveillance Implications

The UCC's explicit threat that VPN users "could be targeted via their devices" represents a significant escalation in surveillance rhetoric. This suggests either existing deep packet inspection capabilities to detect VPN usage, or device-level monitoring infrastructure that could identify circumvention attempts. The simultaneous shutdown of terrestrial internet and Starlink satellite services demonstrates sophisticated coordination to eliminate backup connectivity routes that freedom tech tools increasingly rely on.

## Threat Model Evolution

This incident reveals how authoritarian governments are adapting their approaches to defeat circumvention tools. The combination of infrastructure-level shutdowns, platform-specific blocking, VPN detection threats, and satellite service disruption creates multiple failure points for traditional censorship resistance methods. The fact that over 100 VPNs were blocked in 2021, combined with new device targeting threats, suggests authorities are building comprehensive anti-circumvention capabilities.

## What Builders Should Consider

The Uganda pattern demonstrates why mesh networking solutions like Bitchat (recently warned against by the same UCC) become critical during complete connectivity blackouts. Traditional circumvention tools failed when facing coordinated infrastructure shutdowns, highlighting the need for solutions that can operate without internet connectivity entirely. The device targeting threats also underscore the importance of traffic analysis resistance and plausible deniability features in freedom tech design.

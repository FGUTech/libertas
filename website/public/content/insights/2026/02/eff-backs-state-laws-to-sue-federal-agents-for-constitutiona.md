# EFF Backs State Laws to Sue Federal Agents for Constitutional Violations

**TL;DR:** EFF supports California S.B. 747 and similar state laws enabling lawsuits against federal agents who violate constitutional rights, as Supreme Court has gutted federal protections while ICE/CBP violate filming rights.

## Key Points

- ICE/CBP agents are violating First Amendment right to record law enforcement, including before killing Alex Pretti and Renee Good in Minnesota
- Supreme Court has systematically weakened Bivens doctrine that allowed suing federal agents for constitutional violations since 1971
- No federal statute exists to sue federal officials (unlike 42 U.S.C. § 1983 for state/local officials from Reconstruction era)
- California S.B. 747 ('No Kings Act') would create state-level remedy to sue federal agents for constitutional violations
- EFF argues state laws don't violate Supremacy Clause because they enforce federal constitutional rights
- Legal accountability systems encourage agents to follow law while impunity encourages violations
- Model legislation from Protect Democracy called 'Universal Constitutional Remedies Act' spreading to other states

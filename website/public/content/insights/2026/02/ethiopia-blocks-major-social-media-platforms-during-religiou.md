# Ethiopia Blocks Major Social Media Platforms During Religious Protests

**TL;DR:** Ethiopia restricted Facebook, Telegram, TikTok and YouTube on state ISP Ethio Telecom during anti-government protests over Ethiopian Orthodox Church tensions, with NetBlocks confirming circumvention via VPN services.

## Key Points

- NetBlocks confirmed restriction of Facebook, Telegram, TikTok on Feb 9, 2023, YouTube added Feb 10
- Restrictions implemented on state internet provider Ethio Telecom amid religious protests
- Protests sparked by government attempt to split Ethiopian Orthodox Tewhado Church (EOTC)
- Users migrated to YouTube after initial platforms blocked, prompting additional restrictions
- NetBlocks measured disruption from 20 vantage points showing targeted platform blocking
- VPN services confirmed as effective circumvention method for these restrictions
- Pattern consistent with Ethiopia's history of social media blocks during protests
- Authorities typically implement restrictions without public announcement

## Deep Dive

## Context and Pattern

Ethiopia's February 2023 social media restrictions follow a well-established pattern of internet censorship during politically sensitive periods. The country has previously implemented similar measures during exam paper leaks and the Tigray conflict, indicating systematic use of platform blocking as a crisis response tool [NetBlocks]. The trigger—tensions over splitting the Ethiopian Orthodox Church—demonstrates how religious issues can rapidly escalate into broader anti-government sentiment requiring digital suppression.

## Technical Implementation

NetBlocks' technical analysis reveals sophisticated, targeted platform blocking rather than broad internet shutdowns. The restriction was implemented at the ISP level through Ethio Telecom, Ethiopia's state-controlled internet provider, allowing for precise platform targeting while maintaining general internet connectivity. The sequential blocking pattern—Facebook, Telegram, TikTok first, then YouTube after user migration—shows adaptive censorship responding to user behavior in real-time.

## Circumvention Reality

The explicit confirmation that VPN services successfully circumvent these restrictions highlights both the limitations of platform-level blocking and the continued effectiveness of basic circumvention tools. This creates a two-tier information environment where technically capable users maintain access while others face restrictions, potentially limiting the government's ability to fully control information flow during sensitive periods.

## Implications for Freedom Tech

This incident demonstrates the continued reliance on centralized platforms and infrastructure for protest coordination, creating clear chokepoints for government interference. The effectiveness of VPN circumvention suggests current government capabilities focus on platform-level blocking rather than deep packet inspection or VPN detection, indicating opportunities for circumvention tools to operate effectively in the Ethiopian context.

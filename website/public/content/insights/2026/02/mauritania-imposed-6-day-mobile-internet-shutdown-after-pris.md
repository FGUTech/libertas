# Mauritania Imposed 6-Day Mobile Internet Shutdown After Prison Break

**TL;DR:** Mauritania cut mobile internet nationwide for 6 days following a violent prison escape, affecting businesses and demonstrating how infrastructure-level shutdowns bypass VPN protections.

## Key Points

- Four high-risk prisoners escaped Nouakchott Civil Prison on March 5, 2023, killing two guards
- Government responded with nation-scale mobile internet shutdown lasting 6 days (March 6-12)
- Fixed-line internet remained available while mobile data was completely cut
- VPN services ineffective against this infrastructure-level disruption method
- Local businesses severely impacted, especially those relying on mobile payment apps
- NetBlocks confirmed shutdown using technical measurement data from Google Transparency
- Mauritania has history of election-related internet shutdowns (June 2019)
- Service restored after authorities reported fugitives killed or captured

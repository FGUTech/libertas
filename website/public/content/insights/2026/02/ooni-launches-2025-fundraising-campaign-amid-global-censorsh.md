# OONI Launches 2025 Fundraising Campaign Amid Global Censorship Surge

**TL;DR:** Internet freedom measurement organization OONI launches fundraising campaign as they document increasing censorship of social media, messaging apps, and news sites across 10+ countries in 2024.

## Key Points

- OONI (Open Observatory of Network Interference) launched end-of-year fundraising campaign
- Organization documented surge in internet censorship globally throughout 2024
- Social media blocks observed in Togo, Cameroon, and Tanzania
- Messaging apps (Telegram, WhatsApp) blocked in Kenya, Vietnam, Nepal, and Iran
- Independent news media websites blocked in India, Egypt, and Jordan
- Censorship often coincides with protests and elections, limiting critical communication
- OONI provides essential measurement tools for documenting network interference

## Deep Dive

## Critical Infrastructure for Documentation

OONI's fundraising campaign comes at a pivotal moment when internet censorship is escalating globally. The organization's measurement platform provides the technical infrastructure that activists, researchers, and journalists rely on to document and prove censorship events. Their data serves as crucial evidence for advocacy efforts and legal challenges against information restrictions.

## Pattern of Increasing Restrictions

The documented cases reveal a clear pattern: governments are increasingly targeting communication platforms during sensitive periods. The blocking of messaging apps like Telegram and WhatsApp directly impacts activists' ability to coordinate, while social media restrictions limit information flow during protests and elections. News media blocks in India, Egypt, and Jordan represent direct attacks on independent journalism.

## Technical and Strategic Implications

OONI's work provides the evidentiary foundation that freedom tech builders need to understand censorship techniques and develop effective circumvention tools. Their measurement data helps identify network-level blocking methods, DNS manipulation, and deep packet inspection deployment. For the broader ecosystem, OONI's financial sustainability directly impacts the community's ability to maintain visibility into global censorship trends.

## Funding Critical Infrastructure

The campaign highlights a persistent challenge in freedom tech: funding essential but non-commercial infrastructure. OONI's measurement platform generates no revenue but provides immense public value. Their financial needs reflect the broader ecosystem's dependence on sustained funding for digital rights organizations that provide foundational services.

# Cambodia Sentences Two Journalists to 14 Years for Border Conflict Photography

**TL;DR:** Cambodian court sentenced two journalists to 14 years for treason after photographing soldiers near disputed Thai border, part of broader press freedom crackdown targeting at least four journalists since July 2025.

## Key Points

- Phorn Sopheap (Battambang Post TV) and Pheap Pheara (TSP 68 TV) sentenced to 14 years for treason on February 20
- Journalists arrested in July 2025 for photo near Ta Krabei Temple showing alleged landmines with Cambodian soldiers
- Charged under article 445 for 'supplying foreign state with information prejudicial to national defense'
- Photo used by Thai media to allege Cambodia violated 1997 Mine Ban Treaty by laying new landmines
- At least two other journalists detained: Meas Sara (incitement) and Luot Sophal (incitement, demoralizing armed forces)
- Systematic targeting of press freedom since Thai-Cambodian border conflict began
- Demonstrates urgent need for secure communication tools and censorship-resistant publishing platforms
- Pattern shows how border disputes create pretext for broader media suppression

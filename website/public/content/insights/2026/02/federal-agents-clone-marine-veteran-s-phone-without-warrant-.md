# Federal Agents Clone Marine Veteran's Phone Without Warrant After Minneapolis Shooting

**TL;DR:** Federal ICE/Border Patrol agents detained Marine veteran Steven Saari for 6+ hours, forced biometric collection, and cloned his phone without a warrant after he observed the Alex Pretti shooting scene in Minneapolis.

## Key Points

- Steven Saari, Iraq/Afghanistan Marine veteran, observed federal shooting scene in Minneapolis
- Federal agents initially mistook him for undercover officer, then detained him for 6+ hours
- Agents forced biometric data collection including facial scans and DNA samples
- Phone was cloned without warrant, consent, or judicial authorization according to Saari
- Detention included denial of attorney access despite repeated requests
- Legal experts cite clear Fourth Amendment violations referencing Riley v. California (2014)
- ACLU notes narrow emergency exceptions don't apply once person and device are secured
- Released without charges into sub-zero weather with no transportation

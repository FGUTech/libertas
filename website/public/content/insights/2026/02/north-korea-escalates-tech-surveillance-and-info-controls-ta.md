# North Korea Escalates Tech Surveillance and Info Controls Targeting Youth

**TL;DR:** North Korea's 2026 Party Congress coincides with intensified crackdowns on tech-savvy youth accessing foreign media, with new laws criminalizing information access and public executions for sharing content.

## Key Points

- North Korea's Workers' Party Congress convenes amid escalating repression since 2021
- Government specifically targets 'tech-savvy young people' for surveillance and punishment
- Three new laws criminalize foreign media access, South Korean expressions, and 'non-socialist' cultural practices
- Punishments range from forced labor to execution - witness reports 22-year-old executed for sharing foreign media in 2022
- Kim Jong Un praised youth who sacrificed 'precious dreams' for state demands, including military deployment to Ukraine
- Crackdowns focus on information control through schools, workplaces, and party youth organizations
- UN reports worsening repression over past decade, with forced labor potentially constituting crimes against humanity
- Human Rights Watch calls for ending criminal penalties for information access and independent monitoring

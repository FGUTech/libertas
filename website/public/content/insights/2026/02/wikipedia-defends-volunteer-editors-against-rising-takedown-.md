# Wikipedia Defends Volunteer Editors Against Rising Takedown Demands

**TL;DR:** Wikimedia Foundation received 664 takedown requests in 2024 (up from 304 over two years a decade ago) but granted only 4, defending community-governed content creation through Section 230 protections.

## Key Points

- Takedown requests against Wikipedia content jumped from 304 over two years (decade ago) to 664 in 2024 alone
- Wikimedia Foundation granted only 4 of the 664 requests, defending volunteer editors through expanded legal team
- Section 230 enables community dispute resolution rather than centralized content moderation
- Without Section 230, potential litigation costs could bankrupt the platform (~$20k minimum per court filing)
- EU lacks Section 230 protections; Digital Services Act allows courts to order content removal via injunctions
- German court rejected Wikipedia sources as insufficient, demanding state media over trade publications
- EU litigation costs range from $30k per hearing to hundreds of thousands for complex cases
- Pseudonymous editing protection critical for sensitive topics like political history and religious content

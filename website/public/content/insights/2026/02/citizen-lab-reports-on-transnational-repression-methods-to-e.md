# Citizen Lab Reports on Transnational Repression Methods to EU Parliament

**TL;DR:** Citizen Lab researchers present findings on transnational repression tactics within EU to Parliament committee, recommending EU-wide TNR definition and central knowledge hub for counter-strategies.

## Key Points

- Citizen Lab presenting transnational repression (TNR) report to EU Parliament Foreign Affairs Committee on January 28
- Research team includes senior associate Emile Dirks and independent researchers examining TNR within EU borders
- Report analyzes perpetrators and methods of cross-border surveillance and harassment targeting activists
- Key recommendations include developing EU-wide TNR definition and creating central 'knowledge hub'
- Study addresses systematic methods used to intimidate civil society and digital rights advocates
- EU Parliament setting suggests potential policy responses to protect activists from state-sponsored harassment
- Citizen Lab's involvement brings technical expertise on digital surveillance methods to policy discussions

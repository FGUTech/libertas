# UN Rules Pakistani Human Rights Defender's Detention Violates International Law

**TL;DR:** UN Working Group found Pakistan's imprisonment of Pashtun human rights defender Idris Khattak unlawful, calling for immediate release after 5+ years detention for documenting enforced disappearances.

## Key Points

- UN Working Group on Arbitrary Detention ruled Idris Khattak's detention violates international law
- Khattak is Pashtun human rights defender known as 'Pakistan's expert on enforced disappearances'
- Abducted November 2019, disappeared for 7 months, then convicted by military court under Official Secrets Act
- WGAD found he was detained specifically for his human rights work and freedom of expression
- Due process violations included: 2 years pretrial detention, denied counsel, forced confession, secret evidence
- Military court conviction improper under international law for civilian human rights defender
- Pakistan's Freedom House score declined from 43/100 (2017) to 32/100 (2025)
- Case represents broader pattern of silencing dissent and targeting minority rights defenders

# Freedom House Documents Artists Imprisoned for Creative Dissent Across 7 Authoritarian States

**TL;DR:** Freedom House catalogs political imprisonment of artists in Russia, Belarus, Cuba, Azerbaijan, Egypt, China, and Venezuela, highlighting systematic state repression of creative expression that challenges power.

## Key Points

- Freedom House documents crackdowns on artists across 7 countries: Russia, Belarus, Cuba, Azerbaijan, Egypt, China, and Venezuela
- Luis Manuel Otero Alcántara, Cuban artist and San Isidro Movement cofounder, imprisoned since July 11, 2021 protests
- Sentenced to 5 years for 'contempt, public disorder, and insulting symbols of the homeland' after protesting Decree 348
- Maykel Osorbo Castillo Pérez, musician and co-writer of protest song 'Patria y Vida', arrested 121 times in 5 months
- Cuba's Decree 348 (2018) requires government pre-approval for even private artistic performances
- Artists targeted because creative work can 'expose, ridicule, and condemn in emotive and powerful ways'
- Pattern shows systematic use of state machinery to silence criticism through imprisonment of creative dissidents

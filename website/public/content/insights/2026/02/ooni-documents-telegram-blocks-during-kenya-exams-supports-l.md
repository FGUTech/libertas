# OONI Documents Telegram Blocks During Kenya Exams, Supports Legal Challenge

**TL;DR:** OONI provided technical evidence of Telegram blocking during Kenya's national exams in 2023 and 2024, which is being used in a High Court case challenging internet shutdowns.

## Key Points

- OONI documented systematic Telegram blocking during Kenya's KCSE national exams in November 2023 and November 2024
- Blocking targeted Telegram website, web interface, and app endpoints across multiple ISPs
- Technical measurements show coordinated censorship likely aimed at preventing exam cheating
- OONI's data is being used as evidence in High Court of Kenya petition filed in May 2025
- Case represents landmark public interest litigation challenging unlawful internet disruptions
- Demonstrates how technical measurement organizations can support legal resistance to censorship
- BAKE and 6 other organizations are challenging the shutdowns in court

## Deep Dive

## Context and Significance

This case represents a critical example of how technical documentation can enable legal challenges to government censorship. Kenya's practice of blocking communications platforms during national exams reflects a broader pattern of "exam season" internet restrictions seen across multiple African countries. The High Court petition marks a significant escalation in legal resistance to these practices.

## Technical Documentation as Legal Evidence

OONI's measurements provide forensic-quality evidence of network interference, documenting specific blocking methods across multiple ISPs and timeframes. The organization's rigorous methodology and established credibility make their data particularly valuable in legal proceedings. This case demonstrates how freedom tech organizations can serve dual roles - both documenting censorship and enabling legal accountability.

## Implications for Builders

The success of this legal strategy could establish important precedents for challenging internet shutdowns across the region. Technical measurement tools like OONI Probe become critical infrastructure for accountability when combined with strategic litigation. The case also highlights the importance of continuous monitoring during predictable censorship events like exam periods, elections, or protests.

# Coalition Demands California Revoke Federal ALPR Surveillance Permits on Border Highways

**TL;DR:** EFF-led coalition demands California revoke permits allowing CBP and DEA to deploy automated license plate readers disguised as traffic barrels on border highways, citing mass surveillance risks.

## Key Points

- Coalition of 25+ orgs led by EFF and Imperial Valley Equity & Justice sent letter to Gov. Newsom and Caltrans
- Federal agencies CBP and DEA have permits to deploy ALPRs along California border highways
- Surveillance devices often disguised as traffic barrels to avoid detection
- Coalition argues ALPRs put residents and migrants at risk of harassment, detention, and deportation
- Letter references return of USBP Chief Greg Bovino who led 'brutal campaigns' in other cities
- Demands immediate revocation of permits and removal of existing surveillance infrastructure
- Broad coalition includes nurses, teachers, ACLU, privacy groups, and immigrant rights organizations

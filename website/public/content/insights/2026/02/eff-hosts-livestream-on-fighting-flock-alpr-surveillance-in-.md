# EFF Hosts Livestream on Fighting Flock ALPR Surveillance in Cities

**TL;DR:** EFF is hosting a livestream Feb 19 on fighting Flock automated license plate readers, featuring case studies of successful campaigns that blocked surveillance deployments across US cities.

## Key Points

- EFF hosting 'Get the Flock Out of Our City' livestream February 19, 12-1PM Pacific
- Event focuses on fighting Flock Automated License Plate Reader (ALPR) surveillance systems
- Speakers include EFF staff, Fight for the Future, and Rural Privacy Coalition representatives
- Will cover legal implications of ALPR data collection and successful resistance campaigns
- Cities from Austin to Cambridge to Texas towns have rejected Flock contracts
- Evidence shows Flock systems used to surveil abortion seekers, protesters, and targeted communities
- Free event includes live Q&A and will be recorded for later viewing
- Demonstrates growing organized resistance to municipal surveillance infrastructure

## Deep Dive

## Context and Background

Flock Safety's automated license plate reader systems represent a significant expansion of municipal surveillance infrastructure across the United States. These systems create detailed databases of vehicle movements that can be used to track individuals' daily activities, locations, and associations. The EFF has documented cases where this technology has been misused to monitor constitutionally protected activities including reproductive healthcare access and First Amendment activities.

## Strategic Importance for Freedom Tech

This livestream represents more than just advocacy—it's intelligence gathering and strategy sharing for the broader freedom tech community. The event will provide concrete case studies of successful resistance campaigns, legal frameworks that can be replicated, and tactical approaches that have proven effective against surveillance infrastructure deployment. The participation of Fight for the Future and Rural Privacy Coalition indicates this is part of a coordinated, multi-organizational effort to build systematic resistance to surveillance expansion.

## What Builders Should Consider

The success of anti-Flock campaigns demonstrates that surveillance infrastructure deployment is not inevitable and can be disrupted through organized community action. For freedom tech builders, this suggests opportunities for tools that support grassroots organizing, public records research, and community surveillance mapping. The legal and policy victories being discussed could also inform technical design decisions for privacy-preserving alternatives to traditional municipal safety systems.

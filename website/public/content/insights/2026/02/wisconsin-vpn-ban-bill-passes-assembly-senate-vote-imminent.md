# Wisconsin VPN Ban Bill Passes Assembly, Senate Vote Imminent

**TL;DR:** Wisconsin's S.B. 130/A.B. 105 effectively bans VPNs and mandates invasive age verification for websites, passed State Assembly and faces Senate vote tomorrow.

## Key Points

- Wisconsin S.B. 130/A.B. 105 passed State Assembly, Senate vote scheduled for tomorrow
- Bill requires websites to block VPN users and implement invasive age verification
- Targets websites with content lawmakers deem 'sexual' with broad, vague definitions
- EFF sent letter to entire Wisconsin Legislature urging rejection of the bill
- Technical enforcement is unworkable - sites can't reliably determine VPN user location
- Creates privacy nightmare requiring government IDs, financial data, biometric identifiers
- Broadens 'harmful to minors' definition beyond court-recognized categories
- Forces websites to choose: over-block VPNs, block all Wisconsin users, or implement nationwide restrictions

## Deep Dive

## Legislative Context

Wisconsin's S.B. 130/A.B. 105 represents one of the most aggressive state-level attacks on VPN usage in the United States. The bill has already passed the State Assembly and is scheduled for a Senate vote, making this an imminent threat to digital rights infrastructure. The legislation combines two dangerous precedents: effective VPN bans and expansive age verification mandates that function as surveillance systems.

## Technical Implementation Problems

The bill creates technically unworkable mandates for website operators. Websites cannot reliably determine whether a VPN user is actually located in Wisconsin versus another state or country. This forces compliance through over-broad blocking strategies that will impact users far beyond Wisconsin's borders. Sites face three bad options: aggressively block commercial VPN IP ranges (affecting legitimate users nationwide), geofence all Wisconsin traffic, or implement nationwide age verification to avoid liability exposure.

## Surveillance and Censorship Implications

The bill expands the definition of content "harmful to minors" far beyond traditionally regulated explicit adult material to include any content that "describes sex or depicts human anatomy." Combined with mandatory collection of government IDs, financial information, and biometric identifiers, this creates a comprehensive surveillance apparatus. The vague definitions invite over-censorship as websites err on the side of caution to avoid prosecution.

## What This Means for the Ecosystem

If enacted, this could establish a dangerous precedent for other states to follow. The combination of VPN blocking and mandatory identity verification represents a significant escalation in state-level internet regulation. Builders should monitor whether other states introduce similar legislation and consider how to maintain service availability for users in increasingly restrictive jurisdictions.

# Cameroon Police Seize Journalists' Equipment, Threaten Sources Over US Deportation Story

**TL;DR:** Cameroonian police detained 4 journalists for 5 hours, seized phones/laptops, and threatened to 'crush' them while investigating secret US migrant deportations, raising source protection concerns.

## Key Points

- Four journalists detained by Cameroon judicial police while reporting on secret US deportations
- Police seized cellphones, laptop, and other equipment - devices not yet returned
- AP reporters Nalova Akua, Angel Ngwe, Arnold Ndal plus freelancer Randy Joe Sa'ah affected
- Journalists threatened they would be 'crushed' and would 'disappear without a trace'
- Police investigating 'criminal trespass' charges for reporting at deportation center
- Lawyer concerned police will circumvent device passwords and compromise anonymous sources
- Story covered secret deportation flights of African migrants under Trump administration
- Demonstrates operational security risks for journalists in hostile environments

# OONI Launches Enhanced Censorship Detection Platform, Seeks Community Feedback

**TL;DR:** OONI has released major platform upgrades including new censorship findings tools and improved methodologies, and is requesting community feedback to evaluate impact.

## Key Points

- OONI released new Explorer Censorship Findings platform for better censorship event monitoring
- Added thematic censorship pages for targeted analysis of specific censorship patterns
- Published new research reports based on OONI measurement data
- Implemented improved throttling detection methodology for more accurate measurements
- Deployed OONI Pipeline v5 with enhanced data analysis capabilities
- Seeking community feedback to evaluate impact and guide future improvements
- Enhancements aim to better support internet freedom community responses to censorship

## Deep Dive

## Context and Significance

OONI (Open Observatory of Network Interference) serves as one of the most critical infrastructure pieces for global internet freedom monitoring. These platform enhancements represent a significant evolution in how censorship data is collected, analyzed, and presented to researchers and activists. The timing is particularly relevant given increasing global internet restrictions and the need for more sophisticated detection capabilities.

## Technical Improvements

The new throttling methodology addresses a key limitation in previous censorship detection - the ability to distinguish between intentional throttling and network congestion. OONI Pipeline v5 represents a complete data processing overhaul, likely improving both speed and accuracy of censorship measurements. The thematic censorship pages suggest more granular analysis capabilities, allowing researchers to track specific types of content blocking or regional patterns.

## Strategic Implications

By actively seeking community feedback, OONI is positioning these tools to better serve real-world use cases. For builders, this represents an opportunity to influence the development of critical censorship detection infrastructure. The enhanced research reports could provide better evidence for policy advocacy and technical countermeasure development.

## What This Means for the Ecosystem

These improvements strengthen the foundation that many other freedom tech projects depend on for understanding the censorship landscape. More accurate and accessible censorship data enables better-informed development of circumvention tools and more effective activist responses to internet shutdowns.

# FBI Counterterrorism Unit Targets Climate Activists Under Trump's NSPM-7 Directive

**TL;DR:** FBI counterterrorism agents visited former Extinction Rebellion member's home for questioning, marking escalation in Trump administration's targeting of activist groups as domestic terrorists under NSPM-7.

## Key Points

- FBI Joint Terrorism Task Force agents questioned former Extinction Rebellion NYC member at his home
- Agents traveled hundreds of miles and made multiple contact attempts over weeks
- Investigation linked to Trump's NSPM-7 directive targeting progressive groups for 'anti-fascism' beliefs
- Similar visits occurred to climate activists in Boston area in March 2024
- Internal FBI report shows active NSPM-7 investigations in 27 locations including New York
- Extinction Rebellion NYC conducts only public, non-violent direct actions typically charged as misdemeanors
- Assistant AG Harmeet Dhillon threatened investigation after group protested Rep. Suozzi's ICE funding vote
- Pattern suggests systematic federal surveillance of climate and anti-ICE activists nationwide

## Deep Dive

## Context and Escalation

This FBI investigation represents a significant escalation in federal surveillance of climate activists under the Trump administration's broader crackdown on progressive movements. The targeting of Extinction Rebellion NYC—a group that conducts exclusively public, non-violent civil disobedience—through counterterrorism resources demonstrates how NSPM-7's vague language is being operationalized to criminalize protected speech and protest activities. The fact that agents traveled hundreds of miles and made repeated contact attempts suggests substantial investigative resources are being devoted to what the group's attorney characterizes as misdemeanor-level activism.

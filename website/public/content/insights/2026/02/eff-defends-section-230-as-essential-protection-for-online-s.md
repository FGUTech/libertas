# EFF Defends Section 230 as Essential Protection for Online Speech and Freedom Tech

**TL;DR:** EFF argues Section 230's immunity protections are critical for free expression online, warning that weakening them would force platforms to pre-censor content and chill speech across the internet.

## Key Points

- EFF published op-ed defending Section 230 on its 30th anniversary
- Section 230 prevents civil suits against online services based on user-generated content
- Law protects both platforms and users who act as intermediaries (forwarding emails, retweeting)
- Alternatives like duty-of-care standards would create chilling effects and mass takedowns
- Notice-and-takedown systems invite abuse and lead to over-censorship
- AI cannot make legal determinations about content at scale
- Without immunity, platforms would need to review all content before publication
- Benefits small platforms and users, not just 'big tech' companies
- Essential for email, messaging, blogging, and social media to function

# 17 Orgs Fight FTC's Retaliatory Investigation Into Media Matters

**TL;DR:** Coalition led by The Intercept filed amicus brief to stop FTC's allegedly retaliatory investigation into Media Matters after they published critical Trump administration coverage.

## Key Points

- 17 nonprofits filed amicus brief urging D.C. Circuit Court to halt FTC investigation into Media Matters
- Investigation allegedly launched after Media Matters published critical reporting on Trump allies
- Brief describes 'pretextual investigations' as troublingly common government intimidation tactic
- Coalition includes EFF, Freedom of the Press Foundation, Center for Investigative Reporting
- Pattern cited across agencies: DOJ, FCC using similar coercive investigation tactics
- Brief seeks to uphold preliminary injunction against the investigation
- Argues such investigations chill coverage adverse to those in power
- Mentions related press freedom attacks: journalist arrests in Minnesota, seizure of communications in D.C.

# Pakistani Academic Sentenced to Death for Facebook Posts Spends 13 Years in Solitary

**TL;DR:** Junaid Hafeez has been imprisoned for 13 years, mostly in solitary confinement, after being sentenced to death for alleged blasphemous Facebook posts, highlighting Pakistan's systematic suppression of online speech.

## Key Points

- Junaid Hafeez arrested in 2013 for alleged blasphemous Facebook comments, sentenced to death in 2019
- Held in solitary confinement since June 2014, appeal still pending after 13 years
- His defense lawyer Rashid Rehman was assassinated in 2014 after receiving threats
- Pakistan's blasphemy laws carry mandatory death sentences and increasingly target social media posts
- Laws routinely used against minorities and for personal vendettas with little evidence required
- Government announced procedural safeguards in October 2025 but implementation unclear
- Case demonstrates extreme risks of unprotected social media usage in authoritarian contexts

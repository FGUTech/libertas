# CBP Signs Deal with Clearview AI for Border Surveillance Face Recognition

**TL;DR:** US Border Patrol intelligence units will deploy Clearview AI's facial recognition system built on billions of scraped internet images for 'tactical targeting' operations.

## Key Points

- US Customs and Border Protection (CBP) signed contract with Clearview AI for facial recognition access
- System built on billions of images scraped from social media and internet without consent
- Border Patrol intelligence units will use tool for 'tactical targeting' operations
- Expands government surveillance capabilities at borders and potentially inland
- Threatens anonymity of travelers, activists, immigrants, and anyone with online presence
- Clearview AI previously faced lawsuits and bans over privacy violations
- Deal represents normalization of mass biometric surveillance infrastructure

## Deep Dive

## Surveillance Infrastructure Expansion

This CBP-Clearview AI partnership represents a significant escalation in government biometric surveillance capabilities. The system's foundation on billions of scraped images means virtually anyone with an online presence could be subject to identification at borders or during 'tactical targeting' operations. The term 'tactical targeting' suggests proactive identification of individuals rather than reactive verification.

## Technical and Privacy Implications

Clearview AI's database includes images scraped from social media platforms, news sites, and other online sources without user consent. This creates a comprehensive biometric database that connects online identities to physical presence. For privacy-conscious individuals, activists, and journalists, this eliminates practical anonymity when crossing borders or operating in CBP jurisdiction.

## Threat Model Evolution

The deployment signals a shift toward preemptive identification rather than traditional document-based verification. This affects threat models for anyone requiring operational security, as past online activity becomes permanently linked to biometric identity. The 'intelligence units' designation suggests this extends beyond routine border processing into investigative and enforcement operations.

## Builder Considerations

This development highlights the critical need for privacy-preserving identity solutions and anti-surveillance tools. The mass deployment of facial recognition based on scraped data demonstrates how personal information shared online can be weaponized for surveillance. Developers should consider how their platforms might be scraped and incorporated into similar systems.

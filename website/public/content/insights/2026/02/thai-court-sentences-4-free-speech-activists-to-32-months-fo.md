# Thai Court Sentences 4 Free Speech Activists to 32 Months for Royal Criticism

**TL;DR:** Thai activists including Pimsiri Petchnamrob received 32-month prison sentences for criticizing the monarchy under lese majeste laws, highlighting severe censorship repression in Thailand.

## Key Points

- Bangkok Criminal Court sentenced 4 democracy activists to 32 months prison for lese majeste violations
- Pimsiri Petchnamrob convicted for 2020 speech criticizing monarchy's unchecked power and UN statement on lese majeste law
- Arnon Nampa, Somyot Pruksakaseksuk, and Promsorn Viradhammajari also sentenced for criticizing King Maha Vajiralongkorn
- Sentences reduced from 4 years due to 'useful testimony'; Pimsiri granted $4,800 bail pending appeal
- Thailand has prosecuted 1,987 people for free expression since 2014 coup, with 285 facing lese majeste charges
- Lese majeste law carries 3-15 years prison per offense under Penal Code section 112
- UN human rights experts called for charges to be dropped; Working Group on Arbitrary Detention demanded Arnon's release

# Pakistan Implements Total Internet Shutdowns During Political Arrests

**TL;DR:** Pakistan disrupted social media platforms and imposed total mobile internet shutdowns following former PM Imran Khan's arrest, with NetBlocks confirming multi-day blackouts affecting fundamental rights.

## Key Points

- NetBlocks confirmed disruption of Twitter, Facebook, and YouTube across multiple Pakistani ISPs on May 9, 2023
- Total internet shutdowns implemented on mobile networks in some regions during former PM Imran Khan's arrest
- Social media restrictions could be circumvented with VPNs, but mobile broadband shutdowns created total blackouts
- Disruptions continued for multiple days, intensifying in some areas with Instagram also becoming unreachable
- Hundreds of protesters reportedly detained during the information blackout period
- NetBlocks used 120 measurements from 60 vantage points across Pakistan to document the censorship
- Shutdowns demonstrate government willingness to use internet disruption as tool for political control

## Deep Dive

## Context and Scope

Pakistan's response to Imran Khan's arrest represents a textbook case of coordinated internet censorship during political unrest. NetBlocks' technical measurements from 60 vantage points across Pakistan documented a multi-layered approach: selective social media blocking on fixed-line connections that could be bypassed with VPNs, combined with total mobile internet shutdowns that created complete information blackouts in affected regions [NetBlocks]. This dual approach maximizes censorship effectiveness while providing plausible deniability.

## Technical Analysis

The government's strategy reveals sophisticated understanding of circumvention tools. By maintaining partial fixed-line connectivity while shutting down mobile networks, authorities acknowledged that technically sophisticated users could still access blocked platforms via VPN, but ensured that the broader population—primarily dependent on mobile internet—remained completely disconnected. The gradual expansion to include Instagram and the multi-day duration suggest authorities were responding dynamically to protest coordination attempts.

## Implications for Freedom Tech

This incident highlights critical gaps in censorship resistance infrastructure. While VPNs provided some circumvention capability for social media blocks, they offered no protection against total network shutdowns. The effectiveness of mobile shutdowns in limiting protest coordination demonstrates the vulnerability of freedom movements that depend on traditional internet infrastructure, making this a strong use case for mesh networking, satellite communications, and other infrastructure-independent communication tools.

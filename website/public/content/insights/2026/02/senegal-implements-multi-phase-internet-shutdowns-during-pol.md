# Senegal Implements Multi-Phase Internet Shutdowns During Political Protests

**TL;DR:** NetBlocks documented systematic internet censorship in Senegal during 2023 protests, including social media blocks, mobile data curfews, and platform-specific restrictions targeting opposition to Ousmane Sonko's sentencing.

## Key Points

- Social media platforms (Facebook, Twitter, WhatsApp, Instagram, YouTube, Telegram) restricted starting June 1, 2023
- Mobile data completely suspended June 4-7 in daily curfew-style pattern (1pm-2am local time)
- Restrictions triggered by protests over opposition leader Ousmane Sonko's sentencing and arrest
- Second wave of mobile data curfews began July 31 after Sonko's arrest, lasting at least 6 days
- TikTok specifically blocked August 2 due to alleged 'hateful and subversive messages'
- Shutdowns affected major providers Orange (Sonatel) and Free (Tigo) based on 4000 measurements
- VPNs could circumvent social media blocks but not mobile data shutdowns
- Government also faced activist cyberattacks that took down state websites on ADIE network

## Deep Dive

## Technical Implementation Pattern

Senegal's 2023 censorship campaign demonstrates a sophisticated escalation model for information control. Authorities began with platform-specific restrictions that could be circumvented via VPNs, then escalated to complete mobile data shutdowns that left users fully offline. The curfew-style implementation (daily 17-hour blackouts) suggests authorities balanced suppressing coordination while maintaining economic functionality.

## Measurement and Documentation

NetBlocks' technical analysis provides crucial intelligence on real-world censorship implementation. Their 4000 measurements across 120 vantage points confirm restrictions affected major providers Orange (Sonatel) and Free (Tigo). This level of documentation is essential for understanding how governments deploy censorship infrastructure and which technical approaches prove most effective at control.

## Implications for Freedom Tech

The progression from social media blocks to complete mobile shutdowns illustrates why mesh networking and satellite connectivity remain critical. Traditional circumvention tools like VPNs proved inadequate against infrastructure-level shutdowns. The government's response to activist cyberattacks (targeting state websites) also demonstrates how digital resistance can provoke broader crackdowns. Freedom tech builders should note that successful deployment often triggers escalated government responses.

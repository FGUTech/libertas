# NetBlocks Documents Systematic Internet Shutdowns During School Exams Across MENA

**TL;DR:** NetBlocks documented coordinated internet shutdowns and platform restrictions across Kenya, Iraq, Syria, and Algeria during 2023 school exams, revealing systematic government censorship tactics.

## Key Points

- Kenya selectively blocked Telegram during morning exam hours in November 2023, targeting exam paper leak channels
- Iraq implemented 4-hour daily internet shutdowns (4-8 AM) during June and August exam periods, excluding Kurdistan region
- Syria conducted 4-hour morning internet blackouts in May, June, and August coinciding with exam schedules
- Algeria throttled internet connectivity twice daily matching morning/afternoon exam times during June Baccalaureate exams
- Kurdistan region operated independently with 2-hour shutdowns (6-8 AM) during high school exams
- Kenya authorities arrested 6 Telegram channel administrators running exam leak groups with 18,000+ members
- Shutdowns demonstrate government capability for rapid, coordinated digital infrastructure control
- Pattern reveals systematic use of internet disruption as policy tool across multiple authoritarian contexts

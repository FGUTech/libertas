# Bruce Schneier: AI Arms Races Threaten Democratic Participation and Concentrate Power in Big Tech

**TL;DR:** Security expert Bruce Schneier warns that AI is creating adversarial arms races across domains like government-citizen interactions, benefiting Big Tech monopolies while potentially undermining authentic democratic participation.

## Key Points

- Schneier identifies AI-driven arms races across multiple domains: academia, legal systems, open source development, and democratic processes
- Government-citizen interactions increasingly involve AI on both sides - constituents using AI to generate comments, agencies using AI to process them
- 2017 FCC comment fraud exemplifies early version: broadband providers and activists both used bots to flood regulatory feedback
- One-fifth of Consumer Financial Protection Bureau submissions reportedly AI-generated by 2025
- US Congress staff using AI for constituent correspondence; politicians automating fundraising and outreach
- Primary beneficiaries are Big Tech corporations extracting trillions from AI infrastructure and model development
- Schneier advocates dual strategy: use AI for community advantage while resisting corporate power concentration
- Suggests antitrust regulation, human rights protections, and public AI alternatives as resistance tools

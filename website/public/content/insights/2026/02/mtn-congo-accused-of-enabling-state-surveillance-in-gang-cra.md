# MTN Congo Accused of Enabling State Surveillance in Gang Crackdown

**TL;DR:** Access Now alleges MTN Congo sent mass SMS bounty messages for security forces targeting suspected gang members, potentially violating human rights commitments and data protection laws.

## Key Points

- Congo's Presidential Security (DGSP) conducting crackdown on 'Kuluna' gang members since September 2024, reportedly with extrajudicial killings
- MTN Congo allegedly sent mass SMS messages offering rewards up to $1,760 USD for information on suspected gang members
- Access Now claims this violates MTN's UN Global Compact commitments and Global Network Initiative membership obligations
- MTN Congo has 3.3M subscribers (over half the market), making mass surveillance capability significant
- Alleged violations of Congo's 2019 data protection law requiring impact assessments for high-risk processing
- DGSP operating outside legal mandate - tasked with presidential security, not public order maintenance
- Human rights groups CAD and OCDH documented extrajudicial killings during the operation
- Access Now demanding independent investigation into MTN's data sharing practices with authorities

# Angola's Parliament Passes Restrictive NGO Bill Threatening Civil Society Operations

**TL;DR:** Angola's parliament approved legislation granting sweeping government control over NGOs, including mandatory authorization, extensive monitoring, financial restrictions, and administrative suspension powers that undermine civil society independence.

## Key Points

- Angola's parliament passed restrictive NGO regulation bill on January 22, 2026 with 106 votes in favor
- Bill transforms freedom of association from right to government-granted privilege requiring administrative authorization
- Authorities gain power to monitor activities/finances, determine project locations, and assess leadership 'suitability'
- Administrative suspension allowed up to 120 days based on vague 'strong indications' of unlawful conduct
- Financial controls mandate all funds through domestic banks and ban international capital transfers
- Vague terminology like 'immoral' and 'non-compliant' grants wide discretionary power to authorities
- UNITA opposition party voted against, criticizing shift of control from courts to administrative bodies
- Local civil society groups warn law is unconstitutional and will further shrink civic space
- Parliament also approved anti-misinformation bill, signaling broader crackdown on civic freedoms

# Proton Guide: Technical Tools to Resist AI Training Data Scraping

**TL;DR:** Privacy company Proton outlines practical methods for creators to protect their work from AI training scraping, including pixel-level cloaking tools, data poisoning techniques, and GDPR opt-outs.

## Key Points

- Major AI companies like OpenAI and Meta scrape public internet content without creator consent for model training
- Meta admits to scraping all public Facebook/Instagram posts back to 2007 for AI training
- Technical protection tools available: Glaze (pixel cloaking) and Nightshade (data poisoning)
- Glaze makes subtle pixel changes that confuse AI models while remaining invisible to humans
- Nightshade introduces poison data causing AI to mislearn associations (cars as cats, etc.)
- Have I Been Trained website lets creators check if work appears in known AI datasets
- EU residents can leverage GDPR to demand exclusion from AI training datasets
- Research shows current protections may weaken as AI systems evolve
- Double standard: AI companies claim fair use for scraping but treat outputs as proprietary

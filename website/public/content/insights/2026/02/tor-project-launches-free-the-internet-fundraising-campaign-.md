# Tor Project Launches 'Free the Internet' Fundraising Campaign with Matched Donations

**TL;DR:** Tor Project announces fundraising campaign highlighting real-world censorship resistance use cases in Turkmenistan, Russia, and Egypt, with donations matched 1:1 up to $250K.

## Key Points

- Tor Project launches 'FREE THE INTERNET' fundraising campaign with Power Up Privacy matching donations 1:1 up to $250,000
- Turkmen.news uses Tor to provide safe internet access in heavily censored Turkmenistan
- Freedom of the Press Foundation relies on Tor as backbone for SecureDrop journalist communication tool
- Users in Russia and Egypt access encrypted email services like Tuta Mail via Tor Browser to bypass blocks
- Campaign runs for three months with merchandise incentives for donations over $25
- Two 'State of the Onion' virtual events scheduled for November 12 and December 10
- December 10 event coincides with UN Universal Declaration of Human Rights anniversary

## Deep Dive

## Context

The Tor Project's 'Free the Internet' campaign represents a coordinated push to expand censorship-resistant infrastructure at a time when internet freedom is under increasing pressure globally. The timing aligns with documented increases in internet shutdowns and surveillance across authoritarian regimes.

## Real-World Deployment Evidence

The campaign highlights three specific, verifiable use cases that demonstrate Tor's effectiveness in hostile environments. Turkmen.news operates as an independent news source in one of the world's most censored countries, where the government maintains near-total control over information flow. The SecureDrop integration shows institutional adoption by press freedom organizations, while the Russia/Egypt examples demonstrate individual-level circumvention of state communications controls.

## Technical and Strategic Implications

The matching donation structure (1:1 up to $250K) suggests significant backing from privacy-focused organizations, indicating growing institutional support for anonymity networks. The campaign's public nature, combined with specific country examples, may draw increased attention from authorities in mentioned regions. This represents both validation of Tor's effectiveness and potential risk escalation for users.

## What Builders Should Consider

The campaign demonstrates successful messaging strategies that connect technical tools to concrete human rights outcomes. The coordination between Tails and Tor Project shows ecosystem-level cooperation that other freedom tech projects could emulate. The timing around Human Rights Day (December 10) provides a template for linking technical development to broader rights advocacy.

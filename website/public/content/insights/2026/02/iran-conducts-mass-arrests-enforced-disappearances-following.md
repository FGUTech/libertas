# Iran Conducts Mass Arrests, Enforced Disappearances Following January 2026 Massacre

**TL;DR:** Iran arrested tens of thousands following January 8-9 massacres, conducting mass enforced disappearances, torture, and death sentences in coordinated crackdown to suppress dissent.

## Key Points

- Tens of thousands arrested since December 28, 2025 following countrywide protests and January 8-9, 2026 massacres
- IRGC Intelligence Organization summoned at least 11,000 people as of January 26, with 8,843 indictments issued by February 17
- Authorities conducting enforced disappearances by refusing to disclose detainees' whereabouts or fate
- Systematic torture documented including beatings, sexual violence, food deprivation, and psychological torture
- Death sentences issued including 19-year-old Saleh Mohammadi sentenced to public execution after forced confession under torture
- Heavy military presence and martial law-like restrictions imposed across multiple cities
- Over 2,800 named detainees documented by activist networks, with state broadcasting forced confessions including children
- Pattern mirrors 2022 crackdown tactics that resulted in arbitrary executions

## Deep Dive

## Context and Scale

This represents one of the most severe state crackdowns in recent Iranian history, with Human Rights Watch documenting systematic violations across 13 provinces following mass killings of protesters. The coordination between FARAJA police, IRGC, Ministry of Intelligence, and judicial officials demonstrates a state-level strategy to terrorize the population into submission. The scale—tens of thousands arrested—exceeds previous protest crackdowns and indicates authorities view this as an existential threat.

## Implications for Freedom Tech

This crackdown illustrates precisely the conditions where freedom tech becomes essential for survival. The systematic communication restrictions, enforced disappearances, and information blackouts create an environment where secure communications tools, anonymous networking, and censorship-resistant platforms become literally life-or-death infrastructure. The state's focus on extracting forced confessions and broadcasting them suggests authorities are particularly concerned about protest coordination mechanisms.

## Threat Model Considerations

The involvement of multiple intelligence agencies and systematic denial of legal access creates an extreme threat environment. Any digital communications or organizing tools must assume state-level adversaries with signals intelligence capabilities. The pattern of enforced disappearances means that operational security failures don't just risk arrest—they risk complete erasure from official records.

## What Builders Should Consider

This situation validates the need for tools that can operate under complete internet shutdowns, provide deniable encryption, and enable anonymous coordination. The broadcasting of forced confessions highlights the importance of forward secrecy and plausible deniability features. Emergency dead-man switches and automated evidence preservation become critical when users face torture and execution.

# Iraq Blocks Telegram Nationwide, Kurdistan Region Remains Online

**TL;DR:** Iraq restricted Telegram across most of the country citing national security and leaked personal data concerns, while the autonomous Kurdistan region maintains access.

## Key Points

- Iraq's Ministry of Communications ordered Telegram suspension on August 6, 2023 over national security and personal data concerns
- NetBlocks confirmed restriction affects major ISPs Zain and Earthlink but not Kurdish operators Kurdistan Net and Korek Telecom
- Block excludes autonomous Iraqi Kurdistan region, creating geographic disparity in access
- Restriction targets both Telegram frontends and backends, indicating comprehensive technical implementation
- VPN services can circumvent the censorship measures according to NetBlocks analysis
- Follows pattern of Iraq using internet restrictions during political crises and exam periods
- Telegram's popularity with activists and relatively unregulated status makes it frequent government target

## Deep Dive

## Context and Pattern

Iraq's Telegram restriction follows a well-established pattern of the country using internet controls as a governance tool. The government has previously implemented extended internet shutdowns to prevent exam cheating and restrict communications during political protests, often obscuring casualty reporting and human rights documentation. This selective enforcement—maintaining access in Kurdistan while blocking elsewhere—demonstrates sophisticated technical capabilities and political considerations.

## Technical Implementation

NetBlocks' real-time metrics reveal a comprehensive block targeting both Telegram's frontend interfaces and backend infrastructure on major ISPs Zain and Earthlink. The geographic selectivity suggests implementation at the ISP level rather than national internet infrastructure, allowing Kurdish operators Kurdistan Net and Korek Telecom to maintain service. This approach indicates coordination between government authorities and specific network providers.

## Implications for Activists and Builders

The restriction highlights Telegram's continued importance as activist infrastructure despite known security limitations. The government's explicit citation of "leaked personal data" as justification may indicate successful use of the platform for transparency efforts that authorities wanted to contain. The geographic disparity creates operational security challenges for Iraqi activists who may not realize their communications exposure varies by location and network provider.

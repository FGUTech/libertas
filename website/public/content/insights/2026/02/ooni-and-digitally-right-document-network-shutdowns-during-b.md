# OONI and Digitally Right Document Network Shutdowns During Bangladesh Protests

**TL;DR:** OONI collaborated with local digital rights group Digitally Right to document internet shutdowns and censorship during recent Bangladesh protests, providing crucial evidence of network interference.

## Key Points

- Open Observatory of Network Interference (OONI) partnered with Bangladesh-based Digitally Right
- Study documented network interference and shutdowns during Bangladesh protests
- Research involved Network Measurement Fellows tracking disruptions across Bangladesh
- Key contributors include Tohidul Islam Raso (Digitally Right) and Maria Xynou (OONI)
- Journalist Suhadha Afrin from Prothom Alo contributed to research and documentation
- Study combines technical measurement data with on-ground reporting
- Operators and service providers provided interviews and information
- Report provides evidence base for documenting government censorship tactics

## Deep Dive

## Context and Significance

This collaboration represents a crucial model for documenting internet shutdowns in real-time during periods of political unrest. OONI's technical measurement capabilities combined with Digitally Right's local presence in Bangladesh created a comprehensive documentation effort that captures both the technical aspects of network interference and the human impact on activists and protesters.

## Methodological Approach

The study leverages OONI's established network measurement tools while incorporating local knowledge through Digitally Right's Network Measurement Fellows distributed across Bangladesh. This distributed approach is particularly valuable for documenting selective or regional shutdowns that might be missed by centralized monitoring. The inclusion of journalist perspectives from Prothom Alo adds crucial context about how shutdowns affected information flow during critical political moments.

## Implications for Freedom Tech

This type of collaborative documentation is essential for building evidence of government censorship tactics and their impact on digital rights. The methodology demonstrates how international technical organizations can work with local digital rights groups to create comprehensive records of network interference. For builders, this highlights the importance of measurement infrastructure that can operate during disruptions and the value of distributed monitoring networks.

## Strategic Value

The report will likely serve as evidence for policy advocacy and legal challenges to internet shutdowns. It also provides valuable intelligence on government censorship capabilities and tactics that can inform the development of circumvention tools and resistance strategies.

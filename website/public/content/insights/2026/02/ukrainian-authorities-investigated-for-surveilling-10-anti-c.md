# Ukrainian Authorities Investigated for Surveilling 10 Anti-Corruption Journalists

**TL;DR:** Ukrainian police opened criminal investigation after NABU uncovered alleged surveillance of 10 journalists covering corruption, with their personal data found on seized devices from a criminal organization involving government officials.

## Key Points

- National Anti-Corruption Bureau of Ukraine (NABU) discovered personal information of 10 investigative journalists on seized digital devices during 15-month 'Operation Midas'
- Criminal organization allegedly included government officials and businessmen who embezzled energy sector funds and collected intelligence on journalists, politicians, and anti-corruption officials
- Ukrainian national police launched criminal investigation to determine if journalists' privacy was violated
- Nine journalists identified include editors from major outlets like Forbes Ukraine, Censor.NET, and investigative platforms Nashi Groshi and Ukrainska Pravda
- Follows pattern of surveillance targeting Ukrainian investigative journalists - in 2024, SBU's Department for Protection of National Statehood spied on 30 journalists at Bihus.Info
- Committee to Protect Journalists calls for accountability, noting surveillance threatens corruption investigations
- Case highlights state-level surveillance infrastructure targeting press freedom advocates

# Pro-Palestine Student Activist Fights Deportation Despite Government Lacking Evidence

**TL;DR:** Mahmoud Khalil faces deportation to Algeria for pro-Palestine activism at Columbia, with newly unsealed DHS memo showing government lacked evidence but proceeded anyway as retaliation for speech.

## Key Points

- Student activist Mahmoud Khalil faces deportation to Algeria for pro-Palestine campus activism at Columbia University
- Other student activists targeted in same crackdown (Öztürk, Mahdawi) have successfully beaten their deportation cases
- Newly unsealed March 2025 DHS memo admits government lacked evidence to support deportation case against Khalil
- Government initially used rare 'foreign policy grounds' provision, later added immigration fraud charge after legal pushback
- ICE transferred Khalil from NYC to Louisiana within hours - unusual speed designed to isolate from support networks
- Immigration judge refused to consider First Amendment claims, denied waiver application without hearing
- Legal team filing motion to move case back to New York and introduce new evidence of government retaliation
- Case demonstrates use of immigration system as tool for political censorship of protected speech

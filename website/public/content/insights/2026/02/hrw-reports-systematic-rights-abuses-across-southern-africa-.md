# HRW Reports Systematic Rights Abuses Across Southern Africa in 2025

**TL;DR:** Human Rights Watch documents widespread government crackdowns on protesters, journalists, and activists across Southern Africa in 2025, with security forces using lethal force and arbitrary detention while regional bodies fail to intervene.

## Key Points

- Security forces in Angola, Eswatini, Mozambique, and Zimbabwe used excessive/lethal force against protesters in 2025
- Governments severely restricted freedom of expression, association, and assembly across the region
- Journalists and human rights defenders faced targeted attacks, detention, and judicial harassment
- African Union and SADC failed to address violations or enforce human rights obligations
- Angola and Mozambique announced investigations into protester deaths but results remain unpublished
- Eswatini still has no accountability for 2021 pro-democracy protest crackdown
- Pattern of impunity creates 'vicious cycles of abuse' according to HRW assessment
- South Africa failed to protect human rights defenders, with some killed

# Cameroon Police Seize Journalists' Equipment Investigating US Migrant Deportations

**TL;DR:** Cameroonian police detained 4 journalists for 5 hours, seized their phones/laptops while investigating secret US deportation flights, threatening to breach device security and expose confidential sources.

## Key Points

- Four journalists detained by Cameroon judicial police while covering secret US migrant deportations
- AP reporters Nalova Akua, Angel Ngwe, Arnold Ndal plus freelancer Randy Joe Sa'ah held for 5 hours
- Police confiscated cellphones, laptop, and other equipment under criminal trespass investigation
- Officers threatened to circumvent device passwords and access private information
- Lawyer warned anonymous sources could be compromised through device forensics
- Journalists threatened with being 'crushed' and told one would 'disappear without a trace'
- Equipment remains with police despite no formal charges filed against journalists
- Incident highlights digital security risks for journalists in authoritarian environments

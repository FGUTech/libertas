# Dave Farber, 'Grandfather of Internet' and EFF Board Member, Dies at 91

**TL;DR:** Dave Farber, longest-serving EFF Board member and internet pioneer known as the 'Grandfather of the Internet,' has died at 91, ending decades of advocacy for digital rights and open source protections.

## Key Points

- Dave Farber, 91, died in Tokyo where he was Distinguished Professor at Keio University
- Served as EFF's longest-tenured Board member since early 1990s, before WWW creation
- Known as 'Grandfather of the Internet' for foundational contributions to internet development
- Managed influential 'Interesting People' email list connecting internet pioneers globally
- Served as Chief Technologist at U.S. Federal Communications Commission (2000-2001)
- Mentored generations of internet developers and open source community members
- Consistently advocated for user rights and developer liberties throughout internet's growth
- Provided credibility and institutional knowledge to EFF's mission for over 30 years

# Iran Arrests Japanese NHK Bureau Chief Amid Systematic Press Crackdown

**TL;DR:** Iranian authorities arrested Japanese journalist Shinnosuke Kawashima and implemented comprehensive surveillance tactics including device confiscation and internet shutdowns during recent protest crackdown.

## Key Points

- IRGC arrested NHK Tehran bureau chief Shinnosuke Kawashima on January 20, transferred to Evin Prison February 23
- Authorities confiscated videographer Mehdi Mohammedi's passport, phone, laptop, camera, and his wife's cell phone
- One NHK staff member fled Iran in early February after repeated summons following bureau chief's arrest
- Part of broader crackdown: 12 journalists arrested since late December protests, 7 remain detained
- Iranian authorities enforced near-total internet shutdown during crackdown period
- Systematic tactics include home raids, bank account blocking, SIM card deactivation, and reporter interrogations
- HRANA reports 7,000+ confirmed killed, 53,000+ arrested in January crackdown alone
- Japanese government demanding early release through diplomatic channels

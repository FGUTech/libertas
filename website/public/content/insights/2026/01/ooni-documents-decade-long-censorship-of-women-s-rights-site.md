# OONI Documents Decade-Long Censorship of Women's Rights Sites in Iran

**TL;DR:** OONI measurements show Iran has systematically blocked women's rights websites since at least 2014, with repression intensifying despite 2022 Women, Life, Freedom protests.

## Key Points

- OONI data confirms women's rights websites blocked in Iran since at least 2014
- Censorship persists despite massive 2022 Women, Life, Freedom protests following Mahsa Jina Amini's death
- UN reports intensified repression of women despite mass demonstrations
- Iran maintains severe restrictions on both women's rights and information access
- Pattern shows systematic targeting of human rights content, not just political sites
- Long-term censorship data provides baseline for measuring circumvention needs
- Demonstrates sustained government commitment to information control around gender issues

## Deep Dive

## Context and Background

OONI's measurements reveal a systematic, decade-long campaign by Iranian authorities to block access to women's rights information online. This censorship predates the 2022 Women, Life, Freedom movement by eight years, indicating that restrictions on gender-related content have been a consistent priority for the regime. The blocking of these sites represents part of Iran's broader information control strategy, which OONI data shows affects numerous categories of content beyond just political opposition materials.

## Technical and Measurement Implications

The OONI data provides concrete technical evidence of blocking mechanisms targeting women's rights content, offering circumvention tool developers specific insights into Iran's filtering infrastructure. This measurement data is crucial for understanding which bypass techniques work effectively for this category of content and how blocking methods may have evolved over the decade-long period. The systematic nature of the blocking suggests sophisticated, policy-driven filtering rather than ad-hoc censorship.

## Threat Model Considerations

The persistence of these blocks through major political upheavals demonstrates that Iranian authorities view women's rights information as a fundamental threat to their control structure. For activists and circumvention tool developers, this indicates that accessing such content carries sustained risk and requires robust operational security. The long timeline also suggests that temporary circumvention victories may be quickly countered by authorities.

## What Builders Should Consider

The combination of sustained censorship pressure with demonstrated user demand (evidenced by the massive protests) creates a clear use case for circumvention tools specifically designed for accessing human rights content. Developers should consider how their tools perform specifically against the blocking methods used for this category of content, as techniques effective for general web browsing may not work for sites that receive heightened filtering attention.

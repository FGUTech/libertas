# Gabon Implements Nationwide Internet Blackout During 2023 Presidential Elections

**TL;DR:** Gabon cut internet access nationwide during August 2023 elections, with service restored after military coup. Government cited preventing violence and misinformation as justification.

## Key Points

- NetBlocks confirmed nation-scale internet blackout across Gabon on August 26, 2023 election day
- Government justified shutdown as preventing 'calls for violence' and 'false information' spread
- Blackout lasted from election day through August 30, when military officers seized power
- Internet connectivity restored after President Ali Bongo was placed under house arrest
- Communications Minister imposed territory-wide curfew alongside internet restrictions
- Pattern continues trend of election-period internet shutdowns across 12+ African nations
- Shutdown targeted multiple ISPs simultaneously, indicating coordinated government action

## Deep Dive

## Regional Pattern of Election Interference

Gabon's internet shutdown represents part of a broader regional trend where African governments systematically cut connectivity during elections. NetBlocks documented similar actions in Zambia, Congo, Uganda, Tanzania, Guinea, Burundi, Togo, Mauritania, Benin, Cameroon, and Mali during recent electoral periods. This pattern suggests coordinated learning between governments about digital control tactics.

## Technical Implementation

The shutdown affected multiple internet service providers simultaneously, indicating government coordination at the ISP level rather than targeting specific platforms. NetBlocks' real-time IP address space mapping (diffscans) confirmed the nation-scale nature of the blackout, distinguishing it from technical failures through their attribution methodology that analyzes network patterns.

## Political Context and Implications

The timing proved significant - internet restoration coincided precisely with military officers announcing they had seized power from President Ali Bongo, whose family had ruled for 56 years. This suggests the blackout may have been intended to prevent opposition coordination during vote counting, but ultimately failed to prevent the government's overthrow.

## Lessons for Freedom Tech

The incident highlights the vulnerability of centralized internet infrastructure to government control and the need for mesh networking, satellite connectivity, and other censorship-resistant communication tools during critical political moments. The pattern across multiple African nations indicates systematic government adoption of digital control tactics.

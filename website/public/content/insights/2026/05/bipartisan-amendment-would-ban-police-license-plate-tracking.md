# Bipartisan Amendment Would Ban Police License Plate Tracking Nationwide

**TL;DR:** A bipartisan federal highway bill amendment would strip funding from cities and states that operate automated license plate tracking programs, effectively ending mass surveillance via ALPR technology.

## Key Points

- Bipartisan amendment targets automated license plate reader (ALPR) surveillance programs
- Would strip federal highway funding from jurisdictions operating plate tracking systems
- Exception carved out for toll collection systems only
- Represents rare bipartisan agreement on surveillance limits
- Would effectively end widespread police use of ALPR mass surveillance
- Uses federal funding leverage to enforce privacy protections nationwide
- Could eliminate databases tracking billions of location records annually

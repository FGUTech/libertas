# EFF: Revised GUARD Act Still Threatens Privacy Despite Narrowed Scope

**TL;DR:** Congress narrowed the GUARD Act to focus on AI companions rather than all AI systems, but EFF warns mandatory age verification still creates serious privacy, speech, and access concerns.

## Key Points

- GUARD Act revised to target 'AI companions' instead of broadly applying to all AI-powered tools
- Bill still requires identity-linked age verification for AI companion services
- Penalties increased dramatically from $100K to $250K per violation
- Age verification systems exclude millions without government ID or bank accounts
- Vague definitions could still capture customer service chatbots and other conversational AI
- Creates incentives for developers to over-restrict access or avoid building certain tools
- EFF maintains opposition, citing ongoing privacy, speech, and security concerns

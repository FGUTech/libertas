# States Block Public Oversight of License Plate Surveillance After Abuse Revelations

**TL;DR:** Multiple US states are passing laws to block public access to ALPR surveillance data after transparency advocates used records requests to expose tracking of protesters, racist policing, and abortion seekers.

## Key Points

- States including Arizona and Connecticut introducing bills to exempt ALPR data from public records laws
- EFF and others used FOIAs to expose ALPR abuses including tracking protesters and targeting Romani people
- Public records revealed police falsely claimed abortion tracking was for 'missing person' case [Source]
- Communities have successfully terminated ALPR contracts after seeing disclosed data-sharing reports
- Proposed exemptions would block access to hit ratios, sharing agreements, and error analyses
- EFF opposes blanket bans, advocates for case-by-case privacy vs transparency balancing
- Pattern shows government limiting oversight after surveillance systems exposed

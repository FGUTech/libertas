# Chinese Authorities Force Malaysia to Detain and Deport Uyghur Scholar

**TL;DR:** Malaysia detained and expelled Uyghur scholar Abdulhakim Idris at China's request, preventing him from launching a book about Chinese pressure on Muslim nations to stay silent on Uyghur persecution.

## Key Points

- Abdulhakim Idris, head of Center for Uyghur Studies, was detained nearly a day and expelled from Malaysia
- Chinese authorities reportedly pressured Malaysia to prevent his book launch on CCP influence in Islamic world
- Book documents how China pressures Muslim governments to remain silent about Uyghur persecution
- Idris has 24 missing family members, including mother, brother, and sister, since 2017
- Uyghurs represent over 20% of incidents in Freedom House's transnational repression database (2014-2025)
- China conducts world's most comprehensive transnational repression campaign targeting activists abroad
- Incident sets precedent threatening American advocates, journalists, and researchers operating internationally

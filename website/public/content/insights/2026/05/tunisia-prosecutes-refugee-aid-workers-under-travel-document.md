# Tunisia Prosecutes Refugee Aid Workers Under Travel Document Laws

**TL;DR:** Tunisia sentenced two refugee aid workers to 2 years prison for providing accommodation to asylum seekers, part of broader crackdown shutting down civil society groups.

## Key Points

- Tunisian Council for Refugees director Mustapha Djemali and manager Abderrazek Krimi sentenced to 2 years prison (6 months suspended)
- Convicted under 1975 passport law for 'harboring persons entering illegally' despite working with UNHCR-registered asylum seekers
- Authorities shut down the refugee council in May 2024, froze bank accounts, and arrested leadership
- Part of broader pattern: at least 9 civil society workers sentenced to prison since January 2026 for legitimate work
- Government suspended UNHCR asylum processing in June 2024, leaving asylum seekers in legal limbo
- Creates chilling effect on humanitarian organizations and demonstrates need for secure coordination tools
- Shows how governments weaponize immigration laws to suppress civil society operations

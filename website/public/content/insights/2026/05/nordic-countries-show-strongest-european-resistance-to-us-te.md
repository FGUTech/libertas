# Nordic Countries Show Strongest European Resistance to US Tech Surveillance

**TL;DR:** 85% of Nordic citizens find Europe's reliance on US tech concerning, with geopolitical tensions driving preference for European alternatives to avoid NSA surveillance and FISA data collection.

## Key Points

- Proton survey of 4,000 Nordic citizens shows 83% believe Europe is reliant on US apps, 85% find this concerning
- Higher resistance than UK/France/Germany (73%) amid Trump-Denmark tensions over Greenland
- 64% of news-following respondents say US-Europe tensions changed their feelings about US tech
- Key concerns include NSA data sharing via Five/Nine/Fourteen Eyes agreements
- FISA Section 702 allows warrantless US surveillance of European communications since 2008
- France dropping Windows, Germany pursuing digital sovereignty as part of broader trend
- Data sovereignty and pro-European sentiment are strongest motivators for switching
- Growing Nordic software industry focusing on specific industries rather than data collection

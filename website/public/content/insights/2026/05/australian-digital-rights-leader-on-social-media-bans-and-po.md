# Australian Digital Rights Leader on Social Media Bans and Post-Pandemic Institutional Distrust

**TL;DR:** Digital Rights Watch founder Lizzie O'Shea discusses Australia's strict under-16 social media ban and how post-pandemic institutional distrust complicates balancing corporate power with democratic oversight.

## Key Points

- Australia enacted world's strictest social media restrictions for minors under 16
- Policy framed as necessary protection from 'dangerous' platforms, excluding youth voices from debate
- O'Shea argues tech debates are ancient power distribution questions, not novel problems
- AI systems have become more opaque and concentrated since her 2019 book 'Future Histories'
- Post-pandemic institutional distrust complicates government regulation of corporate tech power
- Australia sits between US free speech absolutism and European regulatory approaches
- Young people report using social media differently than adults assume, fear different risks

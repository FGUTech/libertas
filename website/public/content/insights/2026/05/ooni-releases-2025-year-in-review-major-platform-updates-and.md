# OONI Releases 2025 Year in Review: Major Platform Updates and Censorship Measurement Advances

**TL;DR:** OONI launched multiplatform mobile apps, thematic censorship pages, and anonymous credential systems in 2025, significantly enhancing global internet censorship measurement capabilities.

## Key Points

- OONI launched unified multiplatform mobile app for Android and iOS in March 2025
- New app architecture enables feature parity and faster deployment across platforms
- Launched OONI Explorer thematic censorship pages for better data visualization
- Developed anonymous credential system to enhance user privacy protection
- Conducted security audits to strengthen platform security
- Published multiple research reports documenting global censorship findings
- Joined Global Network Initiative (GNI) expanding institutional partnerships
- Held Open Measurement Gatherings (OMG) to engage researcher community

## Deep Dive

## Platform Sustainability Milestone

OONI's launch of a multiplatform mobile app represents a critical infrastructure advancement for global censorship measurement. The unified codebase will enable faster feature deployment and ensure consistent functionality across Android and iOS platforms, addressing a key sustainability challenge for decentralized measurement networks.

## Enhanced Privacy and Usability

The introduction of an anonymous credential system and thematic censorship pages on OONI Explorer demonstrates OONI's evolution toward more user-friendly and privacy-preserving tools. The thematic organization likely improves researchers' ability to quickly identify censorship patterns across different content categories and geographic regions.

## Institutional Growth

OONI's membership in the Global Network Initiative (GNI) signals growing institutional recognition of network measurement as essential internet freedom infrastructure. This partnership could provide additional resources and legitimacy for censorship documentation efforts globally.

## What This Means for Builders

The multiplatform architecture and enhanced privacy features provide a template for other freedom tech projects seeking to balance usability, sustainability, and user protection. The security audit practices and community engagement models also offer valuable operational frameworks for similar initiatives.

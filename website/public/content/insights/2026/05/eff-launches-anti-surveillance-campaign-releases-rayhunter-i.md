# EFF Launches Anti-Surveillance Campaign, Releases Rayhunter IMSI Catcher Detection Tool

**TL;DR:** EFF launches 'Claw Back' campaign against mass surveillance as Congress considers Section 702 renewal, highlighting new open-source Rayhunter tool for detecting cell-site simulators used to track activists and journalists.

## Key Points

- EFF launches 'Claw Back' fundraising campaign to combat what NSA whistleblower William Binney called the 'turnkey totalitarian state'
- Congress preparing to renew Section 702 international mass surveillance program affecting millions
- EFF released Rayhunter, open-source tool for detecting cell-site simulators (IMSI catchers) that track protesters and journalists
- Organization suing to stop warrantless searches of Automated License Plate Reader (ALPR) databases revealing driver movements
- EFF published 'Selling Safety' report exposing misleading marketing claims by police surveillance technology companies
- Privacy Badger browser extension continues blocking hidden trackers used by Big Tech and data brokers
- Campaign emphasizes removing surveillance tools from government hands rather than just changing leadership

## Deep Dive

## Context and Timing

EFF's 'Claw Back' campaign comes at a critical moment as Congress debates renewing Section 702, the warrantless international surveillance program that affects millions of Americans. The timing suggests coordinated advocacy to influence legislative decisions, building on William Binney's warning about a 'turnkey totalitarian state' where surveillance infrastructure remains constant regardless of political leadership changes.

## Technical Tools and Legal Strategy

EFF's multi-pronged approach combines technical tools with legal advocacy. Rayhunter represents a significant advancement in counter-surveillance, providing open-source detection capabilities for IMSI catchers that law enforcement uses to track activists, journalists, and protesters. This democratizes surveillance detection previously available only to sophisticated actors. Simultaneously, their legal challenges to warrantless ALPR searches target the infrastructure enabling mass location tracking of millions of drivers.

## Strategic Implications for Freedom Tech

The campaign demonstrates how established civil liberties organizations are increasingly adopting technical solutions alongside traditional advocacy. By releasing open-source tools like Rayhunter and Privacy Badger, EFF is directly empowering individuals to protect themselves rather than relying solely on policy changes. This dual approach—technical empowerment plus legal/legislative pressure—provides a model for other freedom tech initiatives.

## What Builders Should Consider

EFF's approach shows the importance of combining technical development with institutional advocacy. The success of Privacy Badger (used by millions) demonstrates demand for accessible privacy tools. For builders, this suggests opportunities in developing user-friendly counter-surveillance tools, particularly for detecting and mitigating location tracking. The emphasis on open-source development ensures tools remain trustworthy and auditable by the community.

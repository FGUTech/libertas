# Zambia Cancels Major Digital Rights Summit RightsCon 2026 Amid Foreign Pressure

**TL;DR:** Zambia effectively canceled RightsCon 2026, a leading human rights and technology summit, citing vague administrative concerns, with reports suggesting Chinese government displeasure over Taiwanese participants influenced the decision.

## Key Points

- Zambian government postponed RightsCon 2026 scheduled for May 5-8 in Lusaka, effectively canceling the event
- Official reasons cited 'comprehensive disclosure' needs and 'pending administrative and security clearances' for speakers
- Civil society sources report Chinese government expressed displeasure about invited Taiwanese participants
- Summit venue Mulungushi Conference Center was refurbished with $60M Chinese government funding in 2020
- Access Now, the organizers, advised registered participants not to travel to Lusaka
- Over 130 digital rights stakeholders condemned the cancellation as closing civic space in Zambia
- Timing coincides with new restrictive Cyber Security Act and Cyber Crimes Act enacted in April 2025
- Cancellation occurs weeks before August 2026 general election campaign period begins
- Pattern follows previous RightsCon access issues in Costa Rica (2023) and censorship concerns in Saudi Arabia (2024)

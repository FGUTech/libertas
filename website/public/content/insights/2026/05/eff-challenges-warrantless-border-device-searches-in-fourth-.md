# EFF Challenges Warrantless Border Device Searches in Fourth Circuit

**TL;DR:** EFF filed amicus brief in U.S. v. Belmonte Cardozo urging Fourth Circuit to require warrants for all electronic device searches at borders, as CBP conducted 55,318 warrantless device searches in FY2025.

## Key Points

- EFF, ACLU, and NACDL filed amicus brief in Fourth Circuit case U.S. v. Belmonte Cardozo
- Case involves warrantless manual search of U.S. citizen's phone at Dulles airport after Bolivia trip
- CBP conducted 55,318 device searches (manual and forensic) in FY2025 without warrants
- EFF argues both manual and forensic searches should require probable cause warrants
- Current Fourth Circuit precedent requires individualized suspicion only for forensic searches
- Manual searches involve officers browsing devices; forensic searches use extraction tools
- Both methods access highly personal data including political, religious, health information
- Fourth Circuit heard oral arguments May 8, could be first circuit to fully protect border device privacy

# Indonesia Plans Government Vetting System for Human Rights Defenders

**TL;DR:** Indonesia's government announced plans to create an assessment team to determine who qualifies as a 'genuine' human rights defender, following acid attack on activist Andrie Yunus by soldiers.

## Key Points

- Human Rights Minister Natalius Pigai announced plan to amend 1999 Human Rights Law to allow state vetting of rights defenders
- Proposal follows March 2026 acid attack on activist Andrie Yunus allegedly by four Indonesian soldiers
- Assessment team would include government officials, civil society, and law enforcement to evaluate activist legitimacy
- Paid activists would be disqualified from protection, potentially undermining NGO operations
- Concurrent draft bill on 'foreign propaganda' would give state authority to designate criticism as disinformation
- Rights groups argue government shouldn't control who qualifies as defender since state often violates rights
- Plan would weaken independence of National Commission on Human Rights through forced merger
- Pattern of escalating attacks on Indonesian activists including shootings and arrests during protests

# Framework for Human Rights-Based AI Governance Proposed

**TL;DR:** Feminist technologist Rebecca Ryakitimbo proposes five non-negotiable rights for human-centered AI: life/liberty, equality, free speech, access to essentials, and privacy/data sovereignty.

## Key Points

- Article proposes framework grounding AI governance in human rights law dating back to ancient civilizations
- Five core rights identified: life/liberty with human oversight, algorithmic fairness, free speech protection, equitable resource access, privacy/data sovereignty
- Emphasizes AI should be 'of the people, by the people, and for the people' similar to democratic governance principles
- Calls for technical safeguards including differential privacy, federated learning, bias audits, and 'right to be forgotten'
- Author Rebecca Ryakitimbo works on AI/gender justice intersection through African Women School of AI and community connectivity initiatives
- Framework addresses power imbalances in AI design from training data to compute access
- Published as part of Global Voices series emphasizing human knowledge sharing over AI dependence

# EFF Defends Section 230 Immunity for App Store Payment Processing

**TL;DR:** EFF argues to 9th Circuit that app stores shouldn't lose Section 230 immunity for processing payments, warning this could force widespread content censorship across platforms.

## Key Points

- EFF filed second amicus brief defending Apple, Google, Facebook app stores in social casino lawsuit
- Lower court ruled platforms lose Section 230 immunity when processing payments for user content
- Cases involve social casino apps where users buy virtual chips with real money
- EFF warns ruling would expose all payment-facilitating platforms to liability
- Platforms like Etsy, Patreon could be forced to censor content to avoid legal exposure
- Brief argues no distinction should exist between hosting content and processing payments for same content
- Stakes include foundational protections for internet intermediaries and user speech

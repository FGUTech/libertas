# Myanmar Junta Revokes Licenses of 3 Media Outlets Under Martial Law

**TL;DR:** Myanmar's military junta revoked licenses of three news outlets serving conflict-affected Chin state under martial law, forcing journalists underground and into exile operations.

## Key Points

- Myanmar junta revoked licenses of Khonumthung Media Group, Chin World, and Myatmaukkhit News Agency on April 9
- Bans imposed under martial law across 60 townships for alleged violations of national security
- Outlets prohibited from publishing on any media platform or technology
- Several journalists from banned outlets have gone into hiding to avoid arrest
- Independent Myanmar Journalists Association coordinating via messaging apps with press freedom groups
- Part of broader crackdown since 2021 coup that has driven media operations into exile
- Myanmar holds at least 18 journalists in prison, ranking second globally for journalist imprisonment
- Demonstrates escalating information warfare in conflict zones

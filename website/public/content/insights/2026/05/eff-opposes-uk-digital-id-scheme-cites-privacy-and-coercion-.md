# EFF Opposes UK Digital ID Scheme, Cites Privacy and Coercion Concerns

**TL;DR:** EFF submitted formal opposition to UK's proposed national digital ID system, highlighting mission creep, privacy violations, and coercive digital participation risks.

## Key Points

- UK PM Keir Starmer announced digital ID scheme creating virtual IDs on personal devices with biometric data
- 2.9 million people signed parliamentary petition opposing the national digital ID plans
- EFF joined 12 UK civil society organizations in formal opposition to the scheme
- EFF submission identifies 6 core issues: mission creep, privacy violations, security risks, inaccurate tech, discrimination, power imbalances
- System would function as gateway controlling access to essential services, not just identity verification
- Government consultation 'Making public services work for you with your digital identity' seeks public input
- EFF argues no safeguards can address fundamental power shift from individuals to state
- Opposition emphasizes coercive nature forcing digital participation for public life access

# EFF Warns Federal SECURE Data Act Would Weaken Existing State Privacy Protections

**TL;DR:** EFF says the Republican-backed SECURE Data Act would preempt dozens of state privacy laws while offering weaker consumer protections and no private enforcement rights.

## Key Points

- House Republicans released SECURE Data Act draft without bipartisan support
- Bill would preempt 21 existing state consumer privacy laws and potentially hundreds of related protections
- No private right of action - only FTC and state AGs can enforce violations
- Companies get 45-day 'cure period' with no penalties after being caught violating law
- Requires opt-out rather than opt-in for invasive practices like targeted advertising
- Would eliminate California's data broker deletion tool and automatic opt-out signals
- Bill includes vague Commerce Secretary powers over 'international flow of personal data'
- EFF assessment: weaker than previous federal proposals and most state laws

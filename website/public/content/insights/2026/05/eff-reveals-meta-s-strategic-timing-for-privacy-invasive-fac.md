# EFF Reveals Meta's Strategic Timing for Privacy-Invasive Face Recognition Release

**TL;DR:** Internal Meta documents show the company planned to launch face recognition for smart glasses during periods when civil rights groups would be distracted by other political concerns.

## Key Points

- Internal Meta document reveals strategic timing to launch face recognition software for smart glasses when civil society groups are distracted
- EFF reports Google broke promises to inform users about government surveillance requests
- Palantir allegedly failing to meet human rights commitments despite public policy statements
- EFF actively suing DHS and ICE over efforts to unmask online critics through subpoenas
- Organization developing privacy-enhancing free software tools including RayHunter project
- Demonstrates pattern of coordinated corporate privacy erosion across major tech companies
- EFF leveraging legal action, technical tools, and policy advocacy as multi-pronged response

# Turkish Journalist Arrested Under Disinformation Law for School Weapons Report

**TL;DR:** Turkish journalist Yelis Ayaz arrested for reporting on politician's son allegedly bringing weapons to school, highlighting systematic use of disinformation law to prosecute media.

## Key Points

- Yelis Ayaz, editor-in-chief of Aydınpost newspaper, arrested May 15 for 'publicly spreading disinformation'
- Report concerned allegations that AKP deputy's underage son brought knife and gun to school
- Ayaz claims multiple parents filed complaints about the student; prosecutor says it was toy gun
- Turkey's 2022 disinformation law carries 1-3 year sentences for false information causing 'concern, fear, or panic'
- Committee to Protect Journalists reports disinformation law has become 'most frequently used' against media
- Case demonstrates legal framework being weaponized to suppress investigative reporting
- Pattern shows state using ambiguous 'disinformation' definitions to control information flow

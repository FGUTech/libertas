# DOJ Launches Criminal Investigation Into Trans Youth Healthcare Providers

**TL;DR:** Trump DOJ issued criminal grand jury subpoenas to hospitals including NYU Langone demanding records of trans youth patients and medical staff, escalating from previous civil investigations.

## Key Points

- NYU Langone received criminal grand jury subpoena from Northern District of Texas demanding trans youth patient records and provider information
- Subpoena covers patients under 18 who received gender-affirming care between 2020-2026, including medical records and provider details
- Escalation from previous administrative subpoenas to criminal investigation targeting healthcare providers
- Multiple hospitals reportedly received similar subpoenas, with case strategically filed in conservative Texas district
- NYU Langone already shuttered its trans youth program due to federal funding threats, proving compliance doesn't prevent prosecution
- New York Shield Law designed to protect from out-of-state prosecution, but untested against federal criminal cases
- Previous administrative subpoenas have been successfully challenged - Children's Hospital LA reached settlement withdrawing patient-identifying information demands
- Rhode Island Hospital ordered to comply with similar DOJ subpoena, but state advocates filed emergency motion to quash

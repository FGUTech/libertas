# Venezuelan Journalist Still Faces Charges Despite Post-Maduro Amnesty Law

**TL;DR:** Venezuelan journalist Rory Branker was released after Maduro's ouster but denied amnesty, highlighting inconsistent application of freedom protections and ongoing press censorship via website blocking requiring VPNs.

## Key Points

- Rory Branker, chief content officer of La Patilla, was released from prison after U.S. military ousted Maduro in January
- Despite new amnesty law, Branker was denied amnesty and still faces terrorism, treason, and conspiracy charges
- Eight of 28 released journalists still face criminal charges due to inconsistent amnesty application
- La Patilla remains blocked in Venezuela, accessible only via VPN despite government change
- At least 65 media outlet websites still blocked by government according to SNTP and Safe and Free Connection
- Branker was tortured and held in isolation for seven months after being detained in February 2025
- Interior Minister Diosdado Cabello, who previously sued La Patilla, remains in new government
- 29 journalists detained this year though quickly released, indicating continued press freedom threats

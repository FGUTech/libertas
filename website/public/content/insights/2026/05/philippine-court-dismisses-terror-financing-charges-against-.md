# Philippine Court Dismisses Terror Financing Charges Against NGO; Bank Accounts Remain Frozen

**TL;DR:** A Philippine court dismissed terrorism financing charges against NGO CERNET, but authorities continue weaponizing anti-money laundering laws against civil society despite being removed from FATF grey list.

## Key Points

- Court dismissed terrorism financing charges against Community Empowerment Resource Network (CERNET) for "insufficiently charged" case
- Prosecutors alleged CERNET transferred $2,200 to Communist armed group without sufficient evidence
- Anti-Money Laundering Council froze CERNET's bank accounts in 2023, affecting 70% of organization's operations
- Bank accounts remain frozen despite court ruling in CERNET's favor
- Philippines removed from FATF grey list in February 2025 but continues filing new cases against activists
- Demonstrates how traditional banking systems enable financial censorship of civil society groups
- Pattern shows FATF compliance mechanisms being weaponized for political repression of legitimate organizations

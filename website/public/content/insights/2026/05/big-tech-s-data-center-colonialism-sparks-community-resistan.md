# Big Tech's Data Center 'Colonialism' Sparks Community Resistance in Latin America

**TL;DR:** Major tech companies are rapidly expanding AI data centers across Latin America, creating water shortages and energy price hikes while governments fast-track approvals without community consultation.

## Key Points

- Data center demand projected to rise 20% annually until 2030 driven by AI boom
- Tech executives held thousands of meetings with Latin American officials (2012-2025) lobbying for favorable policies [CLIP investigation]
- Brazil leads region with 206 data centers, plans $3 trillion investment over 5 years with special tax incentives
- Scala's 'AI City' in flood-devastated Eldorado do Sul will be largest Latin American data center (7M sq meters, $50B)
- Brazilian government simplified environmental licensing, waiving Environmental Impact Studies and public hearings
- Facilities require massive resources: Scala facility alone needs 1.8 GW power (equivalent to 6M person city)
- Communities face water shortages, higher electricity bills, and exclusion from approval processes
- Researcher calls expansion 'terraforming' and 'new colonialism' concentrating benefits in Global North
- Chile's data centers increased from 6 (2017) to 66 (2026); Argentina has 42, Uruguay has 10
- IDEC study warns of 'communities running out of water' and projects approved without population consultation

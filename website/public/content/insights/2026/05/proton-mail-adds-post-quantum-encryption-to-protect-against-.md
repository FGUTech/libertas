# Proton Mail Adds Post-Quantum Encryption to Protect Against Future Threats

**TL;DR:** Proton Mail launches optional post-quantum encryption for all users to protect encrypted emails against future quantum computer attacks that could break current cryptography.

## Key Points

- Proton Mail introduces post-quantum cryptography (PQC) as optional upgrade for all plans including free
- New feature protects against future quantum computers using Shor's algorithm to break RSA/ECC encryption
- Implementation uses OpenPGP v6 framework to enable modern quantum-resistant algorithms
- PQC protection applies to new emails going forward, does not retroactively encrypt existing messages
- Proton working with Thunderbird and other providers to standardize quantum-safe email across ecosystem
- Users can generate and manage PQC keys similar to existing RSA/ECC key management
- Addresses 'harvest now, decrypt later' threat where attackers collect encrypted data for future quantum decryption

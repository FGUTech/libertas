# Mercenary Hackers Target Egyptian Opposition Journalists with Spear-Phishing Campaign

**TL;DR:** Access Now investigation reveals Asia-linked mercenary hackers targeted prominent Egyptian opposition journalists Mustafa Al-Aasar and Ahmed Al-Tantawi with sophisticated spear-phishing attacks in 2023-2024.

## Key Points

- Access Now documented spear-phishing campaign targeting Egyptian journalists Mustafa Al-Aasar and Ahmed Al-Tantawi between 2023-2024
- Attackers used sophisticated social engineering impersonating trusted services like Apple and Signal
- Lookout security firm independently attributed attacks to Asia-linked mercenary hacking group
- Infrastructure capable of deploying Android spyware with full device access capabilities
- Al-Tantawi previously targeted with Predator spyware in 2021 and 2023, per Citizen Lab research
- Similar attack successfully compromised Lebanese journalist's Apple account in 2025 (SMEX investigation)
- Attacks failed due to targets' digital security awareness and two-factor authentication
- Campaign shows persistent infrastructure suggesting ongoing operations against MENA civil society

## Deep Dive

## Context and Attribution

This investigation by Access Now, in collaboration with Lookout, reveals a sophisticated mercenary hacking operation targeting Egyptian opposition figures. The timing is significant - attacks occurred during Al-Tantawi's 2023 presidential campaign preparations, before authorities arrested his supporters and family members, forcing his withdrawal from the race. The use of mercenary hackers provides plausible deniability for state actors while maintaining technical sophistication.

## Technical Infrastructure Analysis

The attackers deployed a persistent, interconnected infrastructure spanning domains, hosting providers, and development frameworks. Most concerning is evidence the infrastructure can deploy Android spyware capable of comprehensive device compromise - file access, contacts, SMS, location tracking, microphone/camera activation, and malicious app installation. The spear-phishing component used carefully crafted impersonation of trusted services, including Signal, prompting the company to issue user warnings about such campaigns.

## Broader Surveillance Ecosystem

This campaign fits within Egypt's documented surveillance apparatus. Citizen Lab and Amnesty International have previously exposed Egypt's use of Predator spyware from Intellexa, which specifically targeted Al-Tantawi in 2021 and 2023. The mercenary approach represents an evolution - outsourcing operations while maintaining the same targeting objectives. The successful compromise of a Lebanese journalist in 2025 using similar infrastructure suggests regional expansion of these capabilities.

## Implications for Digital Rights

The failed attacks demonstrate the critical importance of security awareness and proper 2FA implementation. However, the persistent infrastructure and successful Lebanese compromise show these operations continue evolving. The mercenary model enables broader targeting while complicating attribution, potentially lowering barriers for state surveillance programs across the MENA region.

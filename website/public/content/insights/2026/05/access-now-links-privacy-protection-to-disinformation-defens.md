# Access Now Links Privacy Protection to Disinformation Defense in Latin America

**TL;DR:** Digital rights org Access Now argues that strict data protection laws are key to reducing disinformation impact, especially during elections in Latin America where microtargeting exploits personal data for political manipulation.

## Key Points

- Access Now published analysis connecting data privacy enforcement to disinformation mitigation
- Report focuses on Latin American electoral contexts (Argentina, Mexico, Brazil, Colombia, Peru)
- Identifies microtargeting as primary vector: personal data enables targeted political manipulation
- AI-generated deepfakes and bot networks amplify microtargeting effectiveness
- Platform business models incentivize data collection that enables manipulation
- Recommends data protection authorities coordinate with electoral authorities
- Proposes disabling microtargeting tools during electoral periods
- Calls for transparency requirements and algorithmic auditing mechanisms

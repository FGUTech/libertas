# EFF Coalition Opposes UK Age-Gating Requirements That Threaten Web Anonymity

**TL;DR:** EFF and 18 organizations warn UK policymakers that proposed age verification requirements would undermine internet privacy and anonymity while fragmenting the open web into restricted jurisdictions.

## Key Points

- Coalition of 19 organizations including EFF, Mozilla, and Tor Project oppose UK age-gating proposals
- Children's Wellbeing and Schools Bill could mandate age verification across social media, VPNs, and basic websites
- Age assurance technologies are either inaccurate, privacy-invasive, or both according to coalition
- Proposals would force identity verification for web access, expanding surveillance and data breach risks
- Coalition warns of internet fragmentation into restricted jurisdictions and gatekeeper dominance
- Letter argues current approach ignores root causes: engagement-maximizing business models and data collection
- Organizations call for targeting platform design and business models rather than access restrictions
- Policy could cut off vital information access and support networks for young people

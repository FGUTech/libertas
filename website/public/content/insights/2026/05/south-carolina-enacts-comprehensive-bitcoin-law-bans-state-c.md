# South Carolina Enacts Comprehensive Bitcoin Law, Bans State CBDC Use

**TL;DR:** South Carolina passed Senate Bill 163 with bipartisan support, protecting Bitcoin self-custody rights, preventing additional crypto taxes, and banning state entities from using CBDCs.

## Key Points

- Governor Henry McMaster signed Senate Bill 163 into law on May 19, 2025
- Bill passed with overwhelming bipartisan support: 38-1 in Senate, 110-1 in House
- Affirms right of individuals and businesses to use Bitcoin for commerce
- Guarantees right to hold assets in self-hosted or hardware wallets
- Prevents state and local governments from imposing additional taxes on crypto payments
- Bars state agencies from accepting CBDCs or participating in Federal Reserve CBDC testing
- Protects crypto mining operations from discriminatory local regulations
- Exempts mining, node operation, and software development from money transmitter licensing
- Part of broader state-level trend with Kentucky and Missouri pursuing similar measures

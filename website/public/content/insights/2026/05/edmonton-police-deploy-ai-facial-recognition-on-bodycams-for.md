# Edmonton Police Deploy AI Facial Recognition on Bodycams for 'High-Risk Offenders'

**TL;DR:** Edmonton Police Service is trialing bodycam facial recognition technology targeting 'high-risk offenders,' with experts calling it Canada's most dangerous algorithmic surveillance program to date.

## Key Points

- Edmonton Police Service conducting trial of AI facial recognition integrated with bodycams
- Technology targets individuals classified as 'high-risk offenders' by police
- Expert Kate Robertson calls it 'most high risk algorithmic surveillance program' observed in Canada
- Robertson is senior research associate with decade of experience studying algorithmic policing
- Program represents significant escalation in real-time surveillance capabilities
- Bodycam integration enables continuous facial recognition during police interactions
- Sets precedent for widespread deployment of AI surveillance in Canadian policing

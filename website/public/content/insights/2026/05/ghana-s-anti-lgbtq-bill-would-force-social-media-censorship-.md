# Ghana's Anti-LGBTQ+ Bill Would Force Social Media Censorship, Mandate Public Surveillance

**TL;DR:** Ghana's proposed Human Sexual Rights and Family Values Bill 2025 would force social media platforms to censor LGBTQ+ content and require citizens to report suspected LGBTQ+ people to authorities, creating surveillance infrastructure that threatens digital rights.

## Key Points

- Access Now and Rightify Ghana issued joint memo opposing Ghana's Human Sexual Rights and Family Values Bill 2025
- Bill would force social media companies to censor LGBTQ+ content
- Law would mandate public reporting of suspected LGBTQ+ people to police and religious leaders
- Activists advocating for LGBTQ+ rights could face up to 10 years in prison
- Creates surveillance infrastructure exposing private intimacy to state authorities
- Rightify Ghana reports increased entrapment and blackmail on dating apps and messaging platforms
- Bill violates constitutional rights to privacy and freedom of expression online
- Would prevent human rights defenders from documenting discrimination and violence

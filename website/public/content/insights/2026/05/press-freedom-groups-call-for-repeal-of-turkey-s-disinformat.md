# Press Freedom Groups Call for Repeal of Turkey's Disinformation Law Used Against Journalists

**TL;DR:** CPJ and 22 organizations condemned Turkey's disinformation law for prosecuting journalists, calling for imprisoned reporters' release and law's repeal due to vague definitions.

## Key Points

- Committee to Protect Journalists (CPJ) joined 22 press freedom organizations in condemning Turkey's disinformation law
- Joint statement released April 28 targets frequent prosecution and imprisonment of journalists under the law
- Organizations demand release of all journalists imprisoned under disinformation provisions
- Law criticized as 'structurally incompatible with international press freedom standards'
- Vague language fails to clearly define 'untrue information' or national security threats
- Demonstrates broader pattern of governments using disinformation laws as censorship tools
- Creates legal environment that increases demand for censorship-resistant communication platforms

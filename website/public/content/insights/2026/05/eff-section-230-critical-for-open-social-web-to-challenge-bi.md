# EFF: Section 230 Critical for Open Social Web to Challenge Big Tech

**TL;DR:** EFF argues Section 230 protections are essential for decentralized social platforms like Mastodon and Bluesky to survive and compete with Big Tech monopolies.

## Key Points

- EFF warns that weakening Section 230 would benefit Big Tech at expense of open alternatives
- Open Social Web (Fediverse, ATmosphere protocols) puts communities back in control via decentralization
- Section 230 protects small hosts and users from liability for others' speech content
- Without 230 protections, only well-resourced platforms could survive legal costs
- Law enables diverse moderation approaches and community self-governance
- Protections extend to users sharing/boosting content, not just platform operators
- Big Tech can absorb multimillion-dollar lawsuits while small hosts would be 'picked off one by one'
- Article positions decentralized protocols as superior alternative to corporate surveillance platforms

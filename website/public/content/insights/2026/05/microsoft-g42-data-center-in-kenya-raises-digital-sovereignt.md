# Microsoft-G42 Data Center in Kenya Raises Digital Sovereignty Concerns

**TL;DR:** Microsoft and G42's $1B data center project in Kenya mirrors historical exploitation patterns, raising concerns about foreign control of national infrastructure, tax avoidance, and digital colonialism.

## Key Points

- Microsoft and G42 building $1B data center in Naivasha, Kenya on KenGen land
- Project parallels 1980s greenhouse boom that led to worker exploitation and environmental issues
- Foreign entities (US-based Microsoft, UAE-based G42) will control sensitive national data infrastructure
- Kenya-UAE Double Taxation Agreement allows profit shifting from 30% Kenyan tax to 9% UAE rate
- Questions raised about land rates, water/electricity burden on taxpayers vs corporations
- Location at Olkaria geothermal fields provides renewable energy but concentrates infrastructure control
- Activists warn of potential digital colonialism and erosion of economic sovereignty

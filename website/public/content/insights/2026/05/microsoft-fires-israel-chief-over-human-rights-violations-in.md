# Microsoft Fires Israel Chief Over Human Rights Violations in Gaza Surveillance Ops

**TL;DR:** Microsoft terminated its Israel Country General Manager after investigations revealed Azure cloud/AI services were used for mass surveillance and military targeting in Gaza, violating company human rights standards.

## Key Points

- Microsoft fired Israel chief amid ethical controversy over Gaza surveillance operations
- Guardian report revealed Microsoft Azure used for mass surveillance and military targeting systems
- Company suspended certain services in September 2025 after initial investigation
- Civil society coalition demanded Microsoft release investigation findings and implement safeguards
- EFF, Access Now, Amnesty pressed for suspension of business tied to human rights abuses
- Microsoft distinguished itself from peers by taking accountability action rather than denial
- Google and Amazon face similar criticism but haven't shown comparable responsiveness
- Sets precedent that human rights violations can have employment consequences for tech leaders

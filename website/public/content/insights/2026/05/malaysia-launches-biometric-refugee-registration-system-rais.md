# Malaysia Launches Biometric Refugee Registration System Raising Surveillance Concerns

**TL;DR:** Malaysia's new biometric refugee registration system lacks privacy safeguards and enables increased surveillance of 210,000+ refugees, replacing UN oversight with government control.

## Key Points

- Malaysia launched Dokumen Pendaftaran Pelarian (DPP) system in January 2026 to replace UNHCR registration
- System developed by MIMOS Berhad collects biometrics for comprehensive refugee database covering 210,000+ people
- No indication DPP complies with data protection standards or includes appeal mechanisms [HRW]
- Rollout began with Rohingya refugees in detention centers, expanding to broader refugee population
- Previous 2022 system (TRIS/MyRC) failed - cardholders continued facing arrest and deportation
- Intensified enforcement saw 92,000 irregular migrants arrested in 2025 vs 12,000 in 2021
- Activists report living in fear, cutting back community support work due to surveillance concerns
- System operates under unpublished National Security Council Directive No. 23
- UNHCR access to detention centers denied since 2019, limiting oversight of registered refugees

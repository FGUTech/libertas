# EFF Publishes Comprehensive Guide to Circumventing Internet Shutdowns

**TL;DR:** EFF released a practical guide covering mesh networks, peer-to-peer messaging, ham radio, and satellite internet for maintaining communications during government shutdowns and disasters.

## Key Points

- EFF published comprehensive guide on circumventing internet shutdowns using multiple technologies
- Covers Meshtastic mesh networking ($20 devices for local peer-to-peer messaging via LoRA)
- Reviews peer-to-peer apps: Briar (Bluetooth mesh), Bitchat (Nostr/Tor/Bluetooth), Delta Chat (PGP over email)
- Discusses ham radio for long-distance communications and satellite internet for shutdown circumvention
- Highlights recent real-world usage: Bitchat in Iran 2026 shutdown, satellite internet sharing
- Emphasizes pre-deployment necessity - tools must be installed before shutdowns occur
- Notes 14 ongoing internet shutdowns worldwide as of publication
- Cites recent shutdowns in Iran (656+ executions following), Venezuela (US military operation), and others

## Deep Dive

## Context and Escalating Pattern

The EFF's guide comes amid an alarming escalation in both frequency and severity of internet shutdowns globally. The article documents particularly severe cases including Iran's 2026 shutdown that preceded 656+ executions, and a reported US military operation in Venezuela coordinated with cyber attacks on internet infrastructure. With 14 ongoing shutdowns at time of publication, the need for practical circumvention tools has become critical.

## Technical Solutions Assessment

The guide evaluates multiple technical approaches with honest assessments of their limitations. Meshtastic emerges as particularly promising due to low cost ($20) and use of unlicensed spectrum, but faces adoption challenges. Peer-to-peer messaging apps like Briar and Bitchat offer sophisticated routing (Bluetooth mesh, Tor, Nostr) but suffer from network effects - they're most useful when widely deployed before shutdowns occur. Delta Chat's use of email infrastructure provides resilience even with minimal connectivity.

## Strategic Implications for Freedom Tech

The article underscores a critical insight: effective shutdown circumvention requires community-level preparation, not individual technical solutions. The emphasis on accessibility ("You shouldn't have to be a tech wizard") highlights the importance of usability in freedom tech design. The documented real-world usage of these tools during actual shutdowns provides valuable validation of technical approaches and user adoption patterns.

## What Builders Should Consider

The guide reveals that adoption remains the primary challenge for mesh networking solutions. Technical capabilities matter less than community deployment and ease of use. The success of tools like Delta Chat in Iran suggests that leveraging existing infrastructure (email) may be more effective than purely decentralized approaches. Builders should prioritize pre-crisis adoption strategies and focus on tools that can piggyback on familiar interfaces or existing infrastructure where possible.

# EFF Urges EU to Prioritize Privacy and User Sovereignty in Digital Fairness Act

**TL;DR:** EFF calls for EU's proposed Digital Fairness Act to ban dark patterns, tackle surveillance-based business models, and strengthen user control rather than expanding platform surveillance powers.

## Key Points

- EFF provides recommendations for EU's proposed Digital Fairness Act (DFA)
- Calls for explicit prohibition of dark patterns that manipulate user choices
- Advocates tackling surveillance advertising and banning pay-for-privacy schemes
- Proposes supporting automated privacy signals from browsers and OS
- Emphasizes strengthening user sovereignty over digital goods and services
- Warns against age verification mandates that expand surveillance
- Addresses post-sale restrictions and technical lock-ins that limit user control
- Part of broader EU enforcement era following DSA, DMA, and AI Act implementation

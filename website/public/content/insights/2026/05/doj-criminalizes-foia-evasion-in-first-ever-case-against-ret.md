# DOJ Criminalizes FOIA Evasion in First-Ever Case Against Retired NIH Scientist

**TL;DR:** Trump DOJ charged retired NIH scientist David Morens with felony crimes for using personal email to evade FOIA requests, marking unprecedented criminalization of transparency law violations.

## Key Points

- David Morens, 78, arrested and charged with up to 45 years in prison for allegedly evading FOIA using personal Gmail accounts
- Former NIAID senior adviser reportedly deleted emails and wrote about making records 'disappear' before FOIA searches
- Justice Department historically treated FOIA violations as civil matters, not criminal - this represents major precedent shift
- Requests came from Heritage Foundation, Judicial Watch, and U.S. Right to Know regarding Covid research communications
- Selective enforcement concern: unclear if same charges would apply to violations involving progressive requesters
- Broader context: Trump admin has gutted FOIA offices, removed public health data from websites, fired FOIA officials
- HHS Secretary RFK Jr. has hollowed out department FOIA offices, with 1,100+ backlogged requests at Morens' former bureau
- Creates dangerous precedent where FOIA compliance may depend on political alignment of requesters

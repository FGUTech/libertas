# ICE Protesters Sue DHS/FBI Over Forced DNA Collection During Arrests

**TL;DR:** Four protesters filed suit against DHS and FBI for allegedly collecting DNA samples from Americans arrested during peaceful ICE protests in Chicago, claiming permanent storage in federal databases violates constitutional rights.

## Key Points

- Four protesters filed federal lawsuit in Illinois against DHS and FBI over DNA collection
- Arrests occurred during 'Operation Midway Blitz' ICE enforcement action in Chicago
- Lawsuit alleges DNA samples taken from peaceful protesters and uploaded to government databases
- Protesters claim violations of First and Fourth Amendments plus Administrative Procedure Act
- Government allegedly storing DNA samples and genetic profiles permanently in federal labs
- Case highlights expansion of biometric surveillance targeting political activists
- Represents potential chilling effect on First Amendment protest activities

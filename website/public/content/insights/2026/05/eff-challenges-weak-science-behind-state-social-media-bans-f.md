# EFF Challenges Weak Science Behind State Social Media Bans for Youth

**TL;DR:** EFF argues that state legislatures are rushing to ban youth social media access based on flawed research and 'pop psychology' rather than settled science, threatening constitutional rights.

## Key Points

- Multiple states including California, Massachusetts, and Minnesota advancing social media bans for minors
- EFF argues legislation based on statistically flawed studies and 'pop psychology' narratives
- Research claiming social media causes teen mental health crisis described as 'mixed, blurry, and contradictory'
- Independent researchers from UC Irvine and Brown University dispute 'great rewiring' theory
- Large-scale meta-analyses fail to show consistent association between social media and declining well-being
- Studies often ignore alternative factors like pandemic isolation, school violence, economic stress
- Legislative push heavily relies on Jonathan Haidt's 'The Anxious Generation' despite his lack of clinical psychology credentials
- EFF frames issue as constitutional rights violation affecting youth free speech and privacy

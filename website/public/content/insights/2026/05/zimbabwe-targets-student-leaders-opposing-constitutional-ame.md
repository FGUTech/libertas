# Zimbabwe Targets Student Leaders Opposing Constitutional Amendment

**TL;DR:** Zimbabwean authorities are systematically abducting, torturing, and arresting student leaders protesting a constitutional amendment that would extend presidential terms from 5 to 7 years.

## Key Points

- Constitutional Amendment No 3 would extend presidential and parliamentary terms from 5 to 7 years, delaying 2028 elections until 2030
- Zimbabwe National Students Union (ZINASU) leaders mobilized opposition to the amendment during public consultations
- Human Rights Watch documented 7 attacks including abductions by suspected Central Intelligence Organisation (CIO) agents
- Student leader Denford Sithole was tortured for 6 hours, beaten with wine bottles, and forced to reveal bank records and contacts
- Multiple students now in hiding after surveillance and death threats; some suspended studies and fled their cities
- Police raided ZINASU offices in Harare as part of broader crackdown on amendment opponents
- Pattern of systematic repression demonstrates conditions where freedom tech becomes essential for activist coordination
- Cases span multiple cities showing coordinated state response to democratic opposition

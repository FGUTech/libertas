# Brazil Considers AI Deepfakes 'Excessive Risk' as Gender Violence Rises

**TL;DR:** Brazilian research center Internetlab calls for classifying non-consensual deepfakes as 'excessive risk' after surge in AI-generated sexual content targeting women and girls in schools.

## Key Points

- Multiple cases reported of teens creating AI-generated nude images of classmates in Rio de Janeiro, Bahia, and Mato Grosso states
- Internetlab research center published technical note recommending AI regulation to combat online gender violence
- 98% of deepfake videos online are sexually explicit, 99% target women; 464% increase in sexual deepfakes 2022-2023 [Security Hero research]
- Brazil's Supreme Court ruled Marco Civil da Internet's Article 19 partially unconstitutional, increasing platform content moderation obligations
- Brazil recorded 1,568 femicides in 2025, a 4.7% increase and highest since law was enacted
- Internetlab specifically calls out X's Grok AI as problematic example of embedded AI amplifying harmful content
- Report recommends 'safety by design' for platforms and digital literacy curriculum guidelines
- Concerns raised about AI-generated political violence content as Brazil approaches election season

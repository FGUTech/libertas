# EFF Issues EU Digital Fairness Act Recommendations Against Surveillance-Based Solutions

**TL;DR:** EFF advocates for EU Digital Fairness Act to ban dark patterns, tackle commercial surveillance, and strengthen user sovereignty rather than expanding platform control through age verification mandates.

## Key Points

- EFF released recommendations for EU's proposed Digital Fairness Act (DFA) targeting dark patterns and exploitative personalization
- Warns against surveillance-based solutions like age verification mandates that undermine fundamental rights
- Advocates banning dark patterns that manipulate users into sharing personal data they wouldn't otherwise disclose
- Calls for addressing commercial surveillance by banning pay-for-privacy schemes and supporting automated privacy signals
- Recommends strengthening user sovereignty by limiting post-sale restrictions and technical lock-ins
- Emphasizes tackling root causes of surveillance-based business models rather than expanding platform control
- Supports recognition of automated privacy signals from browsers and mobile OS to reject tracking

# Bukele Admin Escalates Financial Censorship Against El Faro, Freezes Personal Assets

**TL;DR:** El Salvador's government froze personal bank accounts and property of El Faro shareholders, escalating financial warfare against independent media that previously faced Pegasus surveillance.

## Key Points

- Salvadoran government placed 'preventative annotations' on El Faro shareholders' personal assets Feb-Apr 2026
- Measures target bank accounts and real estate of Trípode S.A. de C.V. partners under alleged tax debt claims
- Escalation follows years of harassment including Pegasus spyware surveillance and forced operational exile
- El Faro relocated administrative operations to Costa Rica in 2023 citing lack of judicial independence
- Asset freezes coincide with release of 'The Deal' documentary exposing alleged govt-gang pacts
- Timing also follows deputy editor's testimony before U.S. Congress Human Rights Commission
- Demonstrates evolution from digital surveillance to financial censorship targeting media ownership structures

# DOJ Pursues Rare Federal Felony Charges Against Journalists Covering Minnesota Protest

**TL;DR:** Committee to Protect Journalists and 16 civil society groups urge DOJ to drop federal charges, including felony conspiracy, against four journalists arrested while covering immigration enforcement protest.

## Key Points

- Four journalists face federal charges for covering immigration enforcement protest at Minnesota church
- Georgia Fort and Don Lemon arrested in January, Michael Walker Beute and Junn Bollmann indicted in February
- Charges include felony 'conspiring to deprive others of their civil rights' and FACE Act violations
- Federal felony charges against journalists described as 'extremely rare' by CPJ
- DOJ pursued grand jury indictment after federal magistrate and appeals court rejected initial arrests
- 17 civil society organizations led by Amnesty International calling for charges to be dropped
- Case represents potential escalation in federal prosecution of press covering protests

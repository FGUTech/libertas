# US-Israel War Strategy Targets Iran's Police Infrastructure to 'Make Iran Ungovernable'

**TL;DR:** Bellingcat investigation reveals systematic targeting of 80+ Iranian police stations and Basij paramilitary sites, part of strategy to degrade regime's 'repressive capacity' and enable civilian uprising.

## Key Points

- Bellingcat documented 80+ destroyed police stations and law enforcement facilities in first three weeks of US-Israel war against Iran
- Targets include 30 police stations, 29 command centers, and 9 Basij paramilitary sites used in protest crackdowns
- Israeli Foreign Ministry stated goal is providing 'brave people of Iran conditions to take their destiny into their own hands'
- Expert analysis confirms strategy aims to degrade regime's 'repressive capacity' and 'make Iran ungovernable'
- Police stations targeted beyond crowd control - broader goal to prevent regime maintaining internal control
- Verification conducted using satellite imagery, videos, and open source mapping data
- Civilian casualties documented with 1,700+ fatalities, including risks to detainees in targeted facilities
- Strategy extends beyond policing to industrial targets as comprehensive governance disruption

## Deep Dive

## Strategic Context

This investigation reveals a deliberate military strategy targeting Iran's domestic control apparatus rather than just external military capabilities. As Ali Vaez from International Crisis Group notes, the widespread targeting of police infrastructure aims to prevent the Islamic Republic from maintaining internal control, going beyond traditional crowd control forces to basic governance functions. The Israeli Foreign Ministry's explicit messaging about enabling Iranian civilians to 'take their destiny into their own hands' confirms this is intended to catalyze domestic uprising.

## Methodology and Verification

Bellingcat's investigation demonstrates sophisticated open source intelligence techniques, using Sentinel-2 satellite data when commercial providers restricted access, combined with geolocation of social media footage and open mapping data from Wikimapia, OpenStreetMap, and Google Maps. The methodology shows how OSINT can track systematic infrastructure degradation even under wartime information restrictions.

## Implications for Repressive Infrastructure

The targeting pattern reveals how modern warfare can strategically degrade state surveillance and control capabilities. However, experts note scale limitations - Iran's size means even complete destruction of policing capacity would require months or years to fully impact regime control. The civilian casualty concerns highlight the complexity of disrupting repressive infrastructure without harming those it oppresses, including detainees in targeted facilities.

## Freedom Tech Relevance

This case study provides real-world data on systematic degradation of state repressive capacity, offering insights for understanding how surveillance and control infrastructure can be disrupted. The documentation methodology also demonstrates advanced techniques for tracking infrastructure changes that could be applied to monitoring surveillance state expansion or contraction in other contexts.

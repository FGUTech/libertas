# Nigerian Cybercrime Law Used to Target 24+ Journalists and Activists

**TL;DR:** Nigeria's 2015 Cybercrimes Act has been weaponized against 24+ journalists and activists through overbroad 'cyberstalking' provisions that criminalize critical speech.

## Key Points

- More than 24 journalists, human rights defenders, and whistleblowers targeted since 2015
- Authorities using overbroad 'cyberstalking' provisions to punish critical online speech
- Law being weaponized to silence opposition voices and dissent
- Access Now organizing April 21 webinar on human rights implications
- Partners include Human Rights Journalists Network, SERAP, and Avocats Sans Frontiers Nigeria
- Focus on rights-based reporting frameworks for cybercrime cases
- Civil society pushing for reform efforts to address legislative overreach

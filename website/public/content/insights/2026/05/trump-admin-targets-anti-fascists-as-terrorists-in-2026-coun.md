# Trump Admin Targets 'Anti-Fascists' as Terrorists in 2026 Counterterrorism Strategy

**TL;DR:** Trump's new counterterrorism strategy formally classifies 'Violent Left-Wing Extremists, including Anti-Fascists' as major terror threats alongside ISIS and cartels, while omitting right-wing groups.

## Key Points

- Trump administration released '2026 Counterterrorism Strategy' authored by NSC official Sebastian Gorka
- Document defines three major terror threats: 'Legacy Islamist Terrorists,' 'Narcoterrorists,' and 'Violent Left-Wing Extremists'
- Anti-fascists labeled as 'anti-American, radically pro-transgender, and anarchist' terror threat
- Strategy makes no mention of right-wing extremist groups despite data showing they're responsible for majority of recent violent attacks
- NSPM-7 memo targets Americans with 'anti-American, anti-capitalist, anti-Christian' views as terror threats
- Document concludes with Trump quote: 'We Will Find You and We Will Kill You'
- Rep. Valerie Foushee calls it 'a plan on how they're going to attack people on the left'
- Formalizes shift of counterterror apparatus from foreign to domestic political targets

## Deep Dive

## Context and Implications

This represents a fundamental weaponization of the counterterrorism apparatus against domestic political dissent. By formally classifying anti-fascist activists as terrorists equivalent to ISIS and Al Qaeda, the Trump administration is laying legal and operational groundwork for deploying the full spectrum of counterterror tools—surveillance, financial sanctions, material support prosecutions—against political opponents [Source].

## Technical and Legal Framework

While Congress has not created domestic terrorism designations, the administration is leveraging National Security Presidential Memorandum 7 (NSPM-7) to target Americans based on ideological positions including 'anti-Americanism, anti-capitalism, and anti-Christianity' [Source]. This creates a framework where expressing certain political views could trigger counterterror investigations and surveillance.

## Threat Model Implications

For freedom tech builders and activists, this represents a clear escalation in state surveillance threats. The formal classification means activist networks, protest organizers, and anti-fascist researchers now face the same surveillance tools previously reserved for international terrorist organizations. The deliberate omission of right-wing extremist groups—despite data showing they're responsible for the majority of recent domestic attacks—reveals this as a politically targeted framework rather than evidence-based counterterrorism [Source].

## What This Means for Builders

The strategy's language around 'material support' prosecutions and financial sanctions suggests increased pressure on digital infrastructure supporting targeted groups. Privacy tools, secure communications, and financial sovereignty technologies become critical infrastructure for political dissidents. The administration's willingness to explicitly target ideological positions protected by the First Amendment indicates an environment where operational security and censorship-resistant tools are essential for basic political participation.

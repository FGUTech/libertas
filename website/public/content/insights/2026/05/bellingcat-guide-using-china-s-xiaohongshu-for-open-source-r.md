# Bellingcat Guide: Using China's Xiaohongshu for Open-Source Research Under Digital Oppression

**TL;DR:** Bellingcat releases comprehensive guide for investigating Chinese social platform Xiaohongshu, revealing how researchers circumvent AI surveillance and real-time censorship to track diaspora activism and expose wrongdoing.

## Key Points

- Bellingcat publishes detailed guide for open-source research on Xiaohongshu (RedNote), China's Instagram-like platform with 300M users
- Platform operates under strict AI surveillance and tens of thousands of 'internet police' monitoring in real-time
- Leaked 143-page internal document shows how censors respond to government directives blocking politically sensitive content
- Researchers use censorship patterns themselves as investigative leads, mapping detention camps through blanked areas on Baidu Maps
- Chinese diaspora communities use platform to coordinate responses to discrimination and organize grassroots activism
- App gained millions of Western users during TikTok ban fears, triggering AI-powered translation features
- Guide provides specific search methods for tracking consumer fraud, women's rights discourse, and transnational advocacy networks
- Platform's female-dominated userbase (70%) has developed algorithm-gaming techniques to create women-only discussion spaces

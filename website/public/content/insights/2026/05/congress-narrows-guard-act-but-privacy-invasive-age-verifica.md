# Congress Narrows GUARD Act, But Privacy-Invasive Age Verification Requirements Remain

**TL;DR:** Congress revised the GUARD Act to target AI companions rather than all AI systems, but still mandates identity-linked age verification and increases penalties to $250K per violation.

## Key Points

- Congress narrowed GUARD Act scope from all AI systems to focus on 'AI companions' - conversational systems simulating emotional interactions
- Bill still requires companies to implement age verification tied to users' real-world identities (financial records, government ID, etc.)
- Penalties increased from $100K to $250K per violation, enforceable by federal and state officials
- EFF warns requirements create privacy risks and barriers for millions without stable digital identity access
- Vague definition of 'AI companion' could still capture customer service chatbots and other conversational tools
- Over-restriction incentives: smaller developers may block minors entirely or disable features to avoid penalties
- Parents wanting teens to use AI companions for legitimate purposes (social practice, storytelling) still face verification hurdles
- EFF maintains opposition despite improvements, citing core speech, privacy, and security concerns

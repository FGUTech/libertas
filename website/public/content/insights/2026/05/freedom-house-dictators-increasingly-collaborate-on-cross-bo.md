# Freedom House: Dictators Increasingly Collaborate on Cross-Border Dissident Hunting

**TL;DR:** Freedom House reports growing collaboration between authoritarian regimes to track and suppress dissidents across borders, escalating the threat environment for activists worldwide.

## Key Points

- Freedom House documents increasing transnational repression campaigns by authoritarian governments
- Multiple dictatorial regimes are collaborating to hunt dissidents who fled abroad
- Cross-border cooperation enables surveillance and harassment of activists in third countries
- Trend represents escalation from isolated domestic repression to coordinated international operations
- Targets include journalists, human rights activists, and political dissidents living in exile
- Validates threat models driving development of censorship-resistant communications tools
- Demonstrates need for robust operational security and privacy-preserving technologies

## Deep Dive

## Context and Escalation

Freedom House's documentation of increased collaboration between authoritarian regimes represents a significant escalation in the threat landscape facing dissidents and activists [Source]. Unlike traditional domestic repression, these coordinated transnational efforts extend the reach of authoritarian surveillance and harassment across international borders, creating a global threat environment where geographic distance no longer provides safety.

## Technical and Operational Implications

This collaborative approach to repression fundamentally changes the adversary model that freedom tech tools must defend against. Instead of facing isolated state actors with limited reach, dissidents now confront coordinated networks of authoritarian governments sharing intelligence, surveillance capabilities, and operational resources. This reality validates the need for robust privacy-preserving technologies, secure communications protocols, and operational security practices that assume persistent, well-resourced adversaries.

## Impact on Freedom Tech Development

The trend documented by Freedom House underscores why censorship-resistant communications, anonymous networking tools, and privacy-preserving technologies are increasingly critical infrastructure for human rights work. As authoritarian cooperation intensifies, the technical requirements for protecting dissidents become more stringent, requiring tools that can withstand not just individual state surveillance apparatus but coordinated international efforts to compromise activist networks and identify vulnerable individuals.

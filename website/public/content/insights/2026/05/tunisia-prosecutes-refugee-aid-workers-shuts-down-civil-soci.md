# Tunisia Prosecutes Refugee Aid Workers, Shuts Down Civil Society Organizations

**TL;DR:** Tunisia sentenced two refugee aid workers to prison for humanitarian work, shut down their organization, and froze accounts as part of broader crackdown on civil society groups.

## Key Points

- Tunisian Council for Refugees shut down in May 2024, founder and program manager sentenced to 2 years prison (6 months suspended)
- Workers prosecuted under 1975 passport law for 'harboring' asylum seekers and facilitating illegal entry/exit
- Organization provided UNHCR-funded accommodation and assistance to registered refugees and asylum seekers
- Authorities froze bank accounts of organization and individuals, preventing operations and creating economic hardship
- Part of systematic crackdown - at least 9 civil society workers sentenced to prison since January 2026
- Tunisia suspended UNHCR asylum processing in June 2024, leaving asylum seekers in legal limbo
- Pattern demonstrates criminalization of humanitarian work and erosion of freedom of association
- Appeals trial scheduled for May 13, 2026 after both defendants and prosecution appealed initial sentences

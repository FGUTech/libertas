# Maldives Jails First Journalists Since 2008, Escalates Press Freedom Crackdown

**TL;DR:** Maldives authorities jailed two journalists for reporting on a gag order, marking the first criminal imprisonment of journalists since the country's 2008 democratic constitution, amid broader media suppression efforts.

## Key Points

- Two Adhadhu journalists sentenced to 10-15 days prison for reporting on court gag order regarding presidential misconduct documentary
- Authorities raided news outlet offices, seized laptops and hard drives, imposed travel bans on media executives
- Government charged outlet leaders under Islamic law for 'false accusation of adultery' (up to 19 months prison, 80 lashes)
- New Media and Broadcasting Regulation Bill (Sept 2025) grants government broad discretionary powers over media control
- Police attacked protesters supporting press freedom; 13 outlets staged coordinated media blackout in protest
- Secret trials conducted without lawyers despite criminal justice system backlog, indicating prioritized persecution
- Pattern matches digital-age censorship: device seizures, travel restrictions, and coordinated legal/technical suppression

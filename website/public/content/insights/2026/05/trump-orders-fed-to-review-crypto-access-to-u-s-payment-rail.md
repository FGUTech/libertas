# Trump Orders Fed to Review Crypto Access to U.S. Payment Rails

**TL;DR:** Trump signed executive order directing Federal Reserve to audit regulations blocking crypto firms from master accounts and payment rails within 3 months, potentially opening direct access to core U.S. financial infrastructure.

## Key Points

- Executive order 'Integrating Financial Technology Innovation into Regulatory Frameworks' targets Fed master account restrictions
- Regulators have 3 months to audit rules that 'unduly impede' fintech partnerships with federally regulated institutions
- Master accounts provide gateway to Fedwire and other payment rails handling high-value dollar settlement
- Crypto firms currently forced to pursue costly banking charters for direct payment access
- Kansas City Fed already approved limited account for Kraken's parent company Payward in March 2024
- Order questions whether regional Fed banks can approve accounts independently without Board of Governors approval
- Fed proposed 'skinny' master accounts in December - restricted access without interest or discount window features
- Traditional banking groups oppose expanded access, citing regulatory gaps between banks and non-bank entities
- 120-day deadline for Fed report transforms slow regulatory process into political pressure campaign

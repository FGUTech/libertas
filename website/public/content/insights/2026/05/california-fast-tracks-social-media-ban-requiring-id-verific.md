# California Fast-Tracks Social Media Ban Requiring ID Verification for All Users

**TL;DR:** California's A.B. 1709 would ban under-16s from social media and force all users to submit government IDs or biometrics for age verification, destroying online anonymity.

## Key Points

- A.B. 1709 bans anyone under 16 from accessing social media platforms in California
- All users regardless of age must submit government ID or biometric data for verification
- Bill passed Assembly Privacy and Judiciary Committees, heading to Appropriations then floor vote
- Creates centralized 'honeypots' of sensitive personal data vulnerable to breaches
- Eliminates online anonymity and chills First Amendment rights for all users
- Australia's similar ban shows overblocking, youth losing support networks, and widespread VPN workarounds
- Age verification systems disproportionately deny access to marginalized communities
- EFF warns other states likely to follow California's lead if bill passes

## Deep Dive

## Context and Urgency

California's A.B. 1709 represents one of the most aggressive attacks on online anonymity and free speech in recent U.S. legislative history. The bill has already cleared two major committee hurdles with near-unanimous support and is heading for final votes within days. Given California's outsized influence on national tech policy, this legislation could trigger a cascade of similar laws across other states, fundamentally reshaping internet access nationwide.

## Technical and Privacy Implications

The bill's age verification mandate would force platforms to implement identity checking systems that require government-issued IDs or biometric data from every user. This creates several critical vulnerabilities: centralized databases of sensitive personal information become high-value targets for attackers, biometric data is immutable when compromised, and third-party verification vendors introduce additional attack surfaces. [Source] notes repeated breaches of age verification companies, yet A.B. 1709 would massively expand this vulnerable ecosystem.

## Freedom Tech Threat Model

This legislation directly attacks core principles that freedom tech aims to protect. The destruction of online anonymity eliminates a fundamental layer of protection for activists, journalists, and vulnerable populations. The mandatory identity verification creates a comprehensive surveillance infrastructure that can be weaponized for political control. Australia's implementation demonstrates the pattern: overblocking legitimate users, driving adoption of circumvention tools like VPNs, and smaller platforms shutting down rather than complying with costly verification requirements.

## Strategic Considerations for Builders

The timing and scope of A.B. 1709 suggests a coordinated push toward identity-gated internet access. Freedom tech builders should prepare for similar legislation in other jurisdictions and consider how their tools can help users maintain anonymity and access in this emerging threat environment. The California precedent, if successful, will likely accelerate adoption of mandatory identity verification across platforms and jurisdictions.

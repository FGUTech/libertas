# EFF Challenges Court Secrecy in Wi-Fi Patent Case Affecting Billions

**TL;DR:** EFF forced disclosure of sealed court filings in Eastern District of Texas patent case involving Wi-Fi 6 technology, exposing widespread over-sealing that hides critical information about standard-essential patents from public view.

## Key Points

- Wilus Institute sued Samsung and HP over Wi-Fi 6 standard-essential patents (SEPs)
- Eastern District of Texas allowed wholesale sealing of filings without specific justification
- Key documents about patent ownership and FRAND licensing obligations completely hidden
- EFF intervention led to partial disclosure of redacted versions of three documents
- Court allowed 'boilerplate' protective orders instead of requiring document-by-document justification
- Pattern of excessive secrecy affects public understanding of essential technology licensing
- Northern District of California cited as example of proper transparency enforcement

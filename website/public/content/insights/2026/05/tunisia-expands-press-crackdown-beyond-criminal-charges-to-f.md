# Tunisia Expands Press Crackdown Beyond Criminal Charges to Financial Warfare

**TL;DR:** Tunisia's government is systematically dismantling independent media through financial crime charges, banking freezes, and dissolution proceedings against outlets like Inkyfada, beyond traditional prosecutions.

## Key Points

- Tunisian authorities expanding beyond Decree-Law 54 prosecutions to target media infrastructure directly
- Financial crime charges now used to extend journalist detention periods (Zghidi, Bsaies sentenced 3.5 years)
- Banking restrictions blocking funds for months without explanation at outlets like Al Khatt/Inkyfada
- Government pursuing dissolution of Al Khatt, the association behind investigative outlet Inkyfada
- Tunisian League for Human Rights suspended one month, signaling broader civil society crackdown
- Pattern shows coordinated strategy to dismantle conditions allowing independent journalism
- Demonstrates financial chokepoints as effective censorship tools beyond direct prosecution

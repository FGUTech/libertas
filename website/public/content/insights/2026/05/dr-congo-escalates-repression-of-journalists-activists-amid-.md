# DR Congo Escalates Repression of Journalists, Activists Amid Constitutional Crisis

**TL;DR:** Congo authorities increasingly detaining journalists, activists, and opposition members amid constitutional amendment tensions and M23 conflict, with intelligence agencies conducting enforced disappearances and arbitrary arrests.

## Key Points

- Human Rights Watch documents systematic harassment and arbitrary detention of journalists and activists in DR Congo between January-May 2026
- Repression escalating amid President Tshisekedi's proposed constitutional amendment to extend presidential term limits beyond two terms
- National Intelligence Agency and National Cyber Defense Council conducting enforced disappearances - 17 documented cases with 8 still detained
- Two journalists forced into hiding after intelligence agents searched their apartments following government criticism
- Military courts convicting activists of 'threatening state security' for organizing peaceful protests about basic services
- Police using tear gas and violence against peaceful protesters demonstrating against constitutional changes
- Authorities targeting individuals claiming they have ties to M23 armed group as pretext for broader crackdown
- Pattern shows government creating legal justifications for surveillance and detention of dissenting voices

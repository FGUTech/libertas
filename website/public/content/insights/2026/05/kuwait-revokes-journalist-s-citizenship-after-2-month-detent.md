# Kuwait Revokes Journalist's Citizenship After 2-Month Detention, Part of 71K Mass Revocations

**TL;DR:** Kuwait revoked citizenship of U.S.-Kuwaiti journalist Ahmed Shihab-Eldin after detaining him for journalism, part of 71,000 citizenship revocations since Oct 2024—4.6% of Kuwait's population.

## Key Points

- Ahmed Shihab-Eldin detained for 52 days on charges of spreading false information and harming national security
- Kuwait revoked citizenship of Shihab-Eldin and his two sisters days after his release
- Part of mass citizenship revocation campaign: 71,000 people since October 2024
- Revocations represent 4.6% of Kuwait's 1.5 million official population
- Journalist was held incommunicado in interrogation facilities before transfer to prison
- Charged for journalism activities including mobile phone use and expressing opinions
- CPJ calls it 'weaponizing nationality to punish reporting' with regional implications
- Demonstrates escalating state repression creating demand for censorship-resistant tools

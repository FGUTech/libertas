# EFF Calls for Privacy-First Approach to EU's Digital Fairness Act

**TL;DR:** EFF recommends EU's Digital Fairness Act prioritize banning surveillance-based business models and strengthening user sovereignty over more invasive regulatory measures like age verification.

## Key Points

- EFF released recommendations for EU's proposed Digital Fairness Act targeting dark patterns and exploitative personalization
- Advocates banning surveillance-based business models rather than expanding platform control over users
- Calls for explicit dark pattern prohibitions that manipulate user choices and data sharing
- Opposes pay-for-privacy schemes - users shouldn't trade data or pay extra to avoid tracking
- Supports automated privacy signals recognition by browsers and OS to reject tracking
- Addresses user sovereignty by limiting post-sale restrictions and technical lock-ins
- Proposes alternative to invasive age verification by limiting data collection by default

# Infrastructure Barriers Block AI Adoption Across Sub-Saharan Africa

**TL;DR:** Poor electricity access affecting 600M Africans and expensive internet connectivity costing 25% of monthly salary create fundamental barriers to AI adoption in Sub-Saharan Africa, widening global tech inequality.

## Key Points

- 600 million Africans lack electricity access as of 2025, with 85% in Sub-Saharan Africa [International Energy Agency]
- Mobile data costs 2-10% of average monthly income, making cloud-based AI applications unaffordable for most
- Daily power outages affect even countries with better infrastructure like South Africa and Côte d'Ivoire
- Rural areas have lowest global internet penetration rates despite 75% of world's mobile coverage expansion in 2024
- High latency and unstable connections make AI tools unusable for professional applications
- African governments announce AI strategies while basic infrastructure remains inadequate
- Educational inequality likely to worsen as most schools lack internet connectivity [UNESCO]
- Startups face competitive disadvantages with higher costs, VPN requirements, and limited tool access

# El Salvador Detains Anti-Corruption Lawyer for One Year, Triggers Mass Exodus of Rights Groups

**TL;DR:** Salvadoran authorities have held prominent anti-corruption lawyer Ruth López in sealed pretrial detention for one year, sparking wider crackdown that forced 140+ activists and journalists into exile.

## Key Points

- Ruth López, head of Cristosal's Anti-Corruption Unit, detained May 18, 2025 on charges changed from embezzlement to illicit enrichment
- Case remains under judicial seal with no public evidence presented after one year in pretrial detention
- López investigated Bukele administration corruption and challenged removal of attorney general and Constitutional Chamber magistrates
- Detention triggered broader crackdown: foreign agents law passed, constitutional lawyer Enrique Anaya arrested on money laundering charges
- At least 140 human rights defenders and journalists fled El Salvador between May-September 2025
- Cristosal suspended operations in El Salvador, relocated to Guatemala and Honduras citing choice between 'exile and prison'
- Association of Journalists of El Salvador and three other civil society organizations closed Salvadoran offices
- Pattern follows systematic dismantling of due process protections since 2022 state of emergency detaining 91,000+ people

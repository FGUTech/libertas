# Global Civil Society Adapts to AI-Driven Authoritarianism with New Tactics

**TL;DR:** Study of 10 global case studies reveals civil society using three key tactics against AI surveillance: co-opting tech for their purposes, countering digital repression, and innovating new resistance forms.

## Key Points

- International Resource for Impact and Storytelling (IRIS) commissioned 10 case studies across Global Majority countries on civil society responses to AI/algorithmic authoritarianism
- Three main tactical approaches identified: co-opting technology, countering surveillance, and innovating new forms of resistance
- Fogo Cruzado (Brazil) using AI to test anti-police violence messaging; Derechos Digitales campaigning against facial recognition in Brazil/Chile
- Hong Kong activists adapting to AI surveillance through humor, coded language, and ephemeral organizational structures
- Shift toward hyperlocal organizing to avoid national-level scrutiny while building transnational solidarity networks
- Flexibility and adaptability identified as essential strategies in fast-changing techno-authoritarian environment
- Study emphasizes need for decentralized, collaborative networks and flexible funding models
- Narrative work, technology adaptation, and political organizing viewed as inseparable elements of resistance

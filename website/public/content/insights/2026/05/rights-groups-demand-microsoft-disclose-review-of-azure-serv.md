# Rights Groups Demand Microsoft Disclose Review of Azure Services to Israeli Military Surveillance

**TL;DR:** Access Now and coalition demand Microsoft publicly release findings of investigation into Azure cloud services used by Israeli military units for mass surveillance and AI-assisted targeting of Palestinians.

## Key Points

- Microsoft completed internal review of Azure services to Israeli military but hasn't released findings despite public promise
- Unit 8200 used Azure to store 11,500+ terabytes of surveillance data including Palestinian phone intercepts
- Israeli Ministry of Defense used Microsoft AI services for translation, transcription of intercepted communications
- Multiple IDF units received Azure services including air force targeting databases and intelligence infrastructure
- Microsoft provided ~19,000 hours of engineering support to customize Azure for Israeli military use
- Services allegedly enabled mass surveillance and AI-assisted targeting systems in Gaza operations
- Microsoft reportedly suspended some Unit 8200 services in Sept 2024 but scope of ongoing services unclear
- Coalition cites potential corporate complicity in international law violations through surveillance infrastructure

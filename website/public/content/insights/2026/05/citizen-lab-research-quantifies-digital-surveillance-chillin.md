# Citizen Lab Research Quantifies Digital Surveillance Chilling Effects Post-Snowden

**TL;DR:** New research by Jon Penney documents measurable self-censorship behavior following Snowden revelations, showing how surveillance awareness directly reduces access to sensitive content.

## Key Points

- Jon Penney's book 'Chilling Effects' examines self-censorship in digital surveillance environment
- Post-Snowden research shows measurable drops in views of privacy-sensitive content
- Chilling effects described as 'deeply ingrained' psychological responses to perceived threats
- Both short-term and long-term behavioral changes documented following Five Eyes surveillance revelations
- Study demonstrates how surveillance knowledge deters exercise of democratic rights
- Research provides empirical evidence for theoretical concerns about surveillance impact
- Findings suggest surveillance programs achieve compliance through behavioral modification, not just data collection

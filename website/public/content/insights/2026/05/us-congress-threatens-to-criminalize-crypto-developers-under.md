# US Congress Threatens to Criminalize Crypto Developers, Undermine Freedom Tech

**TL;DR:** Critical developer protections in the BRCA could be stripped from market structure legislation, leaving non-custodial software creators facing criminal prosecution like Tornado Cash and Samourai Wallet developers.

## Key Points

- Section 604 (BRCA) of Senate draft would protect non-custodial software developers from money transmitter liability
- Without BRCA, developers face criminal prosecution under Section 1960 for publishing code
- Tornado Cash developers Roman Storm faces century+ in prison; Samourai's Rodriguez and Hill already convicted
- BRCA introduced by Senators Lummis (R-WY) and Wyden (D-OR) with bipartisan support
- Provision clarifies writing code ≠ transmitting money, doesn't weaken AML statutes
- CLARITY Act without BRCA protections would accelerate developer exodus to Singapore, Switzerland, UAE
- Threatens emerging AI agent economy requiring permissionless financial infrastructure
- Current prosecutions violate 'toolmaker principle' - holding developers liable for user actions

## Deep Dive

## Context and Stakes

While media attention focuses on stablecoin yield disputes, the real battleground in crypto market structure legislation is Section 604 - the Blockchain Regulatory Certainty Act (BRCA). This provision determines whether developers of non-custodial software can be prosecuted as money transmitters, making it the "load-bearing wall" of the entire policy framework [Source].

## Technical and Legal Implications

The BRCA creates a clear legal distinction between writing code and transmitting money - a line that should be obvious but has been deliberately blurred by federal prosecutors. Without these protections, developers face criminal liability under Section 1960, not just civil penalties. The Tornado Cash and Samourai Wallet prosecutions demonstrate this isn't theoretical - Roman Storm faces over 100 years in prison while Rodriguez and Hill are already serving sentences for publishing privacy-preserving code [Source].

## Economic and Strategic Consequences

A CLARITY Act without robust BRCA protections would trigger mass developer exodus to jurisdictions like Singapore, Switzerland, and UAE - the opposite of Congress's stated goal. The timing is particularly catastrophic as the "agentic economy" emerges, where AI agents require the permissionless, always-on financial rails that only blockchain infrastructure can provide. Traditional payment systems cannot support machine-to-machine transactions at the required scale and speed [Source].

## What This Means for Freedom Tech

The developer prosecutions represent "regulation by prosecution" that criminalizes the foundational tools of financial sovereignty and censorship resistance. If the BRCA is stripped, the rational response from every competent developer will be to leave the US, hollowing out the domestic capacity to build privacy-preserving and sovereignty-enhancing technologies.

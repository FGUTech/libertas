# Gulf States Launch Information Crackdown During Iran War, Target Journalists

**TL;DR:** Gulf governments are systematically suppressing information about Iran war attacks, detaining journalists, revoking citizenships, and blocking social media accounts to control what populations can see and share.

## Key Points

- Committee to Protect Journalists and 26 organizations condemn Gulf states' information crackdown during Iran war
- Kuwait detained US-Kuwaiti journalist Ahmed Shihab-Eldin for 52 days, then revoked his and his sisters' citizenship
- Bahrain sentenced photographer Sayed Baqer Al-Kamel to 10 years in prison
- UAE and Qatar reported brief detentions of media workers
- UAE's public prosecutor blocked access to several X accounts, including Saudi broadcaster Al Arabiya
- Governments targeting those filming, posting, or sharing footage of attacks in their cities
- Coalition calls for release of detained journalists and repeal of laws criminalizing legitimate expression

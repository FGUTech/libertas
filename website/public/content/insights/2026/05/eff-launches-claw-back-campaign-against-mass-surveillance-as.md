# EFF Launches 'Claw Back' Campaign Against Mass Surveillance as Section 702 Renewal Looms

**TL;DR:** EFF launches new fundraising campaign to fight mass surveillance, highlighting recent wins including Rayhunter open-source cell-site simulator detection tool and lawsuits against warrantless ALPR searches as Congress considers Section 702 renewal.

## Key Points

- EFF launches 'Claw Back' campaign to dismantle government surveillance infrastructure
- Released Rayhunter open-source tool for detecting cell-site simulators used to track activists and journalists
- Filed lawsuits challenging warrantless Automated License Plate Reader (ALPR) database searches
- Published 'Selling Safety' report exposing misleading claims by police surveillance technology vendors
- Congress currently considering renewal of Section 702 international mass spying program
- Privacy Badger browser extension continues protecting millions from tracking scripts and cookies
- Campaign references NSA whistleblower William Binney's warnings about 'turnkey totalitarian state'

## Deep Dive

## Context and Background

EFF's 'Claw Back' campaign emerges at a critical juncture as Congress debates renewing Section 702, the warrantless mass surveillance program affecting millions internationally. The campaign explicitly references NSA architect-turned-whistleblower William Binney's warnings about America becoming a 'turnkey totalitarian state' - a surveillance infrastructure ready for authoritarian abuse regardless of who holds power.

## Technical Tools and Legal Victories

EFF's recent technical contributions include Rayhunter, an open-source tool enabling activists and researchers to detect cell-site simulators (IMSI catchers) that law enforcement uses to track protesters and journalists. This complements their ongoing legal challenges against warrantless ALPR surveillance, which creates comprehensive tracking databases of citizens' movements and associations. Their 'Selling Safety' report helps journalists cut through police technology marketing claims, exposing the gap between vendor promises and actual capabilities.

## Strategic Implications for Freedom Tech

The campaign demonstrates a multi-pronged approach: developing open-source detection tools, challenging surveillance programs in court, and educating the public about surveillance overreach. EFF's Privacy Badger continues protecting millions from corporate tracking, while their legal work creates precedents limiting government surveillance powers. The timing suggests urgency around the Section 702 renewal - a program that enables warrantless collection of international communications that often includes U.S. persons' data.

## What This Means for Builders

EFF's approach shows the importance of combining technical tools with legal advocacy and public education. Their open-sourcing of Rayhunter provides a model for transparency in surveillance detection tools, while their lawsuits establish important precedents for challenging mass surveillance programs. The campaign highlights how established civil liberties organizations can amplify freedom tech impact through strategic litigation and policy advocacy.

# Why 'Anonymized' Data Isn't Really Anonymous: 15 Data Points ID 99.98% of People

**TL;DR:** Proton explains how anonymized data can still identify individuals with just 15 data points achieving 99.98% identification rates, and how AI is making re-identification faster and cheaper.

## Key Points

- Anonymization removes obvious identifiers (names, addresses) but leaves behavioral patterns that can re-identify users
- Research shows only 15 data points needed to identify 99.98% of people in datasets of millions [Nature study]
- AI and interconnected databases make re-identification increasingly feasible and automated
- GDPR sets high bar: data only truly anonymous if re-identification not 'reasonably possible'
- Pseudonymization (reversible coding) still considered personal data under GDPR, unlike true anonymization
- Companies use anonymized data without consent for analytics, advertising, and data broker sales
- Common techniques include data masking, generalization, swapping, perturbation, and synthetic data generation
- Biometric data especially difficult to anonymize due to unchangeable nature

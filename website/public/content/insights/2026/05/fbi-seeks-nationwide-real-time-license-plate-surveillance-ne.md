# FBI Seeks Nationwide Real-Time License Plate Surveillance Network

**TL;DR:** FBI published RFP for contractors to provide near real-time access to license plate readers covering 75% of US locations, creating nationwide vehicle tracking capability.

## Key Points

- FBI Directorate of Intelligence issued Request for Proposals for nationwide license plate reader access
- System must cover 75% of US locations with near real-time data access
- Contractors must provide both law enforcement and commercial LPR data through existing platforms
- Search capabilities include vehicle descriptions, time/date, and geo-location criteria
- System must offer search result notifications and query cameras across US territories
- Represents massive expansion of federal surveillance infrastructure beyond current capabilities
- Creates potential for tracking any vehicle's movements across the country in real-time

## Deep Dive

## Context and Implications

This RFP represents a significant escalation in federal surveillance capabilities, effectively creating a nationwide vehicle tracking system. Unlike previous localized license plate reader deployments, this would give the FBI near real-time access to vehicle movements across 75% of the United States through a unified platform.

## Technical Architecture

The system leverages existing commercial and law enforcement LPR networks, suggesting integration with vendors like Vigilant Solutions (now Motorola), Flock Safety, and municipal camera systems. The "near real-time" requirement indicates data streaming rather than batch processing, enabling live tracking capabilities previously limited to specific investigations.

## Threat Model Implications

This infrastructure creates comprehensive location tracking for anyone using vehicles, fundamentally altering the privacy landscape for transportation. The system enables retroactive tracking ("where was this vehicle last month") and prospective surveillance ("alert me when this vehicle moves"). Combined with other data sources, this creates detailed behavioral profiles and association networks.

## What Builders Should Consider

This development underscores the need for transportation privacy tools and alternative mobility solutions. Consider how license plate obfuscation, route randomization, or vehicle-sharing models might mitigate tracking. The normalization of this surveillance infrastructure will likely expand to other use cases beyond law enforcement.

# Afghan Journalist's Letters Spark Cross-Border Solidarity in China Despite Censorship

**TL;DR:** Afghan journalist Khadija Haidary's correspondence about Taliban oppression, translated and shared on Chinese social media, generated grassroots support that helped fund her family's resettlement in Canada.

## Key Points

- Afghan journalist Khadija Haidary's email correspondence about life under Taliban was translated by Chinese journalist Weilin Hong in October 2024
- Letters published on WeChat account 'Positive Links' went viral among Chinese readers despite tight media controls
- Chinese publisher offered book contract and advance royalties, enabling Haidary's family to resettle from Pakistan to Canada
- Over 10,000 copies sold by August 2025, with readers promoting book through reviews on Xiaohongshu, Weibo, and other platforms
- Cross-border solidarity emerged through individual acts rather than organized movement due to China's constrained civil society
- Chinese readers drew parallels between Afghan women's experiences and their own restrictions on feminist expression
- Support network utilized multiple platforms (WeChat, Telegram, Douban) to circumvent information controls
- Demonstrates how personal narratives can bypass official media filters and generate meaningful activism under authoritarian constraints

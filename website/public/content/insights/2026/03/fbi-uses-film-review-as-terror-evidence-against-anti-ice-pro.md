# FBI Uses Film Review as 'Terror Evidence' Against Anti-ICE Protesters

**TL;DR:** FBI seized a horror movie review from protester's home and prosecutors used it as evidence of 'ideologically driven intent' in terrorism case against alleged 'antifa cell.'

## Key Points

- FBI raided home of Daniel Sanchez Estrada, anti-ICE protester facing up to 20 years for 'transporting Antifa materials'
- Prosecutors introduced feminist film review about horror movies as evidence of terrorism in federal trial
- Review titled 'The Satanic Death-Cult Is Real' was seized alongside computers and weapons in itemized evidence list
- Case stems from July 4 protest at ICE's Prairieland Detention Facility where police officer was shot
- 18 people arrested in what prosecutors call first terrorism charges against alleged 'antifa cell'
- Estrada not accused of shooting but charged with conspiracy to conceal zines - First Amendment protected materials
- Trump administration designated 'antifa' as terrorist organization despite it being orientation, not formal group
- Pattern shows government using 'guilt by literature' tactics to criminalize possession of political writings

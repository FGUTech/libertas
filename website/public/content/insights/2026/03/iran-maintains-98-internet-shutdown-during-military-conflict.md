# Iran Maintains 98% Internet Shutdown During Military Conflict, Highlighting Need for D2C Satellite

**TL;DR:** Iran has blocked 98% of internet connectivity for 12+ days during U.S./Israel military attacks, prompting calls for Direct-to-Cell satellite deployment to bypass government shutdowns.

## Key Points

- Iran imposed near-total internet shutdown (98% connectivity drop) starting February 28, 2026 during military conflict
- Shutdown has lasted 12+ days, affecting millions during critical period when information access is vital
- Access Now condemns blackout as continuation of Iran's pattern of using shutdowns to control information during crises
- Shutdown blocks documentation of potential violations, hampers journalists and human rights defenders
- Information vacuum enables disinformation spread and manipulation by various parties
- Access Now highlights Direct-to-Cell (D2C) satellite technology as viable solution to bypass government network controls
- D2C can connect smartphones directly to satellites, circumventing censorship and shutdowns
- Civil society coalition calls for prioritized humanitarian deployment of D2C technology with human rights safeguards

## Deep Dive

## Context and Pattern

Iran's current internet shutdown represents an escalation of established authoritarian tactics during crisis periods. [Access Now] notes this follows earlier comprehensive nationwide blackouts this year, indicating systematic use of connectivity control as a governance tool. The 98% connectivity drop coinciding with military attacks demonstrates how governments weaponize network infrastructure against their own populations when external legitimacy is most threatened.

## Technical Solution: Direct-to-Cell Satellites

The prominence Access Now gives to Direct-to-Cell satellite technology signals a potential paradigm shift in censorship resistance. Unlike traditional satellite internet requiring specialized equipment, D2C connects ordinary smartphones directly to satellites, dramatically lowering adoption barriers. With satellite companies actively developing D2C capabilities and standards being established now, this represents a near-term technical solution to government shutdown capabilities.

## Strategic Implications for Freedom Tech

This incident illustrates the life-and-death stakes of connectivity during conflict. [Access Now] emphasizes shutdowns "put civilians at risk of death, injury, and illness" while blocking critical documentation of violations. The call for "humanitarian deployment" of D2C technology suggests civil society is actively pushing for freedom tech solutions to be prioritized in satellite development roadmaps.

## What This Means for Builders

The timing is critical - satellite companies are developing D2C now while standards are being set. The coalition of Access Now, WITNESS, and other civil society partners provides a policy advocacy framework that builders can align with. The emphasis on "human rights safeguards" suggests need for privacy-preserving implementations that resist both government and corporate surveillance.

# Iran Internet Blackout Isolates 15 Jailed Journalists During Regional Conflict

**TL;DR:** Iran imposed near-total internet blackout during regional escalation, cutting off at least 15 imprisoned journalists from outside contact, with three held at Evin prison in Israeli-ordered evacuation zone.

## Key Points

- Iran implemented near-total internet blackout and severe communication disruptions during ongoing regional conflict
- At least 15 journalists remain imprisoned in Iran, completely isolated from families and outside world
- Three journalists (Reza Valizadeh, Shinnosuke Kawashima, Mohammad Zare-Foumani) held at Evin prison in Tehran evacuation zone
- Evin Prison previously struck in Israeli attacks, killing 80+ people including prisoners
- Prison authorities reportedly abandoned facility, leaving special counter-terrorism forces in control
- Food shortages and deteriorating conditions reported in prison wards
- Internet blackouts being weaponized to suppress information flow and isolate detainees during conflict

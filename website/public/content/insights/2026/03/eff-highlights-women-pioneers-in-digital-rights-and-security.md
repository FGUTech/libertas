# EFF Highlights Women Pioneers in Digital Rights and Security Culture

**TL;DR:** EFF celebrates women who shaped digital rights, from NSA surveillance fighters to the Jane Collective's analog security practices that pioneered compartmentalization and secure deletion for activists.

## Key Points

- EFF honors women who advanced digital rights and freedom of expression on International Women's Day
- Cindy Cohn led 30-year fight establishing code as constitutionally protected speech and battling NSA mass surveillance
- Jane Collective (1960s-70s) developed proto-infosec practices: compartmentalized roles, minimal records, secure deletion
- Jane connected 11,000+ people with abortion access using single phone number and code names before Roe v. Wade
- When raided in 1972, Jane members destroyed index-card files rather than expose patient/helper networks
- Anna Politkovskaya exemplified journalist courage under state pressure, killed for investigative reporting
- Key principles highlighted: collect less data, compartmentalize what you collect, be ready for secure deletion

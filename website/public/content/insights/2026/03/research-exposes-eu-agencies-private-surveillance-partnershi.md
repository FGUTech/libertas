# Research Exposes EU Agencies' Private Surveillance Partnerships Targeting Migrants

**TL;DR:** Access Now research reveals 450+ companies pitch surveillance tech to EU agencies via exclusive industry events, building biometric databases and AI systems that bypass data protection laws.

## Key Points

- Access Now mapped 450+ companies attending EU agency industry events (eu-LISA, Europol, Frontex) pitching surveillance products
- Companies presented 600+ products including biometric systems, airborne surveillance, and AI analytics for migration control
- eu-LISA manages databases collecting migrant biometric data while bypassing EU data protection laws
- Major participants include Airbus, Fujitsu, Idemia, Thales, plus consultancies like Accenture and Deloitte
- Events create exclusive opportunities for private sector to influence EU migration policy and spending
- Research shows 'open door' policy including companies from countries implicated in war crimes
- Surveillance infrastructure increasingly targets migrants, activists, and solidarity groups
- Both Frontex and Europol received significant budget increases for next decade with expanded mandates

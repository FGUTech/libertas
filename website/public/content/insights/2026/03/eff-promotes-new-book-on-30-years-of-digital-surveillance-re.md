# EFF Promotes New Book on 30 Years of Digital Surveillance Resistance

**TL;DR:** Electronic Frontier Foundation's Executive Director Cindy Cohn published 'Privacy's Defender,' chronicling three decades of legal battles against government surveillance from the Crypto Wars to NSA dragnet spying.

## Key Points

- EFF Executive Director Cindy Cohn published 'Privacy's Defender: My Thirty-Year Fight Against Digital Surveillance' (MIT Press)
- Book covers legal battles from 1990s Crypto Wars, NSA internet spying revelations, and FBI gag order resistance
- EFF founded in 1990 after Steve Jackson Games case established email deserves same protection as telephone calls
- Organization has 30,000+ members and 35+ years defending digital civil liberties
- EFF promotes 'Privacy First' approach to addressing online harms through limiting corporate surveillance
- Bernstein v. DOJ case mentioned as precedent-setting encryption publication rights case
- Content serves as recruitment tool following Daily Show interview appearance

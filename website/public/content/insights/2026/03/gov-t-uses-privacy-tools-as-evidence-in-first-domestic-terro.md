# Gov't Uses Privacy Tools as Evidence in First Domestic Terror Case Against Antifa Cell

**TL;DR:** Federal prosecutors worked with far-right think tank researcher to craft terror charges against Texas activists, citing use of Signal and security practices as evidence of antifa membership.

## Key Points

- First-ever domestic terrorism case against alleged 'antifa cell' involves nine defendants facing life sentences for ICE protest
- Kyle Shideler from Center for Security Policy helped DOJ prosecutors write terror indictment language
- Government expert witness cited Signal use, 'black bloc' clothing, and 'security culture' as evidence of antifa practices
- Center for Security Policy is classified as hate group by SPLC, founded by 'Islamophobic conspiracy theorist' Frank Gaffney
- Defendants used fireworks at solidarity demonstration outside Prairieland ICE detention center; officer was shot
- Shideler acknowledged he doesn't use academic methods, peer review, or verify open-source materials authenticity
- Several cooperating defendants testified they didn't consider themselves antifa members
- Case demonstrates how privacy tools and operational security practices are being criminalized as terrorism evidence

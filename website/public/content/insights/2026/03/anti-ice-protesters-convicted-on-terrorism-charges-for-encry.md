# Anti-ICE Protesters Convicted on Terrorism Charges for Encrypted Comms and Black Clothing

**TL;DR:** Eight protesters convicted of terrorism charges for wearing black clothing and using Signal messaging during ICE facility demonstration, marking first federal material support charges against alleged antifa members.

## Key Points

- Federal jury convicted 8 of 9 defendants on terrorism charges for July 4, 2025 protest at ICE detention facility
- Prosecutors used encrypted Signal messages as evidence and cited 'black bloc' clothing as material support for terrorism
- One protester convicted of attempted murder for shooting police officer, faces life in prison
- First federal use of material support for terrorism charges against alleged antifa members
- Government witness described Signal app usage as 'typical of antifa' tactics alongside black clothing
- Convictions could encourage more terrorism prosecutions of activists using encrypted communications
- Trump administration celebrating verdict as part of systematic crackdown on left-wing groups
- Defendants face up to 15 years for material support charges, in addition to riot and explosives counts

# Survey: 78% of US Parents Concerned About Children's Digital Privacy

**TL;DR:** Proton survey reveals widespread parental anxiety about children's online privacy, with 58% fearing identity theft before age 18 and concerns about data collection by apps and schools.

## Key Points

- 78% of US parents concerned about child's online privacy, with 56% 'very concerned'
- 58% of parents worry about identity theft occurring before child reaches 18
- 76% of children already own smartphones/tablets, with age 10 being biggest jump in device ownership
- 44% of children have email addresses, 42% have social media accounts
- Top platforms used by children: TikTok (63%), Facebook (53%), Instagram (50%), Snapchat (45%)
- 65% of parents worried educational platforms collect excessive data about children
- One-third of parents discovered apps collected more child data than expected in past year
- Parents fear digital footprints could affect future employment (55%), education (56%), and safety (70%)

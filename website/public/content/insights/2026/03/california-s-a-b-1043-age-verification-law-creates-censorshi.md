# California's A.B. 1043 Age Verification Law Creates Censorship Trap for Open-Source Developers

**TL;DR:** EFF warns California's A.B. 1043, effective 2027, will force age bracketing that leads to over-censorship and particularly harms open-source developers through compliance burdens.

## Key Points

- A.B. 1043 requires operating systems and apps to create age bracketing systems for all users
- Users must provide birth date/age data to access software and applications
- Age bracket data treated as 'actual knowledge' triggering liability under other laws
- Creates incentive for developers to exclude minors entirely to avoid legal risk
- Broad language covers virtually any digital device that runs software
- Small and open-source developers face disproportionate compliance burdens
- Law entrenches dominance of major tech companies who can afford compliance
- EFF advocates for comprehensive privacy laws instead of age verification mandates

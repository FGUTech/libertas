# Building Self-Hosted Communities: Taiwan's Response to Platform Censorship

**TL;DR:** Shin Yang built Lezismore, an independent self-hosted platform for Taiwan's sexual minorities, after mainstream platforms like Facebook drove LGBTQ+ communities to self-censor or face removal.

## Key Points

- Lezismore is a self-hosted, open-source community platform for sexual minorities in Taiwan
- Built in response to 2013 shutdown of local bulletin boards as Facebook dominated Taiwan's digital ecosystem
- Platform emphasizes anonymity, minimal data collection, and behavior-based accountability
- Designed as alternative to mainstream platforms where sexual expression faces reporting and flagging
- Uses community-elected governance model inspired by traditional bulletin board systems (BBS)
- Creator advocates for 'ecospace' infrastructure enabling survival and growth over just 'safe spaces'
- Addresses intra-community censorship where LGBTQ+ groups self-police to gain mainstream approval

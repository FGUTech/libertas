# EFF Backs Minnesota Lawmaker Against Internet Age Verification Mandate

**TL;DR:** EFF supports Rep. Leigh Finke's opposition to Minnesota's HF1434 age verification bill, which would require government ID/biometric verification for broad categories of online speech beyond explicit content.

## Key Points

- Minnesota HF1434 mandates age verification via government IDs or biometrics for websites with speech deemed 'harmful to minors'
- Bill's definition extends beyond explicit content to include lawful speech about sexual orientation, health, and gender identity
- Rep. Leigh Finke (transgender lawmaker) testified that such laws are already blocking LGBTQ+ youth from educational resources
- Supreme Court's Free Speech Coalition v. Paxton ruling enabled Texas-style age verification, supported by anti-LGBTQ groups
- Minnesota bill goes further than Texas law by expanding 'harmful to minors' definition to broader categories of protected speech
- EFF argues young people have First Amendment rights to access information about sexuality and reproductive health
- Age verification creates surveillance infrastructure that undermines privacy and anonymous access for all users
- Critics mischaracterized Finke's testimony, proving her point about using these laws to silence dissenting voices

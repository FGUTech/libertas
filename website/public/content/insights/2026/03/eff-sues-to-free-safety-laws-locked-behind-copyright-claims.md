# EFF Sues to Free Safety Laws Locked Behind Copyright Claims

**TL;DR:** EFF filed suit against CPSC to release legally binding safety codes that private organizations claim copyright over even after incorporation into federal law.

## Key Points

- EFF partnering with Harvard Cyberlaw Clinic to sue Consumer Product Safety Council (CPSC)
- Client Public.Resource.Org seeking children's product safety codes via FOIA requests
- CPSC refusing to release codes citing private organization copyright claims
- Private standards groups retain copyright control over laws after government adoption
- Fifth Circuit ruled safety codes lose copyright when incorporated into law
- D.C. Circuit previously held that sharing incorporated standards constitutes fair use
- Case challenges fundamental principle that copyright can restrict access to binding law
- Part of broader 'Freeing the Law' campaign for government transparency

# EFF Sues to Free Safety Laws from Copyright Restrictions

**TL;DR:** EFF filed lawsuit against CPSC demanding public access to legally binding safety codes that private organizations claim copyright over even after incorporation into federal law.

## Key Points

- EFF and Harvard Cyberlaw Clinic sued Consumer Product Safety Commission over FOIA denial
- CPSC refusing to release children's product safety codes citing copyright claims
- Private standards organizations claim copyright retention after codes become federal law
- Public.Resource.Org (Carl Malamud's nonprofit) seeking access to make laws publicly available
- Circuit courts split on whether standards lose copyright when incorporated into law
- Fifth Circuit ruled codes lose copyright protection once adopted as law
- D.C. Circuit found publishing standards constitutes fair use even if copyright remains
- Case challenges fundamental principle that private entities can gatekeep public law access

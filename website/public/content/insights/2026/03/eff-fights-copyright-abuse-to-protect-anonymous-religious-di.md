# EFF Fights Copyright Abuse to Protect Anonymous Religious Dissent

**TL;DR:** Watch Tower is using DMCA subpoenas to unmask anonymous Jehovah's Witnesses who created research tools exposing church document changes and failed prophecies.

## Key Points

- Watch Tower Bible and Tract Society issued DMCA subpoenas to Google and Cloudflare targeting anonymous researcher 'J. Doe'
- Doe created JWS Library website with research tools analyzing Jehovah's Witnesses historical documents and policy changes
- Research revealed failed prophecies, erasure of leadership scandals, and increased calls for obedience and donations
- Jehovah's Witnesses punish dissent through 'disfellowshipping' - cutting members off from family and community
- EFF argues Doe's research constitutes fair use and First Amendment-protected anonymous speech
- Case demonstrates copyright law being weaponized to silence religious criticism and unmask anonymous speakers
- Southern District of New York courts will decide whether to quash the subpoenas

# Iran's Internet Connectivity Drops 99% Amid Strikes and Shutdowns

**TL;DR:** Iran has cut internet connectivity by 99%, with air strikes causing additional outages and circumvention tools reportedly failing to provide reliable access.

## Key Points

- Iran has reduced internet connectivity by approximately 99 percent
- Air strikes have likely caused additional infrastructure damage beyond deliberate shutdowns
- Traditional circumvention workarounds are reportedly no longer effective
- Shutdown represents one of the most severe internet restrictions globally
- Physical infrastructure damage complicates restoration efforts
- Demonstrates Iran's willingness to accept economic costs of near-total disconnection
- Highlights vulnerability of centralized internet infrastructure to state control

## Deep Dive

## Context and Scale

Iran's 99% connectivity reduction represents one of the most severe internet shutdowns documented globally, surpassing even previous Iranian restrictions during protests. The combination of deliberate government action and physical infrastructure damage from air strikes creates a compound crisis that goes beyond typical censorship scenarios.

## Technical and Infrastructure Implications

The failure of traditional circumvention tools suggests Iran has implemented more sophisticated blocking mechanisms or that physical infrastructure damage has eliminated the underlying connectivity these tools depend on. This highlights the fundamental vulnerability of internet freedom tools when states are willing to accept the economic and social costs of near-total disconnection.

## What This Means for Freedom Tech

The situation demonstrates the limits of software-based circumvention when facing determined state actors with control over physical infrastructure. It underscores the need for alternative communication networks that don't rely on traditional internet infrastructure, such as mesh networking, satellite connectivity, or other decentralized communication systems that can operate independently of state-controlled infrastructure.

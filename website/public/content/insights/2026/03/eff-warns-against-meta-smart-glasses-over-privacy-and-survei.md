# EFF Warns Against Meta Smart Glasses Over Privacy and Surveillance Concerns

**TL;DR:** EFF raises alarm about Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses, highlighting that footage is reviewed by human contractors, used for AI training, and enables covert surveillance of bystanders.

## Key Points

- Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses automatically upload footage to Meta AI app by default
- Human contractors review and annotate sensitive footage including nudity and bathroom visits for AI training
- Glasses designed to look like regular eyewear, making recording nearly invisible to bystanders
- Small recording indicator light can be disabled with cheap hardware hacks
- Audio conversations with Meta AI are saved by default and must be manually deleted
- Footage may also reach Apple/Google servers via phone storage, potentially accessible to law enforcement
- Google partnering with Warby Parker and Apple reportedly developing competing products
- EFF warns constant recording creates new privacy risks beyond traditional phone cameras

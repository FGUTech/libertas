# NYC Ends $4M Palantir Hospital Contract After Activist Pressure

**TL;DR:** New York City's public hospital system will not renew its $4 million Palantir contract in October after sustained activist pressure against the surveillance company's presence in healthcare.

## Key Points

- NYC Health + Hospitals paid Palantir ~$4 million since 2023 for data analysis services including automated scanning of patient health notes
- Contract enabled Palantir to help with Medicaid billing efficiency through patient data analysis
- Activists protested the deal citing Palantir's role in lethal airstrikes, mass surveillance, and ICE deportation raids
- CEO Mitchell Katz announced at March 16 city council meeting that contract expires in October and won't be renewed
- Hospital system claims 'absolute firewall' prevented patient data sharing with Palantir's government clients like ICE
- Data analysis functions will be brought in-house after contract expiration
- American Friends Service Committee led campaign that successfully pressured the hospital system
- Activists vow to continue targeting Palantir contracts with other institutions globally

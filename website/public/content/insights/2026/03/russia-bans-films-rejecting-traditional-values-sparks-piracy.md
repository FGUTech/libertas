# Russia Bans Films Rejecting 'Traditional Values,' Sparks Piracy Rise

**TL;DR:** Russia's new law lets the Ministry of Culture ban films deemed contrary to 'traditional values,' blocking Hollywood and indie films while driving increased piracy adoption.

## Key Points

- New Russian law effective March 1, 2026 bans films 'discrediting traditional spiritual and moral values'
- Ministry of Culture can refuse distribution licenses for foreign and domestic films, streaming services affected
- Banned films include 'Nuremberg' (Nazi trials drama), 'Splitsville' (relationships), and Jafar Panahi's Palme d'Or winner
- First documentary ban issued to 'Parents Are Here' about autism support
- Authorities target content promoting 'non-traditional relationships' or 'rejecting family values'
- Putin eased copyright penalties in 2024, illegal streaming reportedly rising as alternative
- Pattern extends beyond news media to entertainment censorship, limiting cultural information access

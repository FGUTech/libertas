# EFF Sues Medicare for Records on AI System Evaluating Patient Care Requests

**TL;DR:** EFF filed FOIA lawsuit against CMS seeking transparency on WISeR program that uses AI to evaluate Medicare prior authorizations, affecting 6.4M seniors with potential bias and denial risks.

## Key Points

- EFF filed FOIA lawsuit against Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services over WISeR AI program
- WISeR uses AI algorithms to assess prior authorization requests for Medicare beneficiaries
- Program launched in January 2025 in six states, potentially affecting 6.4 million seniors
- Vendors compensated up to 20% of savings from denied healthcare services, creating perverse incentives
- No public information about AI training data, bias testing, or algorithmic safeguards
- Healthcare providers report delays in care approval and administrative strain since launch
- CMS has not responded to EFF's earlier FOIA request for vendor agreements and testing records
- Lawsuit seeks immediate processing of records about accuracy tests, bias audits, and vendor oversight

# Iranian Women Footballers Granted Asylum After Protest, Facing State Surveillance

**TL;DR:** Australia granted asylum to 5 Iranian women's football players and 1 official who protested by refusing to sing national anthem, amid reports of Iranian security following them and ongoing internet shutdowns in Iran.

## Key Points

- Five Iranian women's football players and one official granted asylum by Australia after seeking protection following March 8 match
- Players protested by refusing to sing national anthem during March 2 match against South Korea
- Iran's state television labeled the squad 'wartime traitors' for their silent protest
- Players reported being followed by Iranian security and handlers since arriving in Australia
- Protest echoed Iranian men's team actions at 2022 Qatar World Cup during 'Woman, Life, Freedom' uprising
- Timeline coincides with conflict in Iran starting February 28 and ongoing internet shutdowns
- Human Rights Watch confirms Iranian government deploys political 'minders' to monitor national team athletes
- FIFPRO criticizes FIFA and AFC for inadequate athlete protection protocols

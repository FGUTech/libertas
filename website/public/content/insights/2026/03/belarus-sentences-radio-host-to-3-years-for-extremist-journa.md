# Belarus Sentences Radio Host to 3 Years for 'Extremist' Journalism Activities

**TL;DR:** Belarusian court sentenced radio journalist Aleh Khamenka to 3 years in prison for 'extremist activities' related to his work with Poland-based Radio Racyja, marking the third journalist conviction in under a week.

## Key Points

- Radio host Aleh Khamenka sentenced to 3 years imprisonment plus $6,800 fine by Minsk court on March 4, 2026
- Charges stem from work with Poland-based Radio Racyja, which KGB labeled 'extremist' in January 2024
- Khamenka detained June 17, 2025 after apartment search; trial began February 19, 2026
- Third journalist conviction in Belarus within one week, indicating sustained press crackdown
- Belarus amended criminal code in 2021 to enable extremism prosecutions against at least 50 media outlets
- Radio Racyja's website blocked in Belarus since September 2021
- 26 journalists currently imprisoned in Belarus according to Committee to Protect Journalists
- Case demonstrates legal framework being used to criminalize independent media operations

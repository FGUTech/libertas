# Sen. Wyden Warns of Classified Section 702 Surveillance Abuse

**TL;DR:** Sen. Ron Wyden revealed existence of classified Section 702 surveillance abuse that affects Americans' privacy rights, warning Congress is debating reauthorization without full information.

## Key Points

- Sen. Wyden disclosed existence of classified Section 702 surveillance abuse during Senate floor speech
- Abuse 'directly affects the privacy rights of Americans' according to Wyden
- Multiple administrations have refused declassification requests for years
- Wyden warns Congress is debating Section 702 reauthorization 'with insufficient information'
- Speech delivered during protest of Joshua Rudd's NSA nomination over constitutional concerns
- Wyden predicts Americans will be 'stunned' when abuse is eventually declassified
- Section 702 reauthorization deadline approaching, creating legislative pressure point

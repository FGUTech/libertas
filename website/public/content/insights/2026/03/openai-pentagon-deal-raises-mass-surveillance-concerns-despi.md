# OpenAI Pentagon Deal Raises Mass Surveillance Concerns Despite CEO Denials

**TL;DR:** OpenAI's new Pentagon contract permits bulk data collection from users, with experts warning about AI-amplified surveillance risks despite CEO Sam Altman's claims otherwise.

## Key Points

- OpenAI signed Pentagon contract allowing bulk user data collection [Citizen Lab]
- CEO Sam Altman denies deal enables mass surveillance capabilities
- Senior researcher Wolfie Christl and other experts express skepticism about Altman's claims
- Government can already purchase personal data for warrantless surveillance operations
- AI systems amplify surveillance risks through 'errors, bias, and lack of accountability'
- Represents potential policy shift in OpenAI's previous surveillance red lines
- Opaque AI processing makes harm from surveillance data more difficult to detect

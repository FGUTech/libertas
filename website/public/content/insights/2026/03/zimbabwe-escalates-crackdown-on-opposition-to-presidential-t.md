# Zimbabwe Escalates Crackdown on Opposition to Presidential Term Extension

**TL;DR:** Zimbabwean authorities are using violence, intimidation, and surveillance against civil society groups opposing a constitutional amendment that would extend President Mnangagwa's rule until 2030.

## Key Points

- Zimbabwe gazetted Constitutional Amendment No 3 on February 16, 2026, extending presidential/parliamentary terms from 5 to 7 years
- Armed men in balaclavas raided National Constitutional Assembly offices, beating leader Lovemore Madhuku who filed court challenge
- Police shut down Constitutional Defenders Forum meetings using Maintenance of Peace and Order Act requirements
- Armed police besieged opposition lawyer Tendai Biti's offices, with reports of assaults and death threats
- SAPES Trust offices suffered suspected arson attack hours before hosting civil society dialogue on constitutional crisis
- Police require 5-7 days advance notice for meetings, selectively enforced to prevent opposition organizing
- Pattern demonstrates state using violence and legal tools to suppress democratic opposition to power consolidation
- Creates conditions where secure communications and coordination tools become essential for civil society survival

# Pakistan Uses Colonial-Era Law to Suppress Women's Rights March, Detains 60+ Activists

**TL;DR:** Pakistani authorities detained over 60 activists and journalists attempting to hold the annual Aurat March on International Women's Day, using Section 144 restrictions and confiscating phones.

## Key Points

- Police detained 35+ women activists and 27+ men during annual Aurat March in Islamabad on March 8
- Authorities used Section 144 of Criminal Procedure Code (colonial-era law) to ban public assembly
- Police confiscated mobile phones and held detainees for nearly 10 hours in cramped conditions
- Journalists covering the event were also detained and threatened with arrest
- Detainees forced to sign affidavits pledging not to participate in similar events again
- Section 144 was imposed citing unrelated Iran-Israel conflict concerns
- Represents escalation in state crackdown on women's rights activism in Pakistan
- Constitutional lawyers cite violation of Articles 16 (assembly) and 19 (expression) rights

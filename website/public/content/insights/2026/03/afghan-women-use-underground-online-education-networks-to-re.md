# Afghan Women Use Underground Online Education Networks to Resist Taliban Restrictions

**TL;DR:** First-person account reveals how Afghan women are creating underground online education networks and digital resistance methods to circumvent Taliban's education bans since 2021.

## Key Points

- Personal testimony from Fareshtah, an Afghan law student whose education was cut short by Taliban takeover in August 2021
- Student prevented from defending thesis at gunpoint when universities banned women in December 2022
- Underground resistance includes online schools for girls, women's reading groups, and virtual courses
- Taliban has repeatedly reversed promises to allow women's education, forcing shift to covert online methods
- Author now teaches in clandestine online schools and university courses for women denied formal education
- Demonstrates pattern of digital education networks emerging under authoritarian censorship
- Shows evolution from formal to informal resistance education infrastructure over 3+ years

# Angola Enacts Sweeping Civil Society Restrictions, Expands State Control Over Media

**TL;DR:** Angola's president signed legislation granting government broad powers to monitor, suspend, and restrict NGOs under vague 'security threat' provisions, part of expanding crackdown on civil society and media.

## Key Points

- President João Lourenço signed new NGO law allowing government to authorize, monitor, suspend, and financially restrict civil society organizations
- Law grants powers under vague 'security threats' language without clear legal safeguards or independent oversight
- Part of broader 2024-2026 legislative crackdown including vandalism law criminalizing peaceful protest participants
- National Security Law empowers security forces to supervise media outlets and interrupt broadcasts without judicial oversight
- Draft 'online disinformation' legislation introduced January 2026 threatens further restrictions on digital expression
- Human Rights Watch and other organizations warn laws violate international freedom of association and expression conventions
- Creates regulatory environment that increases demand for censorship-resistant communications and organizing tools

# EFF's 2026 Foilies Expose Government Transparency Failures

**TL;DR:** EFF and MuckRock's annual "Foilies" highlight worst government transparency violations, including Texas hiding Abbott-Musk communications and DHS abandoning text message archiving.

## Key Points

- EFF and MuckRock released 2026 Foilies recognizing worst government transparency failures during Sunshine Week
- Texas Gov. Greg Abbott's office released 1,400 pages of Abbott-Musk communications with 1,200 pages fully redacted
- Department of Homeland Security stopped automatically archiving official text messages, now relies on manual screenshots
- Vancouver, B.C. imposed $10 CAD fee for non-personal public records requests despite tax funding
- FCC delayed student FOIA requests for 7+ months after previously responding within days/weeks
- Awards highlight systematic erosion of transparency rights across multiple jurisdictions
- Positive example: Northern Nevada Public Health delivers restaurant inspection records within 20 minutes
- Federal Records Act requires agencies to retain all official communications, making DHS policy potentially illegal

# Ugandan Journalist Disappeared After Discussing Censorship Law Ruling

**TL;DR:** Ugandan security forces forcibly disappeared journalist Mukose Arnold Anthony after he discussed a court ruling that struck down sections of the Computer Misuse Act used to target journalists and activists.

## Key Points

- Mukose Arnold Anthony, program director of Alternative Digitalk, was forcibly taken by plainclothes officers outside Metro FM 90.8 on March 22
- Disappearance occurred after live broadcast discussing Constitutional Court ruling that nullified sections of Computer Misuse Act
- Joint Anti-Terrorism Task Force operatives searched his home and seized electronic devices including laptop, modem, and smart watches
- Police confirmed they are holding Mukose in custody, investigation ongoing
- Pattern matches Uganda's documented enforced disappearance tactics, including high-profile cases like opposition bodyguard Eddie Mutwe
- Alternative Digitalk was previously raided in 2022 under Computer Misuse Act provisions later annulled by court
- Case demonstrates ongoing repression despite legal victories against censorship laws

## Deep Dive

## Context and Pattern

This incident represents a troubling escalation in Uganda's crackdown on media freedom, occurring just days after a significant legal victory against censorship. The Constitutional Court's March 17 ruling struck down key sections of the Computer Misuse Act that authorities have weaponized against journalists and activists. Mukose's disappearance immediately after discussing this ruling sends a clear message that legal victories may not translate to practical press freedom.

## Operational Methods

The operation demonstrates sophisticated surveillance state tactics: plainclothes operatives using unmarked 'drone' vehicles (Toyota Noah minivans) for rapid extraction, followed by systematic digital asset seizure. The Joint Anti-Terrorism Task Force's involvement indicates these are not isolated incidents but part of coordinated state repression. Human Rights Watch documents over 100 similar cases by this unit, with some victims dying under torture.

## Technical and Threat Model Implications

The seizure of Mukose's electronic devices—laptop, modem, smart watches—highlights the digital forensics component of state repression. Journalists and activists in Uganda face a threat model where discussing even favorable court rulings can trigger immediate detention and device seizure. This creates a chilling effect that extends beyond individual cases to the broader media ecosystem.

## What Builders Should Consider

This case underscores why censorship-resistant tools remain critical even after legal victories. The gap between judicial rulings and executive enforcement creates dangerous windows where activists remain vulnerable. Tools for secure communications, device encryption, and operational security become essential infrastructure for media freedom in environments where discussing constitutional rights can trigger state violence.

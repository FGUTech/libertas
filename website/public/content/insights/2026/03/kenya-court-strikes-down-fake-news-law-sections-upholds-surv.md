# Kenya Court Strikes Down 'Fake News' Law Sections, Upholds Surveillance Powers

**TL;DR:** Kenya's Court of Appeal nullified cybercrime law sections criminalizing 'false news' publication but upheld cyber harassment and surveillance provisions affecting digital rights.

## Key Points

- Kenya's Court of Appeal struck down sections 22-23 of 2018 Computer Misuse and Cybercrimes Act criminalizing 'false, misleading, or fictitious data'
- Court called these provisions 'so broad, wide, untargeted, akin to unguided missiles' likely to catch innocent citizens
- Bloggers Association of Kenya (BAKE) successfully challenged 26 provisions arguing they violated constitutional free expression protections
- Court upheld Section 27 on 'cyber harassment' carrying up to 10 years prison for communications that 'detrimentally affect' recipients
- Surveillance and search/seizure powers remain intact despite privacy concerns raised by petitioners
- Victory removes major tool used by powerful individuals to suppress unfavorable reporting and commentary [CPJ]
- Al Shifaa Media journalist Peter Maseke Mwita still facing cyber harassment charges over WhatsApp message, court date in April

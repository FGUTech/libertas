# Flock ALPR Cameras Used for Traffic Enforcement Despite Company Claims Otherwise

**TL;DR:** Georgia police used Flock Safety's license plate readers to ticket a motorcyclist for holding a phone, contradicting the company's public claims that ALPRs aren't used for traffic violations.

## Key Points

- Georgia State Patrol issued traffic ticket based on Flock camera footage showing motorcyclist holding phone
- Ticket explicitly stated 'CAPTURED ON FLOCK CAMERA 31 MM 1 HOLDING PHONE IN LEFT HAND'
- Flock Safety previously claimed their ALPRs are 'not used to enforce traffic violations'
- Company now lists six traffic enforcement partners on their website, contradicting earlier statements
- Public records show speed enforcement cameras connected to Flock's ALPR network
- EFF warns this demonstrates classic surveillance mission creep pattern
- Case highlights gap between vendor marketing claims and actual deployment practices

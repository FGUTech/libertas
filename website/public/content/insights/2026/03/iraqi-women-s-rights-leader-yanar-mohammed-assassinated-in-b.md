# Iraqi Women's Rights Leader Yanar Mohammed Assassinated in Baghdad

**TL;DR:** Prominent Iraqi women's rights activist Yanar Mohammed was gunned down by motorcycle assassins in Baghdad, highlighting the deadly environment for freedom advocates in Iraq.

## Key Points

- Yanar Mohammed, 65, founder of Organization of Women's Freedom in Iraq, killed by two men on motorcycles March 2, 2026
- Mohammed built network of women's shelters serving 1,300+ women fleeing violence since 2003
- Published feminist newspaper Al-Mousawat and trained women's rights activists
- No group claimed responsibility; latest in decade-long pattern of activist assassinations in Iraq
- Killing follows backlash against women's rights groups opposing abusive Personal Status Law amendments
- Iraqi authorities have failed to deliver justice for assassinated activists, enabling culture of impunity
- Attack represents direct targeting of feminist movement and freedom advocacy infrastructure

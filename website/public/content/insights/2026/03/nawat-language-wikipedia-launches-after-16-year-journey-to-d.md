# Nawat Language Wikipedia Launches After 16-Year Journey to Digital Sovereignty

**TL;DR:** El Salvador's critically endangered Nawat language launched its own Wikipedia in 2026 after community mobilization by Indigenous speakers seeking digital sovereignty and knowledge preservation.

## Key Points

- Nawat Wikipedia ('Wikiamachti') officially recognized by Wikimedia Foundation in January 2026 after 16 years in incubator status
- Only 1,135 native Nawat speakers remain according to 2024 Salvadoran Census, language classified as 'critically endangered' by UNESCO
- Historical persecution during 1932 La Matanza forced speakers to abandon language to escape state-led repression
- Community collective 'Timumachtikan Nawat' led revival through workshops, translation training, and digital sovereignty advocacy
- Project now features 1,000+ articles including documentation of local knowledge holders like dictionary author Nantzin Sixta Pérez García
- Global participation includes learners from Morocco taking virtual classes to contribute to the platform
- Represents community-controlled knowledge platform allowing autonomous documentation of locally significant information
- Joins other Indigenous Latin American language Wikipedias including Quechua, Aymara, Guaraní, and Náhuatl

# Internet Shutdowns Hit Record High: 313 Documented in 2025 Across 52 Countries

**TL;DR:** Access Now documents 313 internet shutdowns in 52 countries during 2025, increasingly used to conceal war crimes and human rights abuses, with growing attacks on satellite internet systems.

## Key Points

- 313 internet shutdowns documented in 52 countries in 2025, surpassing 2024's record of 304
- Seven new countries joined offender list, bringing total to 100 countries affected since 2016
- Conflict remains leading trigger with 125 shutdowns across 14 countries to conceal war crimes
- Sharp increase in targeting Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellite systems: 14 shutdowns vs 4 in 2024
- 70 shutdowns coincided with grave human rights abuses including murder, torture, and war crimes
- Myanmar leads with 95 shutdowns, followed by India (65), Pakistan (20), and Iran (11)
- Russia imposed hundreds to thousands of disruptions, officially justified as drone countermeasures
- 75 shutdowns persisted from 2025 into 2026, indicating trend toward permanent communications blocks
- Growing resistance: ICC recognizing shutdowns as linked to crimes against humanity, election observers condemning practice

## Deep Dive

## Context and Escalation

The 2025 data represents a 9-year trend of escalating digital authoritarianism, with shutdowns becoming institutionalized as a tool of state control. The persistence of 75 shutdowns into 2026 signals a shift from tactical disruptions to strategic communication isolation. The expansion to 100 countries since tracking began demonstrates the global normalization of this tactic across democratic and autocratic governments alike.

## Technical and Operational Implications

The targeting of Low Earth Orbit satellite systems represents a significant escalation in shutdown sophistication. Previously, satellite internet served as a critical backup during terrestrial network disruptions, but authorities are now actively jamming or blocking these systems. This creates a more complete communication blackout and raises the technical bar for circumvention tools. The Russia case study reveals state capacity for massive-scale disruptions across entire regions, suggesting advanced deep packet inspection and network segmentation capabilities.

## Threat Model Evolution

The systematic use of shutdowns to conceal atrocities indicates a mature threat model where communication disruption is integrated with kinetic operations. The Myanmar air strike example shows authorities using communication loss as an early warning system for attacks, weaponizing the shutdown itself. Cross-border shutdowns in occupied territories demonstrate how digital infrastructure becomes part of territorial control strategies.

## What Builders Should Consider

The satellite targeting trend requires mesh networking and alternative communication pathways that don't rely on traditional internet infrastructure. The scale and persistence of shutdowns demand tools designed for extended offline operation, not just temporary disruptions. The documentation of shutdown evidence by activists highlights the critical need for secure, delay-tolerant networks that can store and forward information once connectivity returns. Builders should prioritize solutions that work in completely air-gapped environments and can automatically synchronize when brief connection windows appear.

# Major Publishers Block Internet Archive Over AI Concerns, Threatening Historical Web Record

**TL;DR:** The New York Times and other publishers are blocking Internet Archive crawlers due to AI scraping fears, potentially erasing decades of preserved web history that researchers and journalists depend on.

## Key Points

- New York Times began blocking Internet Archive crawlers using technical measures beyond robots.txt
- The Guardian and other major publishers reportedly following similar blocking practices
- Publishers cite concerns about AI companies scraping news content as primary motivation
- Internet Archive preserves over 1 trillion web pages used daily by journalists, researchers, and courts
- Wikipedia alone links to 2.6 million news articles preserved at the Archive across 249 languages
- EFF argues archiving qualifies as fair use, similar to search engine protections
- Blocking threatens historical record that often shows original versions of edited or removed articles
- Risk of irreversible loss of three decades of preserved web content

# Iran Targets Amazon Data Centers in First Military Strike on Commercial Cloud Infrastructure

**TL;DR:** Iran's Revolutionary Guard struck Amazon data centers in UAE and Bahrain, marking first military attack on commercial cloud infrastructure due to its dual military-civilian use supporting Pentagon operations.

## Key Points

- Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps launched drone strikes on Amazon data centers in UAE and Bahrain during U.S.-Israeli conflict
- First documented military attack specifically targeting private sector data centers in warfare
- Iran cited these facilities' role in 'supporting enemy military and intelligence activities' as justification
- Amazon, Google, Microsoft provide cloud services to Pentagon through programs like Joint Warfighter Cloud Capability
- Legal experts say dual-use cloud infrastructure becomes legitimate military target when supporting warfare
- Iranian media listed dozens of regional facilities including Google and Microsoft as 'Enemy Technology Infrastructure'
- Attacks caused structural damage, power disruption, and regional service outages across Middle East
- Blurred civilian-military boundaries in cloud computing complicate international law and targeting decisions
- Pentagon's AI integration under Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth expands potential military targets across U.S.
- Commercial cloud providers often cannot distinguish between civilian and military data processing

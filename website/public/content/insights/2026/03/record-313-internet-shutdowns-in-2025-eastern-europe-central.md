# Record 313 Internet Shutdowns in 2025, Eastern Europe/Central Asia Hit 29 Times

**TL;DR:** Access Now reports 2025 saw the highest number of internet shutdowns ever recorded globally (313 across 52 countries), with Eastern Europe and Central Asia experiencing 29 shutdowns across 7 countries.

## Key Points

- 313 internet shutdowns documented globally in 2025, affecting 52 countries with no shutdown-free days
- Eastern Europe and Central Asia saw 29 shutdowns across Belarus, Kyrgyzstan, Russia, Turkmenistan, and Ukraine
- Russia led regional shutdowns, often justified as drone attack countermeasures with limited evidence of necessity
- Belarus imposed platform blocks and throttling around January 2025 presidential election
- Civil society fighting back through strategic litigation in Kazakhstan challenging shutdown legality
- Hundreds to thousands of additional disruptions reported across Russia since May 2025, still being verified
- Shutdowns increasingly normalized as tools of political control during elections and security tensions
- Pattern spreading across region, risking normalization of shutdowns as default government response

## Deep Dive

## Context and Scale

The 2025 internet shutdown data represents a disturbing escalation in digital authoritarianism globally. The fact that not a single day passed without at least one shutdown somewhere in the world indicates systematic deployment of these tactics. In Eastern Europe and Central Asia specifically, the 29 documented shutdowns across seven countries show how quickly this tool of control is proliferating across the region.

## Technical and Tactical Evolution

Russia's approach is particularly concerning, with authorities implementing shutdowns across multiple regions simultaneously, often under the pretext of drone threats. This represents a tactical evolution where governments are becoming more sophisticated in justifying widespread connectivity disruptions. The additional reports of "hundreds to thousands" of disruptions in Russia since May 2025 suggest either a massive escalation or improved detection capabilities by civil society monitors.

## Resistance Patterns

The strategic litigation efforts in Kazakhstan represent an important development in legal resistance to shutdowns. Civil society groups are pursuing dual approaches: challenging government shutdown orders directly while also targeting telecommunications operators for compliance. This strategy recognizes that effective resistance requires pressure on both state and private sector actors in the shutdown infrastructure.

## Implications for Freedom Tech

This systematic deployment of shutdowns across the region creates clear demand signals for censorship-resistant infrastructure. The normalization of shutdowns as political tools suggests that traditional internet infrastructure cannot be relied upon during critical periods. The geographic clustering in Eastern Europe and Central Asia makes this region a priority testing ground for mesh networks, satellite connectivity solutions, and other resilient communication technologies.

# 2025 Broke Records: 313 Internet Shutdowns Across 52 Countries, Zero Days Without Blackouts

**TL;DR:** Access Now reports 2025 had 313 internet shutdowns in 52 countries with no single day free of blackouts. Latin America saw Venezuela continue pattern plus Panama's first shutdowns during social security protests.

## Key Points

- 2025 recorded 313 internet shutdowns across 52 countries - a new global record [Access Now]
- Every single day of 2025 had at least one internet shutdown occurring somewhere globally
- Latin America/Caribbean: 5 shutdowns in 2 countries - Venezuela (repeat offender) and Panama (first-time)
- Panama blocked internet/mobile in Bocas del Toro province for 2 weeks during social security reform protests
- 40% of shutdowns (125) were conflict-related across 14 countries
- 70 shutdowns coincided with serious human rights violations including killings, torture, war crimes
- 100 countries total have experienced connectivity shutdowns since Access Now monitoring began in 2016
- Shutdowns used to influence elections, silence populations, and enable rights violations with impunity

## Deep Dive

## Context and Escalation

The 2025 data represents a concerning escalation in government use of internet shutdowns as a tool of control and repression. Access Now's #KeepItOn coalition has been tracking this phenomenon since 2016, and the fact that 2025 achieved the grim milestone of zero shutdown-free days indicates systematic normalization of this tactic across diverse political contexts globally.

## Regional Patterns and New Actors

While Latin America historically sees fewer shutdowns than regions like Sub-Saharan Africa or South Asia, the emergence of Panama as a new actor is significant. Panama's two-week shutdown of internet and mobile services in Bocas del Toro province during social security protests demonstrates how governments facing domestic unrest increasingly view connectivity disruption as an acceptable response tool. Venezuela's continued presence reinforces its status as a regional outlier in digital authoritarianism.

## Technical and Strategic Implications

The data reveals that 40% of shutdowns were conflict-related, while 70 coincided with serious human rights violations - indicating shutdowns serve dual purposes of operational security for state actors and information control. This pattern suggests governments are becoming more sophisticated about timing shutdowns to maximize their effectiveness in suppressing documentation and coordination of resistance activities.

## What This Means for Freedom Tech

The universality of shutdowns (365 days globally) creates a compelling use case for censorship-resistant communication tools, mesh networking solutions, and offline-capable systems. The regional expansion into previously 'free' areas like Panama suggests builders should prepare for deployment scenarios beyond traditionally authoritarian contexts.

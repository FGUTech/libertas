# US Treasury Acknowledges Legitimate Uses of Crypto Mixers in Congressional Report

**TL;DR:** U.S. Treasury told Congress that crypto mixers serve legitimate privacy purposes, marking a significant policy shift while proposing new 'hold law' for suspicious digital assets.

## Key Points

- Treasury submitted 32-page report to Congress acknowledging legitimate privacy uses for crypto mixers
- Major shift from previous stance that led to Tornado Cash sanctions in 2022
- Report cites lawful uses including protecting personal wealth info and business payment privacy
- Proposes 'hold law' giving financial institutions safe harbor to freeze suspicious digital assets
- Treasury data shows $37.4B in bridge withdrawals since 2020, with $1.6B from mixing services
- DPRK-linked actors stole $2.8B in digital assets Jan 2024-Sep 2025, heavily using mixers
- No new restrictions recommended for non-custodial mixers; FinCEN 2023 recordkeeping rule not finalized
- Comes after Tornado Cash sanctions lifted in March 2025 following federal appeals court ruling

## Deep Dive

## Policy Reversal Context

This Treasury report represents a dramatic recalibration of U.S. government policy toward privacy-enhancing cryptocurrency tools. The acknowledgment that "lawful users of digital assets may leverage mixers to enable financial privacy" directly contradicts the aggressive enforcement actions of 2022-2023, including the sanctioning of Tornado Cash and designation of international mixers as money-laundering hubs [Source]. The shift follows successful legal challenges, including the March 2025 lifting of Tornado Cash sanctions after a federal appeals court found OFAC exceeded its authority.

## Technical and Regulatory Framework

The report's distinction between custodial and non-custodial mixers is crucial for builders. Custodial services must register with FinCEN as money services businesses and can provide identity data, while non-custodial mixers face no new recommended restrictions [Source]. Treasury's analysis reveals sophisticated criminal usage patterns: threat actors combine mixers with stablecoin swaps and cross-chain bridges, with over $900 million concentrated in a single bridge linked to DPRK activity. The proposed 'hold law' would create temporary safe harbor for financial institutions to freeze suspicious assets during investigations.

## Implications for Freedom Tech

This policy shift validates the fundamental premise that financial privacy is a legitimate need, not inherently criminal. However, the proposed expansion of Section 311 of the USA PATRIOT Act to cover digital asset transfers outside correspondent banking relationships could create new surveillance authorities [Source]. The Treasury's approach of targeting actors based on their roles and risks rather than blanket restrictions suggests a more nuanced regulatory framework may emerge, though industry groups like Galaxy Research warn of potential surveillance expansion similar to the original Patriot Act.

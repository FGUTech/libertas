# Japan's Team Mirai Uses Open-Source AI Tools to Reshape Democratic Participation

**TL;DR:** Japan's Team Mirai party won 11 seats using open-source AI tools for direct voter engagement, policy formation, and transparency - demonstrating scalable digital democracy infrastructure.

## Key Points

- Team Mirai won nearly 4 million votes and 11 seats in Japan's House of Representatives using AI-driven voter engagement
- Party's AI Interviewer facilitated 8,000+ hours of direct voter policy input since 2025
- Action Board app captured 100,000 organizer actions daily during election runup
- All software tools (Gikai assembly app, Mirumae transparency tool) are open-source and freely available
- Party formed policy platform from 38,000+ online questions and 6,000+ policy suggestions via AI Policy app
- Secured agreement with Japan's ruling LDP party to adopt Team Mirai's transparency tools
- Founded by software engineer Takahiro Anno, party positions itself as 'utility infrastructure' for democracy
- Demonstrates technology enhancing rather than undermining democratic processes through direct participation

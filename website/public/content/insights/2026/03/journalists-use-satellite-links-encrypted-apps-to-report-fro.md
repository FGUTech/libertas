# Journalists Use Satellite Links, Encrypted Apps to Report from Internet-Cut Iran

**TL;DR:** After Iran cut internet access following strikes on officials, journalists are using satellite connections and encrypted messaging to circumvent state censorship and continue reporting.

## Key Points

- Iran severed internet access after strikes killed senior Iranian officials
- Journalists inside Iran are using satellite links to maintain connectivity
- Encrypted messaging apps being deployed to bypass communications blocks
- Smuggled footage serving as additional workaround for information flow
- Demonstrates real-world effectiveness of censorship-resistant tools during crisis
- Pattern shows satellite connectivity as critical backup during internet shutdowns
- Highlights importance of encrypted communications under authoritarian response

## Deep Dive

## Context

Iran's response to military strikes with complete internet shutdowns represents a predictable authoritarian playbook - cut communications to control narrative and prevent coordination. This creates a natural experiment for testing censorship-resistant technologies under real repression conditions.

## Technical Deployment

The combination of satellite links and encrypted messaging apps represents a multi-layered approach to maintaining information flow. Satellite connectivity provides the fundamental internet access that domestic ISPs can't block, while encrypted apps ensure message security even if traffic is intercepted. The mention of "smuggled footage" suggests physical transport of data across borders when digital channels fail.

## Implications for Freedom Tech

This deployment validates the threat model that freedom tech builders have been designing for - complete state-level internet cutoffs during crisis periods. The fact that journalists can maintain reporting capabilities suggests these tools are mature enough for operational use under extreme conditions.

## What Builders Should Consider

The success of this approach highlights the critical importance of hybrid connectivity solutions that don't rely on domestic infrastructure. The combination of satellite uplinks with encrypted messaging creates redundant pathways that are difficult for states to completely sever without extreme technical measures.

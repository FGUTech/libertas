# EFF Confirms Government Using Ad Surveillance Networks to Access User Data

**TL;DR:** EFF confirms government agencies are tapping into advertising surveillance infrastructure to collect citizen data, including CBP using targeted ads to track locations.

## Key Points

- EFF newsletter confirms government agencies accessing data through advertising surveillance networks
- Customs and Border Protection (CBP) using targeted advertising systems for location tracking
- EFF Staff Attorney Lena Cohen discusses how ad targeting reveals locations to federal law enforcement
- Newsletter covers related privacy victories including Tenth Circuit ruling on Fourth Amendment protections for protesters
- Highlights concerning Department of Defense AI usage conflicts with privacy protections
- EFF launching podcast format to reach broader audience on digital rights issues
- Pattern demonstrates intersection of commercial surveillance and government data collection

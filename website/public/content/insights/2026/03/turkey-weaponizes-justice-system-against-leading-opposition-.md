# Turkey Weaponizes Justice System Against Leading Opposition Politician

**TL;DR:** Istanbul mayor and presidential candidate Ekrem İmamoğlu faces mass corruption trial March 9, after year in detention as Turkey systematically targets opposition party through courts.

## Key Points

- Istanbul Mayor Ekrem İmamoğlu to stand trial March 9, 2026 as central defendant in mass corruption case with 407 defendants
- İmamoğlu detained for one year after being selected as Republican People's Party (CHP) presidential candidate
- Faces up to 1929 years in prison if convicted on all charges including organized crime, corruption, and espionage
- Human Rights Watch documents timeline showing politically motivated targeting coinciding with İmamoğlu's political rise
- Evidence relies heavily on 15 'secret witnesses' and testimonies from co-defendants seeking reduced sentences
- Systematic crackdown includes detention/removal of CHP mayors in Istanbul districts and major cities like Adana, Antalya
- Independent TV channels covering İmamoğlu's arrest face fines, broadcast bans, and journalist detentions
- CHP won 37.8% in March 2024 elections, surpassing Erdoğan's AKP (35.5%) for first time in 22 years

# EFF Warns of Privacy Risks in Meta's Smart Glasses Surge

**TL;DR:** EFF highlights serious privacy concerns as Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses go mainstream, including human review of footage and covert recording capabilities that threaten bystander privacy.

## Key Points

- Meta's Ray-Ban smart glasses have moved from niche to mainstream adoption
- Footage automatically uploads to Meta AI app and undergoes human review for AI training
- Swedish investigation found Meta contractors reviewing sensitive footage including nudity
- Glasses designed to be indistinguishable from regular eyewear, making covert recording easy
- Small recording indicator light can be disabled with cheap modifications
- Constant recording creates new privacy risks like capturing passwords and bank details
- Google partnering with Warby Parker, Apple rumored to enter market
- Bystanders have limited recourse when recorded without consent

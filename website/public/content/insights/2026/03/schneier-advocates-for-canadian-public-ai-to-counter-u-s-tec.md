# Schneier Advocates for Canadian Public AI to Counter U.S. Tech Dependence

**TL;DR:** Security expert Bruce Schneier argues Canada should build public AI infrastructure instead of relying on U.S. companies like OpenAI, citing Switzerland's Apertus model as proof of concept.

## Key Points

- Canada investing $2B in Sovereign AI Compute Strategy with OpenAI aggressively pursuing contracts
- OpenAI failed to alert authorities about B.C. shooter's gun-violence ChatGPT interactions until after incident
- OpenAI for Countries initiative operates 'in coordination with U.S. government'
- Switzerland's Apertus demonstrates viable public AI model built by academic consortium
- Apertus cost fraction of corporate AI budgets while providing adequate performance for most applications
- Public AI enables democratic oversight of critical decisions around bias, access, and sensitive applications
- Canada has existing AI research infrastructure through Vector Institute, Mila, and CIFAR
- Proposal shifts focus from attracting private capital to building fully open public AI model

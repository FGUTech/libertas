# EFF: Age Verification Laws Enable Government Control Over Online Speech

**TL;DR:** EFF defends Minnesota lawmaker who warned that age verification mandates are being used to restrict LGBTQ+ youth access to information, demonstrating broader government control over online speech.

## Key Points

- Rep. Leigh Finke testified against Minnesota HF1434, a broad age verification bill requiring ID/biometric verification
- Bill's 'harmful to minors' definition sweeps in lawful speech about sexual orientation, health, and gender identity
- Finke faced media attacks for noting these laws already block LGBTQ+ youth from accessing educational content
- Supreme Court's FSC v. Paxton case enables states to mandate age verification with broad definitions
- Anti-LGBTQ groups filed amicus briefs supporting Texas age verification law, revealing underlying agenda
- Minnesota bill goes further than Texas law by expanding 'harmful' content to include sex education and health info
- Age verification creates surveillance infrastructure while restricting First Amendment rights of all users
- EFF argues young people have constitutional right to access information about bodies, sexuality, and health

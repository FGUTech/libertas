# Supreme Court Limits ISP Copyright Liability, Protects General-Purpose Internet Services

**TL;DR:** Supreme Court reversed billion-dollar verdict against Cox Communications, ruling ISPs can't be liable for customer copyright infringement unless they actively induce it or provide services tailored for infringement.

## Key Points

- Supreme Court reversed Fourth Circuit decision upholding $1B verdict against Cox Communications
- Court established contributory liability requires either active inducement or providing service tailored for infringement
- Mere knowledge that some customers may infringe is insufficient for liability
- Cox demonstrated good faith efforts including warnings, suspensions, and account terminations
- General-purpose internet connectivity has substantial lawful uses and can't be treated as contributory infringement
- Decision protects ISPs from pressure to over-police user activity or withdraw services
- Ruling prevents expansion of secondary liability that could chill innovation and smaller tech companies
- EFF's amicus brief arguments about patent law framework were adopted by the Court

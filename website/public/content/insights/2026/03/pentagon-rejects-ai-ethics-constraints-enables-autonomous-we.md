# Pentagon Rejects AI Ethics Constraints, Enables Autonomous Weapons Development

**TL;DR:** US Defense Department rejected Anthropic's ethical red lines against fully autonomous weapons and mass surveillance, signing with OpenAI instead under new 'any lawful use' requirements.

## Key Points

- Pentagon on Feb 27 rejected Anthropic's refusal to enable fully autonomous weapons and mass surveillance of US citizens
- Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth designated Anthropic a 'supply chain risk' for maintaining ethical boundaries
- DOD signed deal with OpenAI under new requirement accepting 'any lawful use' of AI products
- January AI memo removed requirement for 'appropriate levels of human judgment' in autonomous weapons
- Policy shift prioritizes 'Military AI Dominance' over human oversight safeguards
- Autonomous weapons pose risks to civilian protection and accountability in armed conflicts
- Built-in algorithmic biases could disproportionately harm marginalized populations
- UN Convention on Conventional Weapons meeting this week to discuss autonomous weapons treaty

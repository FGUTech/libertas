# Federal Jury Convicts Protesters on Terrorism Charges, Cites Signal Use as Evidence

**TL;DR:** Eight anti-ICE protesters convicted on terrorism charges in landmark case where prosecutors cited Signal messaging and 'black bloc' clothing as evidence of terrorist material support.

## Key Points

- Federal jury convicted 8 of 9 protesters on terrorism charges related to July 4, 2025 ICE facility demonstration in Texas
- Benjamin Song convicted of attempted murder for shooting police officer with modified AR-15
- Prosecutors successfully argued wearing black clothing constituted 'material support for terrorism'
- Signal encrypted messages obtained by government used as evidence against defendants
- Right-wing think tank researcher testified that Signal use and black bloc tactics are 'typical of antifa'
- First federal material support for terrorism charges against alleged antifa members
- Case establishes precedent where privacy tools and clothing choices can support terrorism charges
- Convictions could encourage similar prosecutions as Trump administration targets left-wing groups

## Deep Dive

## Legal Precedent for Criminalizing Privacy Tools

This case represents a watershed moment in the criminalization of digital privacy tools. Federal prosecutors successfully convinced a jury that using Signal for protest coordination constituted evidence of terrorist material support. The government's ability to obtain Signal messages suggests either device seizure, informants, or potential technical compromise - though the article doesn't specify the acquisition method.

## Technical and Operational Security Implications

The case demonstrates how operational security failures can expose entire networks. Despite using Signal's encryption, the messages became prosecution evidence, highlighting the importance of device security, key management, and compartmentalization. The prosecution's characterization of encrypted messaging as inherently suspicious creates a chilling precedent for privacy tool users across the political spectrum.

## Broader Threat Model Evolution

This prosecution signals a fundamental shift in how the government approaches digital privacy tools in protest contexts. By successfully arguing that encrypted communications and anonymizing clothing constitute terrorist material support, prosecutors have created a playbook that could be applied to any group using privacy-preserving technologies. The FBI's designation of 'antifa' as a major domestic terror threat, combined with these convictions, suggests expanded surveillance and prosecution of privacy tool users.

## What This Means for Freedom Tech Builders

Builders must now assume that simply providing privacy tools could expose users to terrorism charges if those tools are used in any context later deemed criminal. This case underscores the need for stronger technical protections, better user education about operational security, and potentially anonymous development and distribution models to protect both builders and users from legal targeting.

# UK Advances Social Media Ban with Ministerial Powers to Restrict Internet Access for Under-18s

**TL;DR:** UK Parliament is progressing legislation that would give ministers sweeping powers to ban social media for under-18s and restrict VPN use without accountability mechanisms or evidence requirements.

## Key Points

- House of Commons defeated Lords' amendment to ban under-16s from social media by 307 to 173 votes
- Commons proposed alternative giving Secretary of State power to restrict internet access for under-18s
- New powers include ability to limit VPN use and restrict 'addictive features' without evidence requirements
- Authority shifts from independent regulator Ofcom to political ministers with no accountability mechanisms
- Broad definitions could target LGBTQ+ content and sex education behind age gates
- 107 Labour MPs abstained, showing party split on the issue
- Amendment modifies existing Online Safety Act enforcement framework

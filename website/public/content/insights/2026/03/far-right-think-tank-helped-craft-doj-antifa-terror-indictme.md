# Far-Right Think Tank Helped Craft DOJ Antifa Terror Indictment Against ICE Protesters

**TL;DR:** Researcher from Islamophobic think tank provided language for first-ever domestic terrorism case against alleged antifa cell, citing Signal use and security practices as evidence.

## Key Points

- Kyle Shideler from Center for Security Policy helped DOJ craft terror indictment against Texas ICE protesters
- First domestic terrorism case against purported antifa cell involves 9 defendants facing life sentences
- Charges stem from July 4 protest at Prairieland ICE detention center where officer was shot
- Shideler testified Signal messaging, black bloc clothing, and security culture are 'antifa hallmarks'
- Center for Security Policy founded by Frank Gaffney, labeled hate group by SPLC for Islamophobic views
- Defense highlighted Shideler lacks academic credentials, doesn't use peer review or social science methods
- Prosecutors using privacy tools and operational security as evidence of extremist activity
- Case demonstrates concerning cooperation between federal prosecutors and far-right advocacy groups

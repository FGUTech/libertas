# Mexico Partially Unblocks Tor Access After Years of Government Website Censorship

**TL;DR:** Mexico's new administration under Claudia Sheinbaum partially lifted a Tor network block affecting 20+ million users, allowing access to main government sites while maintaining restrictions elsewhere.

## Key Points

- 21 Mexican government agencies blocked Tor network access across multiple administrations from 2012-2025
- An estimated 20 million people use Tor in Mexico for secure internet access and evading surveillance
- Block was partially lifted in July 2025 under President Claudia Sheinbaum's administration
- Main government portal gob.mx now accessible via Tor, but datos.gob.mx remains blocked
- Previous administration acknowledged blocking Tor traffic as 'security measure' against malicious activity
- Government simultaneously used Tor for anti-corruption whistleblower systems while blocking citizen access
- Civil society, journalists, and human rights groups actively use Tor for anonymous communications
- Tor Project argues technical solutions exist to balance security without wholesale blocking

## Deep Dive

## Context and Background

Mexico's relationship with the Tor network represents a classic government double standard on privacy technology. For over a decade, spanning the Peña Nieto (2012-2018) and López Obrador (2018-2024) administrations, Mexican government websites systematically blocked access from Tor users. This affected an estimated 20 million Mexican Tor users who rely on the network for protection against surveillance and censorship.

## Technical and Policy Implications

The Mexican government's justification reveals a fundamental misunderstanding of Tor network traffic. Officials claimed they couldn't "distinguish between ordinary and automated users," treating all Tor traffic as potentially malicious. The Tor Project's Pavel Zoneff countered this reasoning, noting that existing technical solutions can mitigate DoS attacks and bot traffic without blanket blocking entire network segments. The partial reversal under Sheinbaum suggests a policy shift rather than a technical breakthrough.

## Threat Model Considerations

The selective unblocking exposes the political nature of these restrictions. While gob.mx (general government information) is now accessible, datos.gob.mx (government data portal) remains blocked, and the Tor-enabled whistleblower system was replaced with the non-Tor-compatible SIDEC platform. This suggests authorities want to control which government functions are accessible via privacy-preserving tools.

## What Builders Should Consider

This case demonstrates how governments can simultaneously embrace and restrict the same privacy technology based on their control preferences. The Mexican government's own use of Tor for whistleblowing while blocking citizen access highlights the need for decentralized, government-independent privacy infrastructure. Builders should anticipate selective technical restrictions and design systems that can route around partial blocking attempts.
